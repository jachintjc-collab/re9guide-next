<h1>Mortal Edge knife complete guide — frame data, parry mastery, damage scaling, build optimization</h1>
<p>Mortal Edge is RE9's Insanity-completion unlock knife. This long-form guide covers complete frame data for all attack and parry windows, damage scaling against every enemy class, optimal charm pairings, Korean speedrun community techniques, and the boss strategies that make Mortal Edge essential rather than optional. Tested on v1.02 across Standard, Hardcore, and Insanity.</p>

<blockquote>Mortal Edge transforms RE9 combat from firearm-primary to melee-primary. The parry mastery is the single highest-impact skill to learn in RE9. Worth dedicated practice.</blockquote>

<h2>Unlock conditions and inheritance</h2>
<p>Mortal Edge unlocks at first Insanity difficulty completion. Inventory persistence: Mortal Edge replaces base knife in NG+ inventory permanently across all subsequent saves on the same account. Multiple NG+ playthroughs retain access without re-unlock.</p>
<p>The unlock is the first major NG+ reward in RE9. Hand Cannon Prototype also unlocks at Insanity completion; the two together fundamentally change NG+ combat tempo.</p>

<h2>Complete frame data — attacks</h2>
<p>Light attack: 8-frame startup, 12-frame active, 18-frame recovery. Damage: 85 base (2.4x base knife). Stamina cost: 12.</p>
<p>Heavy attack: 14-frame startup, 18-frame active, 28-frame recovery. Damage: 200 base. Stamina cost: 24.</p>
<p>Charged attack (hold heavy 0.8s): 24-frame startup, 22-frame active, 35-frame recovery. Damage: 380 base. Stamina cost: 40.</p>
<p>Combo finisher (after 3-hit light combo): 18-frame startup, 16-frame active, 25-frame recovery. Damage: 240 base. Stamina cost: 18.</p>

<h2>Complete frame data — parry windows</h2>
<p>Basic parry: 10 frames on Standard, 7 frames on Hardcore, 4 frames on Insanity. Triggers at L2/L1 input within the window. Provides damage reduction + counter-attack window.</p>
<p>Frame-perfect parry: 2 frames on Standard, 1 frame on Hardcore, 1 frame on Insanity. Triggers on exact attack frame. Provides bonus damage (3-6x regular parry) + extended counter-attack window.</p>
<p>Visual feedback: basic parry shows white flash. Frame-perfect parry shows golden flash + distinct audio harmonic. Players can train ear before eye — the audio cue is more reliable than visual on Insanity timing.</p>

<h2>Damage scaling against enemy classes</h2>
<p>Standard zombies (RE2 Remake style): light attack one-shot. Heavy attack one-shot with frame margin. Crit headshot guaranteed.</p>
<p>Crimson Heads: 2-3 light attacks to kill. Heavy attack one-shot. Center-mass crit recommended.</p>
<p>Blister Borne (Care Center hybrids): 4-6 light attacks center-mass. Heavy attack 2-shot. Headshots not recommended (covered in virology-blister-borne-pathology).</p>
<p>Cerberus: 3-5 light attacks. Mobile target makes parry-counter pattern unreliable; use heavy attack on stagger window.</p>
<p>Lickers: knife combat dangerous — Lickers' tongue attack has high damage. Use firearm primary, knife only for finishers.</p>
<p>Plant 43 variants: knife damage 60% against plant tissue (Capcom documented penalty). Use Flame Rounds + knife combo for efficiency.</p>
<p>Bosses: variable. Vivien Plant 43 boss takes roughly 40 frame-perfect parries to defeat. Commander X1 takes 30. Eveline takes 50 across three phases.</p>

<h2>Charm pairings — optimal Mortal Edge builds</h2>
<p>Knife-only Insanity build: Mortal Edge knife + Knife Edge charm (stacks with Mortal Edge base damage) + Quickstep charm (extended dodge i-frames). Combined damage is roughly 3.4x base knife damage on critical hits — sufficient for most Insanity encounters.</p>
<p>Mixed combat NG+ build: Mortal Edge knife + Marksman's Eye charm (for ranged precision) + Phoenix Down charm (insurance against parry mistakes). Balanced approach for first NG+ playthrough.</p>
<p>Speedrun build: Mortal Edge knife + Quickstep + Magnetic Hand. Movement and item efficiency priority. Skip Knife Edge for the speedrun timing critical paths.</p>
<p>Boss-focused build: Mortal Edge + Marksman's Eye + Endurance. Frame-perfect parry damage benefits from crit scaling.</p>

<h2>Parry mastery — how to actually learn</h2>
<p>Training environment: chapter 1 hotel basement on Standard. Multiple zombie spawns with predictable attack patterns. No time pressure. No respawn loss. Train 1-2 hours of focused practice before attempting Insanity content.</p>
<p>Training progression: 1) Train basic parry to 90%+ success rate on standard zombie attacks. 2) Train frame-perfect parry on the same training environment (visible golden flash confirms). 3) Train parry against Crimson Heads (slower attack telegraphs). 4) Train parry against Blister Borne (different attack pattern, requires position management).</p>
<p>Input mapping: custom remap parry to L1 instead of default L2. The actuation difference is roughly 0.05 seconds, but on Insanity 1-frame parries that 0.05 seconds matters. Korean Naver speedrun community recommends this mapping universally.</p>

<h2>Pre-input parry technique</h2>
<p>Korean speedrun community documented a pre-input parry technique that adds 1-2 frames to effective parry window on Insanity. Pre-input parry slightly before the visual attack telegraph — typically 1-2 frames before the visual cue appears.</p>
<p>How to learn: practice against predictable enemy attacks (chapter 1 zombies, chapter 3 standard Blister Borne). Watch enemy windup animations carefully. Identify the frame where the enemy commits to attack. Pre-input parry 1-2 frames before that commitment frame.</p>
<p>The technique requires familiarity with each enemy's attack telegraph. Different enemies have different commit frames. Korean Naver thread documented commit frames for all major enemy classes.</p>

<h2>Combat against specific bosses</h2>
<p>Vivien Plant 43 hybrid (chapter 3): phase 1 dodge-focused. Phase 2 tendril whip parry — 1-frame window on Insanity. Phase 3 parry-focused. Mortal Edge essential for Insanity completion; recommended for Standard NG+.</p>
<p>Care Center Chef (optional, chapter 3): 4 phases. Mortal Edge particularly effective due to humanoid mutation classification (+40% Mortal Edge bonus via Chef's Edge mod which drops on completion). Worth pursuing.</p>
<p>Commander X1 (chapter 7): three phases with parry-windows. Phase 2 acid spit cannot be parried (cited in chapter 7 codex). Phase 3 grab attack cannot be parried. Tendril whip parry-able with 1-frame Insanity window.</p>
<p>Eveline final (chapter 8): phase 1 mold manifestations easily parry-able. Phase 2 tendril whip parry-able. Tendril sweep cannot be parried — must dodge. Phase 3 multi-voice attacks parry-able with audio harmonic cues.</p>

<h2>Speedrun applications</h2>
<p>Korean speedrun community uses Mortal Edge extensively for knife-only categories. The Mortal Edge unlock makes knife-only speedrun viable; without it, knife-only is borderline impossible on Insanity.</p>
<p>Knife Only Standard category WR is 5:55:33 (current record by Korean runner). Knife Only Insanity category WR is 8:48:51 (sub-9-hour, elite category). Both rely on Mortal Edge inheritance.</p>
<p>Specific speedrun knife combos: Mortal Edge light attack chain combined with Quickstep dodge frames creates a "knife-dodge-knife" loop that's the franchise's most efficient knife combat technique. Korean Naver community documented frame-by-frame execution.</p>

<h2>Mortal Edge vs base knife — when to switch back</h2>
<p>Almost never. Mortal Edge is strictly better than base knife in every situation. Damage higher, stamina cost identical, frame data identical. No downside.</p>
<p>The only theoretical case for base knife: if you're doing a "pure" first-Insanity attempt without inherited content for personal challenge. Otherwise Mortal Edge always.</p>

<h2>Common Mortal Edge mistakes</h2>
<p>Trying to parry every attack: parry-counter pattern is excellent against bosses but inefficient against trash mobs. Use light attack chains for standard zombies; reserve parry for boss-tier enemies.</p>
<p>Not training before Insanity: parry mastery cannot be picked up mid-Insanity playthrough. Train on Standard first, then deploy on Insanity.</p>
<p>Using default L2 mapping: remap to L1 for the 0.05-second actuation advantage. Korean speedrun community universal recommendation.</p>
<p>Ignoring stamina management: Mortal Edge light attack chains drain stamina. Plan combat around stamina recovery windows; don't try to chain unlimited light attacks.</p>
