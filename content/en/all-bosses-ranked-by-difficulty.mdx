<h2>How this ranking works</h2>

<p>Each boss is scored on five factors:</p>

<ul>
 <li><strong>Damage output</strong> — how hard their hits land</li>
 <li><strong>Attack pattern complexity</strong>. how many moves they have, how readable</li>
 <li><strong>Parry difficulty</strong>. how tight the parry windows are</li>
 <li><strong>Resource demand</strong> — how much ammo/healing the fight consumes</li>
 <li><strong>Insanity scaling</strong>, how punishing they become on the hardest difficulty</li>
</ul>

<p>The list goes from hardest (#1) to easiest (#8). Order matters: knowing what's coming lets you save resources for the right fights.</p>

<h2>Boss order in the campaign</h2>

<p>For story planning, here's the order you'll fight bosses:</p>

<ol>
 <li><strong>The Chef</strong> (Care Center Kitchen), optional, very early</li>
 <li><strong>Chunk #1</strong> (East Wing) — optional, recommended for Eye Spy Charm</li>
 <li><strong>Chunk #2</strong> (Care Center Attic); mandatory mid-game</li>
 <li><strong>Mr. X / Super Tyrant</strong> (Outside RPD), mandatory, post-Care Center</li>
 <li><strong>Titan Spinner</strong> (Raccoon City Plaza) — mandatory mid-Raccoon City</li>
 <li><strong>Plant 43</strong> (ARK Entrance). mandatory, early ARK</li>
 <li><strong>The Commander</strong> (ARK Corridors); mandatory, mid ARK</li>
 <li><strong>Victor Gideon</strong> (ARK Final Chamber) — final boss</li>
</ol>

<h2>Pre-fight prep checklist</h2>

<p>Universal pre-boss preparation:</p>

<ul>
 <li>Save manually before the fight (use a fresh slot)</li>
 <li>Repair body armor at the nearest Supply Box</li>
 <li>Stock 2+ First Aid Sprays</li>
 <li>Reload all weapons before triggering the cutscene</li>
 <li>Equip the strongest melee weapon (Mortal Edge Hatchet ideally)</li>
 <li>Consider equipping Eye Spy Charm (random fatal damage save) for Insanity</li>
</ul>
