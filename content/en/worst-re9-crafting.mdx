<h1>Worst RE9 crafting — the system that didn't need to be there</h1>
<p>RE9 includes a crafting system inherited from RE4 Remake. The system adds complexity without meaningful gameplay benefit.</p>

<blockquote>RE9 crafting is the worst-implemented system in the game. It adds inventory management without rewarding mastery. Capcom should remove it from RE10.</blockquote>

<h2>What crafting does wrong</h2><p>Materials drop too commonly to feel meaningful. Recipes too simple to feel skill-based. Inventory management cost too high for reward.</p><h2>Why it's there</h2><p>RE4 Remake's crafting was well-received. Capcom replicated without adapting to RE9's tactical-horror tone (vs RE4 Remake's action-survival).</p><h2>What works</h2><p>Healing-item crafting is acceptable. Ammo crafting is acceptable.</p><h2>What doesn't</h2><p>Charm-adjacent crafting components add nothing. Weapon attachment crafting feels arbitrary.</p><h2>My take</h2><p>RE9 would be better without crafting. The system feels bolted on. RE10 should commit either to action-survival (full crafting) or tactical-horror (no crafting).</p>
