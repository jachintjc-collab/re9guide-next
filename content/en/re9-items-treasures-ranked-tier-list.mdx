<h2 id="tldr">TL;DR. what to collect first</h2>
<ul>
 <li><strong>Top priority (don't miss):</strong> Eveline's Locket + Master Key + Antique Coin. These three define your endgame.</li>
 <li><strong>Trophy-required (Platinum hunt):</strong> Family Photograph + Quartz Fragment Set + Silver Pendant. Missing any = no Platinum.</li>
 <li><strong>NG+ unlocks:</strong> Master Key + Silver Pendant + ARK Security Badge — only available in NG+ runs.</li>
 <li><strong>Skip on first playthrough:</strong> Tarnished Necklace, Rusty Spoon, Worn Notebook. Just sell them.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s-tier">S-tier, must-collect items</h2>

<h3>#1: Eveline's Locket (Lore + Insanity unlock)</h3>
<p><strong>Why #1:</strong> The most lore-significant item in RE9. Hidden in ARK Facility Vivien lab. Opening it unlocks the Eveline-Vivien connection codex (the franchise's most important lore reveal in years). On Insanity, possessing this locket triggers Eveline's whisper audio during boss fights. See our <a href="eveline-character-profile-complete.html">Eveline complete profile</a>.</p>

<h3>#2: Master Key (Universal door access. NG+ only)</h3>
<p><strong>Why #2:</strong> NG+ exclusive. Opens every locked door in the game. Unlocks 7 previously-inaccessible rooms across all chapters. Essential for the "Completionist" trophy and exposes the 3 hidden lore documents not findable on first playthrough.</p>

<h2 id="a-tier">A-tier — high-value items</h2>

<h3>#3: Antique Coin (Currency × 10 rare)</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> The single most valuable currency item. 6 total Antique Coins exist in the game (3 in Chapter 5, 3 in Chapter 8). Collecting all 6 = 60,000 in-game currency, enough to buy 3 tier-3 weapon upgrades or 4 high-tier charms.</p>

<h3>#4: Family Photograph (Vivien lore reveal)</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> Found in Care Center records office. Shows Vivien Ashcroft holding a child labeled "E, " (Eveline). This is the smoking gun connecting <a href="grace-ashcroft-character-profile-complete.html">Grace</a> to <a href="eveline-character-profile-complete.html">Eveline</a>. Trophy-required (Platinum).</p>

<h3>#5: Quartz Fragment Set (Puzzle progression)</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> Required for the <a href="all-quartz-puzzle-solutions.html">Quartz puzzle</a>. 5 fragments scattered across chapters. Completing the puzzle unlocks the Insanity-only Eveline reference photo and the Chunk family audio.</p>

<h3>#6: Silver Pendant (NG+ charm slot unlock)</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> Hidden in Chapter 6 orphanage attic. Equipping this unlocks the 4th charm slot in NG+. Massive build flexibility upgrade, see our <a href="re9-all-charms-ranked-tier-list.html">charms tier list</a> for optimal 4-charm loadouts.</p>

<h2 id="b-tier">B-tier — solid items</h2>

<h3>#7-11: Keys and badges</h3>
<p><strong>Bronze Key, RPD Master Key, Care Center Pass, ARK Security Badge</strong>, all gateway items that unlock chapter-specific rooms. The <strong>Brass Whistle</strong> is unique: throwing it generates noise that distracts enemies. Useful on Insanity stealth segments.</p>

<h2 id="c-tier">C-tier, lore-only items</h2>

<h3>#12-15: Lore tokens</h3>
<p><strong>Vintage Pen, Old Diary, Stained Photo, Ceramic Doll</strong> — these unlock codex entries but provide minimal gameplay value. Worth collecting for lore completionists but skippable on speedruns.</p>

<h2 id="d-tier">D-tier. skippable items</h2>

<h3>#16-18: Junk items</h3>
<p><strong>Tarnished Necklace, Rusty Spoon, Worn Notebook</strong>; sell to vendor for 100-500 currency each. No lore value, no gameplay impact. Rusty Spoon is a reference to RE7's starting inventory (easter egg only).</p>

<h2 id="trophy-required">Items required for Platinum trophy</h2>
<p>To achieve Platinum, the following items must be collected:</p>
<ul>
 <li>Family Photograph (Chapter 2), "Vivien's Truth" trophy</li>
 <li>All 5 Quartz Fragments, "Mosaic" trophy</li>
 <li>Eveline's Locket (Chapter 8) — "The Catalyst" trophy</li>
 <li>All 6 Antique Coins; "Treasure Hunter" trophy</li>
 <li>Silver Pendant + Master Key (NG+ only); "Completionist" trophy</li>
</ul>
<p>See our <a href="re9-new-game-plus-complete-guide.html">NG+ complete guide</a> for the optimal collection order across 3 playthroughs.</p>

<h2 id="ngplus">NG+ exclusive item drops</h2>
<p>Three items only appear in NG+ runs:</p>
<ol>
 <li><strong>Master Key</strong> — automatically given at NG+ start</li>
 <li><strong>Silver Pendant 2nd location</strong>, appears in Chapter 8 (different from first-run location)</li>
 <li><strong>Eveline's Reference Photo</strong>. Insanity NG+ only, in Vivien lab</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ — item collection</h2>

<p><strong>Q: Can I collect all 18 items in one playthrough?</strong><br>
A: No. Master Key, Silver Pendant 2nd location, and Eveline's Reference Photo are NG+ exclusive. Minimum 2 playthroughs (1 standard + 1 NG+) required for 100% collection.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Are items missable?</strong><br>
A: Yes. most are missable if you skip optional rooms. The exceptions are Eveline's Locket (mandatory pickup in Chapter 8) and Quartz Fragments (required for puzzle progression).</p>

<p><strong>Q: Which item is hardest to find?</strong><br>
A: Antique Coin #6 in Chapter 8. Hidden behind a secret panel in the ARK Facility executive office. Requires the ARK Security Badge to access the office first.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Do items respawn in NG+?</strong><br>
A: No. Items collected in NG don't respawn, but NG+ adds 3 new items not present in the first run.</p>

<p><strong>Q: What's the best item to sell?</strong><br>
A: Vintage Pen (1,500 currency) and Old Diary (1,200). Both have low lore weight and good resale. Skip selling Antique Coins — keep them as currency multipliers.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Are there hidden items not on this list?</strong><br>
A: The Insanity-only Eveline Reference Photo isn't a standard item, it's a special unlock. Beyond that, no hidden items exist.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Can items break or be lost?</strong><br>
A: No. Items persist in inventory across all playthroughs. Collecting one in NG carries to NG+ as "owned."</p>

<p><strong>Q: Will RE10 keep this collection system?</strong><br>
A: Director Nakanishi confirmed collection systems will return. Form unconfirmed.</p>
