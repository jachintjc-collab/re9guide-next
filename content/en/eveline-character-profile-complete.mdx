<h2 id="origin">Origin; The Connections lab creation</h2>
<p>Eveline (full designation E-001, "E-Series" project) was created in the early 2010s by The Connections — a private bioweapon organization rival to Umbrella Corporation. Her base material is a custom-engineered fungal strain (called "the Mold" in-universe) capable of forming psychic links between infected hosts.</p>

<p><strong>Design intent:</strong> The Connections wanted a controllable bioweapon that could autonomously infect populations through emotional manipulation rather than direct combat. Eveline's "child" form was specifically designed to lower victims' guard. she could ask for help and be received as harmless.</p>

<p><strong>Lead scientist (per Connections files):</strong> A team led by Dr. Lucas Bertolucci (deceased 2015, killed by Eveline). Several junior scientists are mentioned in datamined RE9 files including a "Dr. V. Ashcroft"; see the <a href="#vivien">Vivien theory section</a> below.</p>

<p><strong>Activation timeline:</strong></p>
<ul>
 <li><strong>2009:</strong> Eveline's consciousness imprinted onto initial mold substrate</li>
 <li><strong>2013:</strong> Eveline begins exhibiting independent decision-making</li>
 <li><strong>2014:</strong> First successful infection-controlled victim (test subject "Alpha")</li>
 <li><strong>2014-2017:</strong> Field deployment via tanker E-001 (the wrecked ship found in RE7)</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="forms">Physical forms (child, old woman, mold)</h2>
<p>Eveline has three primary physical manifestations:</p>

<div>
 <table>
 <tr><th>Form</th><th>Appearance</th><th>Purpose</th></tr>
 <tr><td><strong>Child Eveline</strong></td><td>10-year-old girl, white hair, period dress</td><td>Default emotional-manipulation form. Used to lower victim defenses.</td></tr>
 <tr><td><strong>Old woman (true body)</strong></td><td>~70-year-old, severely aged, mold lesions visible</td><td>Her actual biological host. Aged prematurely due to mold burnout. Only seen in RE7's final chapter.</td></tr>
 <tr><td><strong>Mold mass (boss form)</strong></td><td>Hulking organic mass with multiple writhing limbs</td><td>Combat form when threatened. RE7 final boss state.</td></tr>
 </table>
</div>

<p><strong>Form-switching:</strong> Eveline can present any of these to different witnesses simultaneously. Mia perceived her as a child throughout RE7; Ethan saw both the child and the aged form. This is psychic projection — her actual body remains the aged form.</p>

<h2 id="re7">RE7 events, full timeline</h2>
<details>
 <summary>▶ REVEAL: Eveline's RE7 ending fate</summary>
 <div><p><strong>The setup:</strong> After tracking Mia through the Baker estate, Ethan reaches the wrecked tanker where Eveline's "true" body, that of a 70-year-old woman — is hidden. The "child Eveline" persona is a psychic projection from this aging body.</p><p><strong>The kill scene:</strong> Ethan injects E-necrotoxin (custom anti-mold compound) into Eveline's arm. She visibly ages 50 years in 8 seconds, mummifying into a husk. Chris Redfield (in Umbrella tactical gear) extracts Ethan and Mia via helicopter.</p><p><strong>Why "fate" matters:</strong> RE7's ending implied Eveline was definitively destroyed. The mummified body was shown on-screen. Capcom maintained this for 9 years. until RE9's Grace Secret Ending reveals her alive in Bulgaria.</p><p><strong>What we now know:</strong> The body Ethan killed was Eveline's biological host. Her consciousness. tied to the mold strain itself — survived in scattered samples. The "Eveline" walking in Varna 2026 is a regenerated body grown from preserved mold.</p></div>
</details>

<p><strong>Pre-RE7 timeline (Baker family infection):</strong></p>
<ol>
 <li><strong>2014:</strong> Tanker E-001 wrecks off Louisiana coast. Eveline survives in mold form, washes ashore.</li>
 <li><strong>Late 2014:</strong> Baker family rescues a "lost child". Eveline begins infecting them via mold ingestion.</li>
 <li><strong>2014-2017:</strong> 3 years of slow Baker family infection. They believe Eveline is their daughter. Locals who visit the estate are killed or absorbed.</li>
 <li><strong>2017 (RE7 events):</strong> Mia is sent by The Connections to retrieve Eveline. Mission fails. Ethan arrives 3 years later seeking Mia.</li>
 <li><strong>RE7 ending:</strong> Ethan reaches Eveline's aged body, injects E-necrotoxin, "kills" her.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="powers">Powers &amp; abilities</h2>
<ul>
 <li><strong>Psychic dominance:</strong> Can control any infected host within ~100m radius. Hosts experience her commands as their own thoughts.</li>
 <li><strong>Form projection:</strong> Appears differently to different observers. Child form is default; she can also project as a fully grown adult woman or as the aged crone.</li>
 <li><strong>Mold instantiation:</strong> Can manifest new physical forms from raw mold substrate. Her death in RE7 destroyed the aged body, not her consciousness.</li>
 <li><strong>Emotion sensing:</strong> Knows when victims are afraid, deceptive, or about to attack. Combat-relevant; bosses she possesses become more reactive.</li>
 <li><strong>Memory absorption:</strong> When she fully absorbs a host (kills them via mold), she inherits their memories. Explains how she "knew" the Baker family's personal histories.</li>
 <li><strong>Communication via mold network:</strong> Distant mold colonies can transmit information to her. Why she's aware of <a href="/re9-eye-spy-charm-explained/">Plant 43 development</a> in RE9 despite being in Bulgaria.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>The one thing she CAN'T do:</strong> Affect non-infected humans without first introducing mold to their body. This is her primary weakness and why Ethan (initially uninfected) could defeat her in RE7.</p>

<h2 id="re8">RE8 Village connection (Mother Miranda's research)</h2>
<p>Mother Miranda (RE8's primary antagonist) had been studying Eveline's mold samples for decades — her own creation of Lord-class bioweapons (Lady Dimitrescu, Heisenberg, etc.) used insights derived from Eveline's mold strain.</p>

<p><strong>Key RE8 references to Eveline:</strong></p>
<ul>
 <li>Miranda's lab notes mention "E-Series substrate" as the foundation for her Cadou parasite</li>
 <li>The Megamycete (RE8's underground mold network) is genetically related to Eveline's mold</li>
 <li>An audio log in House Beneviento describes Miranda visiting a "Connections facility" in 2018. likely retrieving Eveline samples</li>
</ul>

<p>RE8 didn't show Eveline directly, but established that her mold strain survived in multiple locations after RE7. This was Capcom's narrative groundwork for the RE9 revelation.</p>

<h2 id="sor">Shadows of Rose DLC; does she persist?</h2>
<details>
 <summary>▶ REVEAL: Shadows of Rose DLC (2022) connection</summary>
 <div><p><strong>The DLC:</strong> Released as RE8's post-launch DLC, Shadows of Rose features Rose Winters (Ethan + Mia's daughter, now teenage) entering a mental space tied to mold consciousness.</p><p><strong>Eveline's appearance:</strong> Rose encounters echoes of Eveline's child form throughout the mental space. They're described as "residual psychic imprints" — implying Eveline's consciousness persisted in the mold even after RE7.</p><p><strong>The ending implication:</strong> Rose successfully purges these echoes from her own body. But the mold network itself, and any other host Eveline can grow into, remains intact. This is the bridge between RE7's "she's dead" and RE9's "she's in Bulgaria."</p><p><strong>Significance:</strong> Shadows of Rose retroactively turned Eveline from "RE7 antagonist" into "ongoing franchise threat" — setting up exactly what RE9 then delivered.</p></div>
</details>

<h2 id="voice">Voice acting (Cissy Jones)</h2>
<p><strong>Cissy Jones</strong> voiced Eveline in RE7 (2017) and reprised the role for Shadows of Rose DLC (2022). She's known for emotionally complex female roles in horror games (also voices Katjaa in Telltale's The Walking Dead).</p>

<p><strong>The vocal performance technique:</strong> Jones recorded the child Eveline voice raw (no pitch-shifting). Her ability to convey both childlike innocence and predatory menace in the same line is widely credited as one of RE7's key strengths.</p>

<p><strong>RE9 voice, uncredited:</strong> Eveline doesn't speak on-screen in RE9. The Bulgaria photo is silent. However, datamined audio files include a faint laugh tagged "evel_test_004" — Capcom hasn't confirmed if Jones is returning for RE10.</p>

<p><strong>Fan campaign:</strong> The "#ReturnCissy" hashtag has 50K+ uses on X/Twitter petitioning Capcom to bring back Jones for RE10. As of May 2026, no official response.</p>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ. Eveline lore</h2>

<p><strong>Q: Is Eveline definitively alive in 2026?</strong><br>
A: Yes — confirmed by director Koshi Nakanishi in Famitsu: "The Bulgaria photograph is not metaphorical, Eveline survived in some form." Specific form/limitations are RE10 territory.</p>

<p><strong>Q: What's Eveline's real age?</strong><br>
A: Biologically, ~10 years old (rapid maturation from the 2009 mold imprinting). Her aged body in RE7 looked 70 due to mold burnout. Capcom's creative way of showing the bioweapon was "failing."</p>

<p><strong>Q: Is Eveline a person or just a bioweapon?</strong><br>
A: Both. She's a bioweapon with developing self-awareness. RE7 portrayed her as childlike but predatory. RE9's Bulgaria photo suggests she's now navigating the world autonomously — full sentience.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Why didn't she appear in RE8 Village directly?</strong><br>
A: Capcom's strategic choice. RE8 belonged to Mother Miranda; introducing Eveline alongside would dilute both villains. Eveline was kept off-screen to preserve impact for RE9 (and presumably RE10).</p>

<p><strong>Q: Is Cissy Jones confirmed for RE10?</strong><br>
A: Not officially. Datamined audio suggests Capcom has at least placeholder recordings; whether Jones is returning is unconfirmed as of May 2026.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Does the "Eveline is Vivien Ashcroft's creation" theory have weight?</strong><br>
A: Strong circumstantial evidence: dated 2009 research papers, "Subject E" naming, Vivien's disappearance timeline. Capcom hasn't confirmed but the in-game Easter eggs (<a href="/re9-hidden-easter-eggs-list/">see #7 Vivien notes</a>) make the connection deliberate, not accidental.</p>

<p><strong>Q: What does "E-Series" or "E-001" actually mean?</strong><br>
A: Per The Connections internal naming: E = "Emergence" (the project codename). 001 = first successful prototype. Other E-Series projects existed (E-002 through E-007) but all were destroyed in 2015 lab purge. Eveline is the sole survivor.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Can Eveline be killed permanently?</strong><br>
A: Per RE9 codex: "The Mold persists wherever its substrate exists." As long as mold colonies survive globally, Eveline can theoretically regenerate. True permanent death may require destroying every mold colony on Earth, possibly the RE10 endgame goal.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Is Eveline related to Mia Winters?</strong><br>
A: Antagonistic. Mia was assigned by The Connections to retrieve/control Eveline pre-RE7. Eveline psychologically traumatized Mia during the tanker mission. Their RE10 reunion would be emotionally loaded.</p>
