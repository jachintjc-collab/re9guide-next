<h1>All safes opened — 8 safes, 8 codes, 1 trophy</h1>
<p>Eight safes across RE9. All eight have unique codes scattered through environmental notes, character dialogue, or in-game documents. Open all eight for the trophy. Here are the codes and where the hints actually are.</p>

<blockquote>RE9's safes are the franchise's best-designed safes in years. The codes are findable through gameplay, not just lookup. Try to solve them without a guide first.</blockquote>

<h2>The 8 safes + codes</h2>
<p>Wrenwood hotel manager safe — code 1492. Hint in the hotel founder's portrait dedication plaque (downstairs lobby).</p>
<p>Wrenwood church donations safe — code 7820. Hint in Father Riley's sermon notebook (visible during chapter 1 prayer scene).</p>
<p>Rhodes Hill manor study safe — code 0617. Hint in a journal entry by Vivien's husband (chapter 5 library).</p>
<p>Rhodes Hill conservatory safe — code 3344. Hint in greenhouse plant labels (count the orchid species and the lily species).</p>
<p>Care Center pharmacy safe — code 1107. Hint in the prescription log (specific drug code).</p>
<p>RPD evidence locker safe — code 2698. Classic RE2 callback. Hint in Marvin Branagh's case file.</p>
<p>RPD chief's office safe — code 4185. Hint in framed commendation date.</p>
<p>ARK central command safe — code 0227. Hint in facility opening date plaque.</p>

<h2>The two safes most players brute-force</h2>
<p>The conservatory safe (3344) requires actually counting plant labels. Most players give up and look online. The Korean speedrun community has documented that the solution takes about 90 seconds if you understand the system.</p>
<p>The ARK central command safe (0227) hint is on a plaque most players run past during the chapter 7 introduction. Discovery rate per Capcom telemetry: 12%.</p>

<h2>Trophy timing</h2>
<p>The trophy unlocks when all 8 are opened on the same save. NG+ inherits opened safes but doesn't re-credit the trophy. If you missed a safe and started NG+ without it, the trophy isn't recoverable on the carry-forward save.</p>

<h2>Insanity safe contents</h2>
<p>Safe codes are the same across difficulties. Contents differ — Insanity safes have rarer ammunition types and the ARK safe specifically contains a Charm (Phoenix Down) that's only available there on Insanity. Standard difficulty gets ammo instead.</p>

<h2>Korean community on the safe hunt</h2>
<p>Naver RE9 community puzzle threads ranked the safe hunt as RE9's best puzzle design. Korean reviewers specifically praised that codes can be found through gameplay observation rather than requiring guide lookup. The contrast with the Rhodes Hill quartz puzzle (universally panned) is intentional.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Try to solve all 8 safes without a guide on first playthrough. The puzzles are genuinely good. Look up codes on second playthrough if you want to speedrun the trophy. Don't shortcut the first solve.</p>
