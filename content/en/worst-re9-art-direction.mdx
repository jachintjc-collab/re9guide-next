<h1>Worst RE9 art direction choices — where the visual design fails</h1>
<p>RE9's art direction is generally strong but has specific failure points. Chapter 3 Care Center lighting, Wrenwood NPC face animations, and several environment textures.</p>

<blockquote>Wrenwood townspeople NPC faces are RE9's worst visual element. Care Center interior lighting flattens visual storytelling. Capcom can't blame budget; these are choices.</blockquote>

<h2>Wrenwood NPC face animations</h2><p>Townspeople use a generic facial rig that's distinctly worse than main cast. Static expressions, limited emotional range. Player notice it.</p><h2>Care Center lighting</h2><p>Chapter 3 corridor lighting is uniformly fluorescent-flat. Removes visual storytelling opportunities. Compare to RE Village's varied lighting.</p><h2>Environmental texture repetition</h2><p>ARK sub-level 2 reuses the same wall texture across approximately 60% of the floor. Visual fatigue results.</p><h2>What works</h2><p>Chapter 5 manor is RE9's visual high. Chapter 7 ARK upper levels reward exploration. The failures are localized.</p><h2>My take</h2><p>Capcom should commission specific art-direction passes on weakest areas before RE9's DLC release. The fixes are achievable.</p>
