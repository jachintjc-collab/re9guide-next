<h1>RE9 launch trailer (February 2026) — full cut + final assessment</h1>
<p>The launch trailer dropped one week before release. Two and a half minutes, the most polished marketing piece in the campaign, also the one with the most revealed content. By this point most prospective buyers had already decided. The trailer's job was to seal the sale, not generate interest.</p>

<blockquote>The launch trailer is technically the most polished but narratively the least interesting of the four. It exists to close, not to open. Different job.</blockquote>

<h2>The full transcript</h2>
<div>
<p>[Open: Grace standing at the Care Center entrance. Score: full orchestral.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (voice-over):</strong> "Everyone thought it was over. The towers fell. The labs closed. The patients went home."</p>
<p>[Quick cuts: Wrenwood hotel exterior, Care Center long shot, RPD facade, ARK facility shadow.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (continued):</strong> "It was never over. It was just... quiet."</p>
<p>[Combat sequence: Mortal Edge parry chain. Boss tease: Eveline silhouette.]</p>
<p><strong>VIVIEN:</strong> "What I made wasn't meant to harm. It was meant to heal."</p>
<p><strong>FATHER RILEY:</strong> "Intent doesn't matter when the dead are walking."</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "Grace, I'm coming. Don't go in alone."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I have to."</p>
<p>[Final boss tease: Eveline emerges from mold. Cut to black.]</p>
<p>[Logo card. Release date: <strong>FEBRUARY 27, 2026</strong>]</p>
<p>[Post-card sting: Father Riley alone in confession booth: "Forgive me."]</p>

<h2>What this trailer revealed that earlier trailers didn't</h2>
<p>Vivien's face (first time in marketing). Eveline's return (the franchise's biggest reveal). Father Riley's confession booth final scene (this trailer's strongest moment). The Mortal Edge parry chain footage (combat technique tease).</p>
<p>Each reveal was significant. Together they consumed most of the game's remaining narrative mystery. By launch day, most prospective players knew the major beats.</p>

<h2>Why marketing chose to reveal so much</h2>
<p>The TGS combat trailer (#2) had damaged the reveal trailer's mystery framing. By the launch trailer, Capcom had to choose: protect remaining mystery (risk under-selling) or reveal narrative content (risk over-selling). They chose the latter.</p>
<p>This is the correct commercial decision but the wrong artistic one. Players who avoided trailers entirely got the best first-play experience. Players who watched all four trailers got the least mystery.</p>

<h2>Voice cast in the launch trailer</h2>
<p>Full main cast appearances. Cissy Jones (Grace) carries most of the voice-over. Laila Berzins (Vivien) gets her facial reveal scene with a single line. Lance Henriksen (Father Riley) gets the final confession sting that became the most-quoted line from the marketing campaign.</p>
<p>Eveline's voice (uncredited, possibly returning Erica Lindbeck from RE7) is heard briefly during the final boss tease. The vocal cameo was the first confirmation Eveline returned.</p>

<h2>Korean dub for launch</h2>
<p>The Korean launch trailer dropped simultaneously with English. Capcom Korea invested in the Korean dub of this trailer specifically — extended post-production, multiple takes per line, voice-director oversight. The result was Korean voice work on par with the original English cast.</p>
<p>Korean reception of the launch trailer was the strongest of any RE marketing in Korea, according to Naver discussion metrics. Father Riley's "용서해 주세요" closing line became a 화제 (meme) in Korean RE community.</p>

<h2>Production notes</h2>
<p>The launch trailer was directed by Yuya Nakanishi personally, contrary to the earlier trailers' marketing-team direction. The dev team got full control of the final marketing piece. This shows in the editing — slower pace, longer single takes, more breath room between music cues.</p>
<p>The orchestral score in the launch trailer is a custom arrangement of the in-game main theme. Mitsuhiko Takano scored it specifically; the in-game theme is a different arrangement.</p>

<h2>Reception data</h2>
<p>The launch trailer hit 41M YouTube views in the first 48 hours — the highest first-day for any RE trailer ever. Reception was strongly positive. The Father Riley closing line was widely meme'd across social platforms.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>The launch trailer is RE9's marketing campaign reaching peak professional polish. It's also where the franchise revealed too much of its hand for art's sake. Capcom traded mystery for sales. They got the sales. Whether that was the right trade depends on whether you bought RE9 because you'd seen the trailer or in spite of it.</p>
