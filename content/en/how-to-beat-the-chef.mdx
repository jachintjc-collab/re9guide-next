<h2 id="overview">Boss overview: Who is The Chef?</h2>
<p>The Chef is an optional mini-boss tucked away in the Care Center Kitchen, encountered early in Grace's section. He's described as a large, virus-mutated cook wielding a massive cleaver, and he guards a small cache of useful items including herbs and ammunition.</p>
<p>Mechanically, he's a melee-focused encounter, fast cleaver swings at close range, very limited movement options for Grace in a tight kitchen environment, and no ranged attacks. The risk-reward calculation is straightforward: spend ammo and possibly take some damage in exchange for crafting supplies and a side note that fills out the Care Center's narrative.</p>

<h2 id="access">How to access the encounter</h2>
<p>The Chef is sealed behind a locked door in the Care Center Kitchen. To trigger the encounter, you must first obtain the <strong>Pantry Door Key</strong>, which is hidden elsewhere in the Care Center.</p>
<p>If you're playing a completionist run, the Pantry Door Key is part of the Care Center 100% checklist. If you're speedrunning, this is one of the first items to skip — the time cost of finding the key and clearing the encounter outweighs the resource gain.</p>

<h2 id="strategy">Combat strategy</h2>
<p>The Chef is a straightforward melee fight in a confined space. The kitchen layout limits movement, but the enemy has no ranged options, so positioning is the entire fight.</p>

<h3>// STEP 1; STAY ON THE OPPOSITE SIDE OF THE PREP ISLAND</h3>
<p>The kitchen has a central prep island that acts as a natural barrier. Use it. Keep the island between you and The Chef, force him to circle, and fire shots when he commits to one direction.</p>

<h3>// STEP 2; TARGET THE HEAD</h3>
<p>Like most mutated enemies in <em>Resident Evil: Requiem</em>, headshots are the most efficient damage type. S&S M232 S&S M232 handgun headshots stagger him; W870 Police shotgun blasts at the head stagger and drop chunks of his health per shot.</p>

<h3>// STEP 3 — DODGE THE CLEAVER LUNGE</h3>
<p>The Chef's primary attack is a forward cleaver lunge; well-telegraphed but very fast. When you see the wind-up animation, dodge sideways, not backward. Backward dodges keep you in his hit cone.</p>

<h3>// AMMO RECOMMENDATIONS</h3>
<p>Treat this fight as practice for the Care Center's later encounters. Don't burn premium ammunition like Requiem bullets, S&S M232 handgun rounds and standard W870 Police shotgun shells are sufficient. Total ammo expenditure should be 8-12 rounds across the fight.</p>

<h2 id="worth">Is The Chef worth fighting?</h2>
<p>For a first-time playthrough, <strong>yes</strong> — the encounter is narratively complete (it adds context to the Care Center's collapse) and the herb cache inside the pantry is meaningful for the Plant 43 fight coming up later.</p>
<p>For a Platinum run, <strong>yes</strong>, completing all optional encounters contributes to the bestiary and supports the side-content trophies.</p>
<p>For a sub-4-hour speedrun, <strong>no</strong>. skip it. The 6-8 minutes you save are more valuable than the herbs you'd gain.</p>

<h2 id="after">Rewards and what comes next</h2>
<p>Defeating The Chef grants:</p>
<ul>
<li>Access to the pantry with herbs (typically 2-3 green herbs and 1 red herb)</li>
<li>Ammunition cache (S&S M232 handgun and W870 Police shotgun rounds)</li>
<li>Narrative document explaining the Care Center kitchen's role in the outbreak timeline</li>
<li>Contribution to the optional-encounter bestiary count</li>
</ul>
<p>The herbs are particularly useful — by stockpiling green and red herbs through the Care Center, you can craft enhanced healing items for the Water Treatment Facility encounter with The Girl, where damage is harder to avoid.</p>
