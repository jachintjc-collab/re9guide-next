<h1>Worst RE9 collectibles — Mr. Raccoon, again</h1>
<p>RE9 has multiple collectible categories. Mr. Raccoon plushies are the franchise's worst-aging tradition. RE9 includes them despite Capcom knowing players don't want them.</p>

<blockquote>Mr. Raccoon needs to die. RE9 is the eighth consecutive game with the same tedious collection. Capcom should retire the tradition.</blockquote>

<h2>Why Mr. Raccoon is bad design</h2><p>Hidden in obscure locations. No gameplay reward. Required for completion trophies. Repeated across 8+ games. The fatigue is real.</p><h2>What works in RE9 collectibles</h2><p>Audio logs are excellent. Documents are essential. Treasures contribute to lore. These three categories deserve their slots.</p><h2>What doesn't work</h2><p>Mr. Raccoon plushies (covered above). Weapon attachments scattered too sparsely. Charm-adjacent collectibles redundant with charms themselves.</p><h2>My take</h2><p>Retire Mr. Raccoon. Expand audio log and document content. Reduce attachment scatter density.</p>
