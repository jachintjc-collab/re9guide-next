<h2 id="tips">Tips, tricks, and trophy reward</h2>

<h3>// HOW TO INPUT SAFE CODES</h3>

<p>Approach the safe and interact with it. The dial puzzle screen appears. Use these inputs:</p>

<ul>
 <li><strong>L (Left)</strong> = rotate dial clockwise</li>
 <li><strong>R (Right)</strong> = rotate dial counterclockwise</li>
 <li>Stop precisely on the listed number, overshooting does not work, you must exit and retry</li>
 <li>Press the <strong>Check / Confirm</strong> button after entering all three numbers</li>
</ul>

<h3>// WHY ONLY GRACE CAN OPEN SAFES</h3>

<p>Safe-cracking is one of Grace Ashcroft's character-specific abilities, like Blood Crafting. Leon cannot interact with safes at all — he won't even see the prompt. If you're playing as Leon and trying to open a safe, switch back to Grace's section in the story or use a Grace-only save.</p>

<h3>// "NO SAFE IS SAFE" TROPHY</h3>

<p>Opening all 5 safes (regardless of difficulty) unlocks the <strong>"No Safe Is Safe"</strong> Silver trophy. This is one of the easier collectible-based trophies in the game if you have this guide handy. Combine it with the Mr. Raccoon collectible run for an efficient cleanup pass.</p>

<h3>// WHAT TO DO WITH ANTIQUE COINS</h3>

<p>The 12+ Antique Coins from safes should be spent at <strong>The Parlor</strong> in the West Wing. The Parlor merchant offers:</p>

<ul>
 <li>Weapon attachments (scopes, extended mags, suppressors)</li>
 <li>Healing items at discounted rates</li>
 <li>Crafting materials for Grace's Blood System</li>
 <li>Rare cosmetics for post-game</li>
</ul>

<p>Don't hoard coins. there are more than enough in the game to fully stock up at the Parlor.</p>

<h2 id="speedrun">Speedrun strategy: Memorize codes, skip clues</h2>

<p>For Insanity speedrun attempts, memorizing all 5 codes from this guide saves significant time. The code clues are scattered across optional rooms, skipping them and entering codes directly cuts roughly 8-10 minutes off a typical Care Center run.</p>

<p>Recommended speedrun order:</p>

<ol>
 <li>Safe #1 (Bar &amp; Lounge) — accessible in early Care Center exploration</li>
 <li>Safe #2 (Examination Room). same area, same trip</li>
 <li>Safe #3 (Furnace Room); after getting Level 1 Wristband</li>
 <li>Safe #4 (Sterilization Chamber) — same area as Safe #3</li>
 <li>Safe #5 (Monitor Control Room). Raccoon City, last opportunity</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="faq">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<div>

<details open>
<summary>Where is the Café safe code in RE9?<span>+</span></summary>
<p>The "Café safe" players reference is the <strong>Bar &amp; Lounge Safe (Safe #1)</strong> in the Care Center West Wing. The in-game room is officially named "Bar &amp; Lounge" but is also called Café by some communities because of its lounge layout. The code is <strong>L10 · R80 · L30</strong> on Normal and <strong>L40 · R70 · L20</strong> on Insanity. See <a href="#safe1">Safe #1 details above</a> for the full clue location and reward.</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the RE9 Lab safe code?<span>+</span></summary>
<p>The "Lab safe" is the <strong>Sterilization Chamber Safe (Safe #4)</strong> in the Care Center Basement lab. The chamber is the in-game name but the entire lab area is what players call "the lab." The code is in <a href="#safe4">Safe #4 details above</a>. Open this safe to claim a tactical knife and 3× gunpowder.</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Is there an Observation Room safe in RE9?<span>+</span></summary>
<p>Yes, players use "Observation Room safe" to refer to the <strong>Monitor Control Room Safe (Safe #5)</strong> in the Raccoon City area. The room is officially called Monitor Control Room because of the wall of CCTV monitors, but its observation-deck-style layout has earned the Observation Room nickname. Full details in <a href="#safe5">Safe #5 above</a>.</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>How many safes are there in Resident Evil Requiem?<span>+</span></summary>
<p>There are <strong>5 safes total</strong> in RE9: Bar &amp; Lounge (Café), Examination Room, Furnace Room, Sterilization Chamber (Lab), and Monitor Control Room (Observation Room). Opening all 5 unlocks the <strong>"Safe Master" trophy</strong>. All codes work on every difficulty (Normal codes for Casual/Standard, Insanity codes for Insanity).</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Do RE9 safe codes change between playthroughs?<span>+</span></summary>
<p>No — codes are <strong>fixed per difficulty</strong>. Normal-mode codes are the same every Normal playthrough, and Insanity codes are the same every Insanity playthrough. Casual and Standard share the Normal set. You can bookmark this page and use it on every run.</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I miss any safe code permanently?<span>+</span></summary>
<p>Yes, safes #1, #2, #3, and #4 are all in the Care Center, and once you leave the Care Center for the Courtyard/Water Treatment Plant transition, you <strong>cannot return</strong> on the same playthrough. Open them before triggering the basement sewer cutscene. Safe #5 in Raccoon City stays accessible until the ARK Facility cutscene. See our <a href="/re9-missables-complete-guide/">RE9 Missables Guide</a> for the full miss list.</p>
</details>
