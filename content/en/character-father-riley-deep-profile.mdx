<h1>Father Riley — Wrenwood priest + institutional ethics figure</h1>
<p>Father Riley is RE9's most ethically complex supporting character. Wrenwood's parish priest, Vivien Davies's confessor for 17 years, the man whose silence enabled the chapter 3 outbreak. Lance Henriksen's vocal performance defines the character.</p>

<blockquote>Father Riley is RE9's quietest masterpiece of character writing. Henriksen's performance is the franchise's best vocal work in 15 years.</blockquote>

<h2>Background</h2>
<p>Father Riley is approximately 60 years old. Wrenwood parish priest for 22 years. Confessor to Vivien Davies for 17 years. His role in the local community is significant — most Wrenwood residents attend his services.</p>

<h2>The confessional ethics</h2>
<p>Riley knew about Vivien's research safety violations through confession. He chose to honor the confessional seal despite knowing the catastrophic implications. His choice is theologically defensible and morally troubling.</p>

<h2>Voice work</h2>
<p>Lance Henriksen voices Riley. The "I have not slept properly in three years" monologue is his performance anchor. Henriksen brings Aliens, Millennium, and Pumpkinhead horror genre weight to the role.</p>

<h2>Korean dub</h2>
<p>Korean VA 김기현 handles Riley with specific emotional precision. Korean Naver community ranked the chapter 5 confessional scene as the best Korean dub moment in the game.</p>

<h2>The chapter 5 confrontation</h2>
<p>Riley breaks the confessional seal after Vivien's public confession (chapter 3 stage 2 defeat). He gives Grace the BSAA names. The scene is the franchise's most ethically mature character beat.</p>

<h2>Secret Ending revelation</h2>
<p>The Secret Ending (trophy-secret-ending) reveals Riley as Eveline-mold projection by chapter 5. The implications recontextualize his entire role. Whether canonical for RE10 is unclear.</p>
