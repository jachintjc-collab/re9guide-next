<p><strong>Short answer:</strong> For Insanity: <strong>Crafter's Touch + Iron Skin + Phoenix Down</strong>. For first playthrough: <strong>Crafter's Touch + Iron Skin + Marksman's Eye</strong>. For speedruns: <strong>Marksman's Eye + Quickstep + Magnetic Hand</strong>. Details below.</p>

<h2>Why Crafter's Touch is in 3 of 4 loadouts</h2>
<p><strong>+25% crafting yield</strong> means an extra 30+ items over a campaign. Single biggest economy boost in the game. Unlocked Chapter 2.</p>

<h2>4th charm slot (NG+ only)</h2>
<p>NG+ unlocks a 4th charm slot via Silver Pendant pickup. Recommended additions:</p>
<ul>
 <li><strong>For Insanity:</strong> Add <strong>Survivor's Will</strong> — herbs heal +50% HP</li>
 <li><strong>For speedrun:</strong> Add <strong>Magnetic Hand</strong>; auto-pickup items</li>
 <li><strong>For exploration:</strong> Add <strong>Bloodhound</strong>, enemies on map</li>
</ul>

<h2>What NOT to equip</h2>
<p>These charms are D-tier — skip them:</p>
<ul>
 <li><strong>Whisper</strong>. silent movement (RE9 stealth is mostly scripted)</li>
 <li><strong>Lockpick Mastery</strong>; saves ~1 minute total</li>
 <li><strong>Eagle Vision</strong> — Bloodhound does it better</li>
</ul>
