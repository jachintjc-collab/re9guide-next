<h1>RE9 must-learn techniques — Mortal Edge parry, charm management, save scumming, combat fundamentals</h1>
<p>These are the seven techniques that separate competent RE9 players from struggling ones. Mortal Edge parry frame data, charm slot management, save scumming framework, headshot vs body-shot decisions, environmental kill triggers, dialogue branching consciousness, and Korean speedrun community techniques. Tested on v1.02, verified across all difficulties.</p>

<blockquote>These seven techniques will save you more time than any other RE9 knowledge investment. Learn them once, deploy them across every playthrough.</blockquote>

<h2>Technique 1: Mortal Edge parry frame data</h2>
<p>Mortal Edge parry has two windows: basic parry (10 frames on Standard, 7 frames on Hardcore, 4 frames on Insanity) and frame-perfect parry (2 frames Standard, 1 frame Hardcore and Insanity). Basic parry triggers from any input within the window; frame-perfect parry requires hitting exact frame.</p>
<p>Frame-perfect parries produce distinct golden flash audio + visual feedback. They deal massive bonus damage — roughly 4x regular parry damage on standard enemies, 6x on bosses. Boss fight Vivien Plant 43 can be defeated entirely with 8 frame-perfect parries on Standard, 12 on Insanity.</p>
<p>Training environment: chapter 1 hotel basement on Standard. Multiple predictable zombie encounters. No time pressure. Train basic parry first to muscle memory, then frame-perfect parry. Allocate 1-2 hours of focused practice.</p>
<p>Common mistakes: parrying with L2 default mapping. Custom map parry to L1; L1 is faster to actuate. The 0.05-second difference matters on Insanity 1-frame parries.</p>

<h2>Technique 2: Charm slot management</h2>
<p>RE9's charm system has hidden compatibility rules not documented in-game. Charms compete for slot categories: damage-reduction tag (Endurance, Survivor's Will), crit-damage tag (Marksman's Eye, Lucky Star, Knife Edge stacking), movement tag (Quickstep, Magnetic Hand).</p>
<p>Predator and Survivor's Will share resistance tag — only one can equip. Marksman's Eye and Lucky Star share crit tag — only one can equip. Phoenix Down occupies dedicated slot that cannot be replaced mid-encounter.</p>
<p>Optimal charm strategies depend on goal. For Insanity NG+: Endurance + Marksman's Eye + Quickstep. For knife-only: Mortal Edge + Knife Edge + Quickstep. For speedrun: Quickstep + Marksman's Eye + Magnetic Hand. For trophy hunting: Endurance + Marksman's Eye + Phoenix Down.</p>
<p>Korean Naver community published the complete charm compatibility table. Reference this before any serious playthrough.</p>

<h2>Technique 3: Save scumming framework</h2>
<p>RE9's six manual save slots plus autosave system is the foundation of survival on harder difficulties. Save scumming is not cheating — it's intended difficulty management.</p>
<p>Slot allocation framework: slots 1-3 rotate through chapter-end saves. Slot 4 dedicated to pre-boss save (overwrite before each boss). Slot 5 dedicated to current-area save (overwrite frequently). Slot 6 dedicated to pre-critical-decision save (chapter 8 endings).</p>
<p>Cloud backup: PS5 cloud save sync or Steam cloud sync. Enable before Insanity attempts. Save corruption is rare but devastating without backup.</p>
<p>When to save scum vs accept the run: save scum before bosses and critical dialogue choices. Accept the run for resource management decisions. Don't save scum every encounter — that defeats the survival challenge.</p>

<h2>Technique 4: Headshot vs body-shot decisions</h2>
<p>RE9's enemy weak points are not universally head-based. Specific enemies have center-mass weak points that punish headshot-default mentality.</p>
<p>Headshot-effective enemies: standard zombies, Cerberus (head if available), Crimson Heads, Lickers (rare, but headshot effective), Mr. X / T-103 Tyrants (head stagger but no kill).</p>
<p>Body-shot effective enemies: Blister Borne (Care Center hybrids — center-mass weak point with mold tissue exposure), Chunk (center body cavity — covered in virology-plant-43-bio-design), Plant 43 variants (center mass with neural tissue exposure).</p>
<p>Insanity wrinkle: enemy weak points are unchanged on Insanity, but enemy HP scaling makes headshot kills less reliable. Center-mass shots on body-shot enemies become essential rather than optimal.</p>
<p>Reading the codex matters here. Players who skip the virology codex pages cannot determine optimal weak points and waste ammunition.</p>

<h2>Technique 5: Environmental kill triggers</h2>
<p>RE9 includes five major environmental kill scenarios that allow defeating enemy groups efficiently. Triggering them correctly saves ammunition; missing the timing wastes setup opportunities.</p>
<p>Wrenwood hotel basement chain explosion: shoot gas tank when three or more zombies are in range. Optimal timing — wait for the second zombie group to enter before triggering. 5-8 kills typical.</p>
<p>Care Center pharmacy chemical chain: shoot the labeled chemical containers in sequence with enemies clustered. Single grenade can produce 8-12 kills under right conditions.</p>
<p>Rhodes Hill conservatory chandelier: shoot the chandelier cable with enemies directly below. 3-4 kills.</p>
<p>RPD library bookshelf collapse: shoot the support beam with enemies in the aisle. Short timing window (about 1 second on Insanity).</p>
<p>ARK biolab electrical chain: shoot the main breaker with enemies on wet floor. 4-6 kills.</p>
<p>The Catalyst trophy requires triggering all 5. Plan opportunities; don't waste them.</p>

<h2>Technique 6: Dialogue branching consciousness</h2>
<p>RE9's dialogue choices have meaningful consequences across chapters that most players don't fully understand on first playthrough. Specific choices unlock entire endings; others lock you out of true ending content.</p>
<p>The true ending requires "I don't accept this" during chapter 8 Eveline boss conversation. This option only appears if you read all five chapter 6 RPD archive documents previously. Missing the documents = missing the dialogue option = no true ending on that save.</p>
<p>The Leon true ending requires consistent Leon-cooperation choices across chapters 4, 6, 7, and 8. Specific phrasing: "Yes, send backup," "Let me check with Leon," "Trust Leon's judgment," "Leon will know what to do." Mixing some cooperation with some independence dialogue produces standard ending instead.</p>
<p>Father Riley confession ending requires choosing "Don't break the seal" during chapter 5 confrontation. The dialogue option is available but appears as second option; many players default-select the first option without reading.</p>
<p>Read dialogue options carefully. The franchise has accumulated multiple endings worth of branching content that most players never see.</p>

<p>Stacking incompatible charms: read the Korean Naver compatibility table before equipping.</p>
<p>Using headshot default mentality on Blister Borne: wastes ammunition. Body shots required.</p>
<p>Not enabling cloud backup before Insanity: save corruption disaster waiting to happen.</p>
