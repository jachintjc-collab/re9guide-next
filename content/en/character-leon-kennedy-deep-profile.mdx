<h1>Leon S. Kennedy — RE9 appearance + franchise position update</h1>
<p>Leon S. Kennedy appears as supporting character in RE9, marking his transition from primary protagonist to franchise elder statesman. The chapter 7 farewell scene is the franchise's most explicit Leon handoff.</p>

<blockquote>Leon's RE9 appearance is the franchise saying goodbye to him as primary protagonist. Not as character — he'll return. But as the protagonist position. The handoff is earned.</blockquote>

<h2>Background</h2>
<p>Leon is 48 in RE9. RPD survivor, BSAA senior operative, longest-tenured RE protagonist. His RE9 role is mentor-collaborator to Grace rather than active operative.</p>

<h2>Voice work</h2>
<p>Nick Apostolides reprises Leon from RE2 Remake and RE4 Remake. Continuity matters for series fans. Apostolides has now voiced Leon across three generations of the character.</p>

<h2>Story role in RE9</h2>
<p>Active in chapters 1, 4, 6, and 7. Phone consultation throughout. Chapter 7 farewell scene is the handoff to Grace. Leon's choice to step back is character agency, not plot device.</p>

<h2>The accountability list framing</h2>
<p>Leon's request to be on Grace's public accountability list (chapter 7 farewell) is the franchise's most ethically mature Leon moment. He accepts institutional accountability for the BSAA system that allowed the Care Center research.</p>

<h2>Korean reception</h2>
<p>Korean RE community has 25+ years of Leon investment. RE9's Leon handoff was widely praised on Naver. Korean fans treated the chapter 7 farewell as emotional closure.</p>

<h2>RE10 trajectory</h2>
<p>Leon will appear in RE10 in supporting role. Not primary protagonist. Possibly mentor figure to new Korean BSAA commander.</p>
