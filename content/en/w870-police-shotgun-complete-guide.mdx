<h1>W870 Police shotgun — the workhorse, fully upgraded mandatory</h1>
<p>The W870 Police is RE9's primary shotgun, available from chapter 1. Fully upgraded, it's the franchise's most reliable close-range tool. Skipping upgrades is the most common newer-player mistake.</p>

<blockquote>Fully upgraded W870 Police is non-negotiable for any difficulty above Standard. If you're playing on Insanity without max W870, you're playing wrong.</blockquote>

<h2>Core stats fully upgraded (v1.02)</h2>
<p>Base damage: 95 per pellet × 9 pellets = 855 total point-blank. Magazine: 7 shells. Reload speed: 3.1s. Stagger: maximum at close range, moderate at medium range.</p>

<h2>Critical upgrades</h2>
<p>Magazine extension (chapter 2 merchant). Damage upgrade (chapter 4 merchant). Reload speed mod (chapter 6 workshop). All three are essential for Insanity viability.</p>

<h2>Range considerations</h2>
<p>Point-blank: maximum damage. Medium range (3-5m): 60% damage. Long range (5m+): scattered, unreliable. Use only at close-medium range.</p>

<h2>Best use cases</h2>
<p>Close-quarters zombie groups. Boss stagger at close range. Cerberus encounters. Plant 43 mini-boss damage windows.</p>

<h2>Ammo economy</h2>
<p>Standard shells: 12-pellet drops, common. Heavy shells (chapter 4+): 8-pellet drops, less common but high damage. Mix shell types based on encounter expectations.</p>

<h2>Insanity considerations</h2>
<p>Fully upgraded W870 Police is mandatory for Insanity survival past chapter 3. Standard difficulty allows partial upgrades; Insanity does not.</p>

<h2>Korean community tier</h2>
<p>Korean speedrun community lists W870 Police as A+ on Standard, S on Insanity. Universal consensus on its essential role.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Upgrade W870 Police completely on first playthrough. Don't skip merchant purchases. The shotgun pays for itself within one chapter of full upgrade.</p>
