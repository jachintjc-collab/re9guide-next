<h1>Mosaic — solve all environmental puzzles</h1>
<p>Solve every environmental puzzle in the game. The list includes safes, quartz puzzles, music puzzles, and various other room-specific puzzles. 17 puzzles total. Trophy ticks at the last puzzle solution.</p>

<blockquote>Mosaic rewards puzzle engagement. The quartz puzzle in Rhodes Hill is the franchise's worst puzzle and the only one most players quit on. Push through it for the trophy.</blockquote>

<h2>The 17 puzzles by category</h2>
<p>Safes (8): covered in trophy-all-safes-opened.</p>
<p>Quartz puzzles (6): Wrenwood hotel (1), Care Center (1), Rhodes Hill manor (1 — the bad one), ARK facility (3).</p>
<p>Music/audio puzzles (2): chapter 5 phonograph code, chapter 7 frequency match.</p>
<p>Spatial puzzles (1): the chapter 8 ARK core mosaic that gives the trophy its name.</p>

<h2>The Rhodes Hill quartz puzzle — RE9's design failure</h2>
<p>This puzzle requires clicking specific pixels on four paintings in low light. The click hitbox is small. The pixel target requires perfect mouse precision on PC, or specific stick angle on controller. Most players either give up or look up the solution.</p>
<p>Solution: code 560. But knowing the code doesn't help if you can't get the input registration to work. Adjust controller sensitivity in settings before attempting.</p>

<h2>The ARK core mosaic — the trophy namesake</h2>
<p>Final puzzle of the game. Requires arranging colored tiles in a specific sequence based on hints found throughout chapter 7. Solution involves 9 tiles. The puzzle is the franchise's best spatial puzzle since RE2 Remake's chess piece room.</p>

<h2>Time investment</h2>
<p>All 17 puzzles can be solved in roughly 90 minutes of focused play if you know the solutions. First-time blind solving adds 3-5 hours.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>

Skip the Rhodes Hill one for last — it's the worst experience and you don't want it ruining your puzzle motivation.</p>
