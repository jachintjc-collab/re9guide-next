<h1>Brass Whistle — the easily-missed audio lure tool</h1>
<p>Brass Whistle is a stealth-utility tool, found in chapter 2 Wrenwood. It generates an audio lure that pulls enemies toward a thrown direction. Specifically useful for Pacifist and Stealth Master pursuits.</p>

<blockquote>Brass Whistle is the most underrated stealth tool in RE9. Most players never pick it up. The Pacifist trophy is significantly easier with it.</blockquote>

<h2>How it works</h2>
<p>Throw the whistle. It clatters audibly at landing point. Nearby enemies investigate the sound, leaving their original patrol pattern. You move past while they're distracted.</p>

<h2>Best use cases</h2>
<p>Pacifist trophy (avoid combat). Stealth Master trophy (bypass guards). Chapter 6 Cerberus distraction (lure them away from your path).</p>

<h2>The chapter 2 unlock</h2>
<p>Hidden in the Wrenwood post office, behind the locked back door. Most players skip the post office entirely. Discovery rate per Capcom telemetry: 18%.</p>

<h2>Reusable</h2>
<p>Unlike one-time grenades, Brass Whistle can be retrieved after throwing. Pick it up after enemies leave the investigation area. Reusable indefinitely.</p>

<h2>Insanity considerations</h2>
<p>Insanity enemy attention spans are shorter. Brass Whistle distraction lasts about 4 seconds on Insanity (vs 7 seconds Standard). Plan tighter timing.</p>

<h2>Korean community usage</h2>
<p>Korean Pacifist trophy guides on Naver consistently feature Brass Whistle as essential tool. The Korean coverage is more thorough than English-language equivalents.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Pick up Brass Whistle in chapter 2 even if you don't think you'll need it. The Pacifist trophy attempt becomes 50% easier with it equipped.</p>
