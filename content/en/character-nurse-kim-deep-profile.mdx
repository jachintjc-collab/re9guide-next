<h1>Nurse Kim — Care Center staff member + chapter 3 mini-boss</h1>
<p>Nurse Kim is a Care Center staff member who became Blister Borne hybrid after E-pathogen exposure. She's a chapter 3 mini-boss with specific personal history. The encounter is one of RE9's most pathologically disturbing.</p>

<blockquote>Nurse Kim is RE9's most affecting tragic creature. She wasn't a willing subject. She was a Care Center nurse who got infected through institutional negligence. The fight has emotional weight most RE bosses don't earn.</blockquote>

<h2>Background</h2>
<p>Nurse Kim (Kim Eun-jin in Korean) was a Care Center registered nurse for 6 years pre-outbreak. Korean-American immigrant, late 30s. She was present at the early outbreak and was infected through E-pathogen aerosol exposure.</p>

<h2>The infection progression</h2>
<p>Kim's infection progressed through standard Blister Borne pathology (covered in virology-blister-borne). She remained partially conscious through transformation. Her chapter 3 personal documents reveal her awareness of what was happening to her.</p>

<h2>The mini-boss encounter</h2>
<p>Kim is the second mini-boss in chapter 3 (after the kitchen Chef). She's encountered in the Care Center patient ward. The fight uses her nurse uniform and recognizable staff badge to emphasize the tragedy.</p>

<h2>Voice/audio design</h2>
<p>Kim's vocalizations include partial human speech fragments. Korean phrases mixed with mutation sounds. The audio team specifically wanted players to recognize the human underneath.</p>

<h2>Korean reception</h2>

<p>RE9 includes a hidden interaction during the Kim fight — using a specific medical item allows Grace to attempt cure. The attempt fails (the outbreak has progressed too far) but the option exists. This is the franchise's quietest player choice acknowledgment.</p>
