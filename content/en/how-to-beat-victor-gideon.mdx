<h2 id="overview">Boss overview: Who is Victor Gideon?</h2>
<p>Victor Gideon is the director of the Rhodes Hill Chronic Care Center and the antagonist who has been pulling strings throughout the entire campaign. Once Leon releases Elpis at the ARK terminal. which doubles as a cure for the T-Virus. Victor abandons his humanity entirely. He replaces his left arm with a massive mutated tentacle and takes one last stand at the bottom of the ARK Facility.</p>
<p>This is a true two-phase final boss. Phase 1 is a controlled melee fight against a humanoid Victor with a tentacle arm. Phase 2 transforms him into a full Nemesis-clone with glowing red pustules across his body, sweeping arm attacks, and a tense QTE finisher.</p>

<h2 id="prep">Pre-fight preparation: Don't enter unprepared</h2>
<p>If you have been hoarding Requiem Revolver rounds, stacked grenades, and heavy medical injectors for the last ten hours, this is exactly where you burn them all. Victor Gideon is the last fight in the game, there is no reason to save resources past this point.</p>
<p>Before entering the boss arena, do the following:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Spend any remaining Antique Coins</strong> at the last Supply Box. Max out healing items, Requiem Bullets, and W870 Police shotgun shells.</li>
<li><strong>Equip the Mortal Edge Hatchet</strong>. You should have picked this up after defeating <a href="/how-to-beat-the-commander/">The Commander</a> earlier in the ARK. It is significantly better than Leon's default Hatchet and will be your primary parry-counter weapon. <em>If you skipped The Commander fight, go back and complete it before this point.</em></li>
<li><strong>Max out the Hatchet</strong> if you haven't already. The damage upgrade pays for itself within the first 30 seconds of this fight.</li>
<li><strong>Bring at least 2 Hand Grenades</strong>. Phase 2 has clustered weak points that grenades clear instantly.</li>
<li><strong>Stock up on First Aid Sprays</strong>. You will get hit. Carry at least 3.</li>
</ol>
<div>
⚡ <strong>Cheat tip:</strong> If you have unlocked Infinite Ammo or the Infinite RPG from the Special Content Store, this fight becomes trivial. The RPG eliminates most boss phases in one hit. Cheats do <strong>NOT</strong> disable trophies — safe to use even on Insanity.
</div>
<h2 id="phase1">Phase 1: Mutated human form</h2>
<p>After the platform collapses and Leon lands at the bottom of the ARK, Victor attacks in his humanoid form. He has a mutated tentacle arm, decent movement speed, and a tight arena filled with scrap metal piles that double as both cover and obstacles.</p>
<h3>// ATTACK PATTERN OVERVIEW</h3>
<p>Victor's tentacle arm gives him four primary melee attacks in this phase:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Single swipe</strong>, fast horizontal strike, parry window is generous.</li>
<li><strong>Double swipe</strong>. two consecutive swipes; parry both, or parry the first and dodge the second.</li>
<li><strong>Three-hit combo</strong> — most damaging. Parry all three or roll out of the entire combo.</li>
<li><strong>Thrust attack</strong>. covers a long distance. Parry or sidestep at the last moment.</li>
</ul>
<p>Every single one of these attacks is heavily telegraphed. You will see the wind-up frame clearly. Use the <strong>Mortal Edge Hatchet</strong> to parry, then immediately follow up with shots from the Requiem Revolver or W870 Police shotgun.</p>
<h3>// THE ELECTRIC ATTACK, DO NOT GET CAUGHT</h3>
<p>Periodically, Victor will leap onto one of the large scrap metal piles at the edge of the arena and begin channeling raw electricity. You will see a bright glowing line appear on the ground indicating the exact blast zone.</p>
<p>You have two choices when this happens:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Shoot him while he charges</strong> — this reduces the eventual blast power, but I find this to be a waste of good ammunition.</li>
<li><strong>Sprint out of the glowing zone</strong>, recommended. Watch the floor markers, get to a safe spot, and prepare for the follow-up jump-slam.</li>
</ol>
<p>After the electric blast, Victor lands in a "superhero pose" and stays motionless for about 2 full seconds. <strong>This is your biggest damage window of Phase 1.</strong> Empty Requiem rounds into his head while he is recovering.</p>
<h3>// PHASE 1 STRATEGY</h3>
<ol>
<li>Stay in the <strong>middle of the arena</strong> at all times. The center gives you 360° dodge space and lets you spot weak points faster.</li>
<li>Equip the <strong>W870 Police shotgun or Requiem Revolver</strong> for sustained damage. Avoid handguns in this fight.</li>
<li>Wait for Victor to finish a melee combo, dump 3-4 shots into his chest or head, then back off before his next attack.</li>
<li>When he <strong>drops to one knee</strong> (stagger animation), do not keep shooting him. Instead, run around the arena and smash every wooden crate. You need to pull every W870 Police shotgun shell, herb, and ammo pickup out of this arena before the environment gets destroyed in Phase 2.</li>
<li>Phase 1 ends when Victor takes enough damage and triggers the transformation cutscene.</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="phase2">Phase 2: Nemesis form</h2>
<p>After the cutscene, Victor transforms into a full Nemesis-clone; a massive, pulsating monstrosity with glowing red pustules across his head, arms, and body. This phase functions almost identically to the <a href="/how-to-beat-plant-43/">Plant 43 boss fight</a> earlier in the game, just with more weak points and harder-hitting attacks.</p>
<h3>// THE PUSTULE SYSTEM</h3>
<p>This phase is built around destroying glowing weak points in waves:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Wave 1:</strong> 3 pustules on his head and arms. Use a precision weapon (Requiem or sniper) to pop them.</li>
<li><strong>Wave 2:</strong> After the first 3 are destroyed, the protective layer over his core peels back. Dump your heaviest firepower (W870 Police shotgun, RPG if you have it, grenades) directly into the center.</li>
<li><strong>Wave 3:</strong> Dealing enough core damage causes Victor to collapse onto the platform. He spawns a cluster of new glowing orbs all over the floor around his base. <strong>You only have a few seconds.</strong> A grenade or two clears these instantly. Otherwise, machine gun fire works.</li>
<li><strong>Wave 4-5:</strong> Victor recovers, spawns a fresh wave of pustules (5 this time, then more). Repeat the process.</li>
</ol>
<h3>// PHASE 2 ATTACK PATTERNS</h3>
<p>Victor only has a few attacks in this phase, all of which can be parried with the Mortal Edge Hatchet:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Single arm smash</strong> — strafe left or right.</li>
<li><strong>Alternating double smash</strong>. keep moving, do not stop.</li>
<li><strong>Overhead two-arm smash</strong>; biggest damage, also biggest parry window.</li>
<li><strong>Rapid ground slam combo</strong> — happens after you destroy his lower-body pustules. Keep distance and wait it out.</li>
</ul>
<div>
<div><h4>// KEY TIP</h4><p>Strafe left and right between attack waves. Standing still gets you flattened.</p></div>
<div><h4>// AMMO PRIORITY</h4><p>Save Requiem Bullets for the head pustules. Grenades for clusters. W870 Police shotgun for the core.</p></div>
</div>
<h3>// THE GRAB AND QTE FINISHER</h3>
<p>After you destroy enough waves of pustules, Victor will <strong>grab Leon</strong> with one of his arms. Don't panic. this is meant to happen. A series of button prompts will appear on screen. Tap the indicated buttons (default: square/X repeatedly, then triangle/Y) to escape.</p>
<p>Successfully completing the QTE triggers the final cinematic kill sequence. Leon delivers a Hatchet finisher to Victor's exposed core, the screen cuts to black, the facility crumbles, and you have officially survived Resident Evil Requiem.</p>
<h2 id="insanity">Insanity difficulty: What changes</h2>
<div>
⚠ Insanity unlocks after completing the game once on any difficulty, even Casual. On Insanity, one mistimed dodge can be a one-shot kill.
</div>
<p>The Victor Gideon fight on Insanity is brutal but not unfair. The same mechanics apply, but with these key differences:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Damage multiplier ~3x</strong> — Phase 1 tentacle hits remove 70-80% of your health. Phase 2 ground slams can one-shot if you are below max HP.</li>
<li><strong>Reduced parry window</strong>; Mortal Edge is mandatory; the default Hatchet's parry window is too tight to react to consistently.</li>
<li><strong>Pustule HP increased</strong>; Phase 2 weak points take roughly double the damage to destroy. Bring more ammo.</li>
<li><strong>Electric attack tracking</strong> — the floor markers are slightly more aggressive. Sprint earlier, not later.</li>
</ul>
<p>For Insanity runs, the optimal strategy is to <strong>use Infinite Ammo on the Requiem Revolver</strong> (purchased from Special Content Store post-game). This trivializes the pustule destruction phase and lets you focus purely on dodging.</p>
<h2 id="nodmg">No-damage strategy (advanced)</h2>
<p>Pro players have completed this fight without taking a single hit on Standard difficulty. Here is the boiled-down approach:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Phase 1, never engage in melee.</strong> Only attack after a parry-counter or after the electric jump-slam recovery. Two safe attack windows per attack cycle.</li>
<li><strong>Stay at maximum range</strong> during pustule waves in Phase 2. Use the Marksman 1A or any sniper with a scope.</li>
<li><strong>Parry the overhead smash, dodge everything else.</strong> The overhead smash has the most generous parry window in the entire fight.</li>
<li><strong>For ground orb clusters</strong>, throw a grenade as soon as Victor collapses. This destroys all orbs in one shot and saves you the close-range exposure.</li>
<li><strong>Pre-position before each pustule wave.</strong> When Victor finishes recovering, you should already be at the edge of the arena with your scoped weapon equipped.</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="after">Rewards and what comes next</h2>
<p>Defeating Victor Gideon completes the main story and unlocks several things at once:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>"Requiem Complete"</strong>, Gold Trophy.</li>
<li><strong>Insanity difficulty</strong> — selectable from the New Game menu.</li>
<li><strong>Special Content Store</strong>, opens with new weapons (Matilda IMP, Clatter Carbine, Ghost Grudge, Redemption) and Infinite Ammo unlocks.</li>
<li><strong>Concept art and bonus cosmetics</strong>. viewable in the Special Content menu.</li>
</ul>
<p>If this was your first playthrough, your next move should be a New Game+ run on Insanity using carry-over weapons and Infinite Requiem. With Infinite Ammo equipped, the entire game can be cleared in under 4 hours, unlocking the <em>Speedrunner</em> and <em>Remarkable Agent</em> trophies in a single run.</p>

<h2>Frequently asked questions</h2>
<details>
<summary><strong>Can I beat Victor Gideon without the Mortal Edge Hatchet?</strong></summary>
<p>Yes, but the default Hatchet's parry window is very tight, and on Insanity difficulty it becomes unreliable. If you skipped The Commander fight (where Mortal Edge is rewarded), go back and complete it before attempting Victor Gideon — the upgrade pays for itself in the first 30 seconds.</p>
</details>
<details>
<summary><strong>Do I need Infinite Ammo to beat the fight on Insanity?</strong></summary>
<p>No, Infinite Ammo is not required. However, on Insanity the pustule HP is roughly doubled and one mistimed dodge can be a one-shot kill, so equipping Infinite Ammo on the Requiem Revolver (purchased from the Special Content Store after first clear) makes it significantly easier by letting you focus purely on dodging.</p>
</details>
<details>
<summary><strong>Will using cheats disable trophies on this boss?</strong></summary>
<p>No. Infinite Ammo, the Infinite RPG, and all other Special Content Store unlocks do not disable trophies in Resident Evil: Requiem. They are safe to use even on Insanity difficulty runs.</p>
</details>
<details>
<summary><strong>How long does the Victor Gideon fight take on average?</strong></summary>
<p>Around 12–18 minutes on Standard difficulty for first-time attempts, and 20–30 minutes on Insanity. Speedrunners using Infinite Requiem clear both phases in roughly 4–5 minutes.</p>
</details>
<details>
<summary><strong>What's the best weapon loadout for this fight?</strong></summary>
<p>Mortal Edge Hatchet for parry-counters, Requiem Revolver for head shots and weak-point destruction, MSBG 500 W870 Police shotgun for close-range core damage in Phase 2, and at least 2 Hand Grenades for clustered pustule waves and ground orb clusters during the collapse phase.</p>
</details>
<details>
<summary><strong>Can I die during the QTE finisher?</strong></summary>
<p>Yes; failing the button prompts kills Leon and forces a restart of Phase 2. Default mapping is square/X repeatedly followed by triangle/Y. The button prompts are the same family used throughout the campaign, so practice them in earlier QTE moments to build muscle memory.</p>
</details>
