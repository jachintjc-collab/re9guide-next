<h1>RE9 Insanity NG+ complete survival guide — checklist, charm loadouts, boss strategies, save management</h1>
<p>Insanity is RE9's hardest difficulty. NG+ Insanity combines inherited Mortal Edge knife and Hand Cannon with the franchise's most punishing combat tuning. This guide covers the complete survival checklist: preparation, loadout, chapter-by-chapter strategies, boss tactics, save management, and recovery from common run-killers. Tested on v1.02.</p>

<blockquote>Insanity NG+ is harder than first-Insanity. Inherited tools help, but the difficulty curve is steeper. Most players who try Insanity NG+ first time fail in chapter 3.</blockquote>

<h2>Why Insanity NG+ specifically</h2>
<p>RE9's Insanity NG+ is the canonical "hardest meaningful run" — harder than first-playthrough Insanity (which is borderline impossible without prior playthrough experience) and harder than New Game Plus on Standard or Hardcore (which provides power but not challenge). The trophy True Survivor requires 100% completion on Insanity, which in practice means Insanity NG+. The difficulty is the franchise's most demanding sustained challenge in over a decade.</p>
<p>You should attempt Insanity NG+ only after: completing Standard at least twice for game knowledge, completing Hardcore once for intermediate challenge familiarity, completing first-Insanity once to unlock Hand Cannon and Mortal Edge knife, and accumulating at least 60 hours total RE9 playtime.</p>

<h2>Required inheritance from previous saves</h2>
<p>Hand Cannon Prototype: required. This handgun trivializes boss encounters that would otherwise require near-flawless execution. Save Hand Cannon ammunition for chapter 3 Plant 43 boss, chapter 7 Commander X1, and chapter 8 Eveline phases 2 and 3.</p>
<p>Mortal Edge knife: required. Provides 2.4x base knife damage and unlocks frame-perfect parry damage bonuses. Without Mortal Edge, knife combat is non-viable on Insanity.</p>
<p>Fully upgraded W870 Police shotgun: required. Mid-range stagger damage that keeps you alive in close encounters.</p>
<p>Marksman's Eye charm: required.  Required for headshot reliability against Insanity-tuned enemies.</p>
<p>Endurance charm: highly recommended. Damage reduction that converts certain one-shot scenarios into survivable damage.</p>

<h2>Recommended charm loadout (3 active slots)</h2>
<p>Slot 1: Endurance. Required for Insanity. Damage reduction against attacks that would otherwise one-shot you.</p>
<p>Slot 2: Marksman's Eye. Required for Insanity. Compensates for reduced ammo drops via higher per-shot effectiveness.</p>
<p>Slot 3: Choice between Quickstep (faster dodge frames, recommended for parry-focused play) or Phoenix Down (auto-revive at 1 HP, recommended for survival-focused play).</p>
<p>Don't equip: Knife Edge (use Mortal Edge knife base damage instead), Lucky Star (Marksman's Eye is strictly better), Sharp Vision (too situational).</p>

<h2>Chapter 1 Wrenwood — survival baseline</h2>
<p>Wrenwood is RE9's tutorial chapter. Insanity makes the tutorial considerably harder than Standard. The bridge sequence — slip mechanic on wet planks — will kill you on Insanity if you run instead of walk with the stability button held.</p>
<p>Key tactics: walk all wet surfaces, save before any enemy encounter, use the Silencer 9 only for stealth-killable isolated zombies. The hotel basement has three explosive gas tanks; trigger them with one grenade to clear groups efficiently while collecting the chapter 1 chapter-locked Mr. Raccoon (receptionist desk drawer — back side).</p>
<p>Target completion time chapter 1: 35-45 minutes on Insanity NG+. Allow 15 extra minutes for save scumming if needed.</p>

<h2>Chapter 2 Wrenwood completion — the hotel sequence</h2>
<p>Chapter 2 transitions Wrenwood to Rhodes Hill. The chapter-locked Mr. Raccoon at the rooftop must be collected before the chapter 3 transition. Insanity respawn rates make some encounters more dangerous than Standard.</p>
<p>Key tactics: collect the Brass Whistle from Wrenwood post office (hidden behind locked back door). The whistle's audio lure utility is significantly more valuable on Insanity than Standard. Use it to bypass Cerberus encounters rather than fighting them.</p>
<p>Save strategy: save at chapter end. Don't proceed to chapter 3 without a clean save.</p>

<h2>Chapter 3 Care Center — the difficulty wall</h2>
<p>Chapter 3 is where most Insanity NG+ players fail their first attempt. The Vivien Plant 43 hybrid boss and the chapter 3 Blister Borne encounters compound on Insanity in ways that earlier playthroughs don't prepare you for.</p>
<p>Blister Borne hybrid strategy: body shots, not headshots. The center-mass weak point (covered in the virology-blister-borne-pathology page) is essential on Insanity. Standard headshot mentality kills you on Insanity. Pattern: shotgun stagger from medium range, then Mortal Edge knife at close range. The parry-counter pattern works but the frame window shrinks to 1 frame on Insanity.</p>
<p>Vivien Plant 43 boss strategy: phase 1 is dodge-focused. Phase 2 introduces the tendril whip attack with a 1-frame parry window. Use Hand Cannon (1 round) at phase 1 end to skip phase 2's most dangerous patterns. Phase 3 is parry-focused; use Mortal Edge for guaranteed parry damage. Total expected ammunition usage: 2 Hand Cannon rounds, 6 W870 shells, 4 Match Pistol rounds.</p>
<p>Save before the boss room. Reload on any HP drop. The boss has no checkpoint.</p>

<h2>Chapter 4 traverse — the pacing problem</h2>
<p>Chapter 4 is RE9's worst-paced chapter. On Insanity NG+, the over-long traverse becomes a save management problem rather than a combat problem.</p>
<p>Skip strategy: use the forest path (Korean speedrun community's documented chapter 4 glitch saves 6 minutes). Pacifist trophy attempts add stealth complexity but are not required for Insanity NG+. Standard non-Pacifist run is faster.</p>
<p>Pacing target: complete chapter 4 in 30 minutes. If you're past 45 minutes, you're over-exploring; cut content and proceed.</p>

<h2>Chapter 5 Rhodes Hill — the puzzle chapter</h2>
<p>The Rhodes Hill manor quartz puzzle is the franchise's worst-designed puzzle. On Insanity, the puzzle's frustration compounds with the combat pressure outside the manor.</p>
<p>Puzzle solution: code 560. Sum of hidden numbers on four manor paintings. The input is the actual difficulty — adjust controller sensitivity to lower setting before attempting. PC mouse users should set DPI below 1000.</p>
<p>Combat strategy: Father Riley confrontation in chapter 5 is dialogue-only; no combat. Manor exterior Blister Borne encounters use the same body-shot strategy as chapter 3. Conservatory Plant 43 hybrid boss is the chapter's main combat — strategy identical to chapter 3 Vivien boss.</p>

<h2>Chapter 6 RPD — the institutional sabotage</h2>
<p>Chapter 6 includes the Mr. X chase. On Insanity, his contact damage is one-shot at default health. The chase is pure routing — combat ineffective against T-103 in any survivable form.</p>
<p>Correct route: archive room exit → western corridor → main lobby → eastern stairwell. Total time 3-4 minutes if executed correctly. Mr. X intercepts on incorrect routes. Korean speedrun community documented the precise route on Naver.</p>
<p>Don't attempt: chief's office (secondary patrol), basement shortcut (Cerberus trigger), library aisle 7B unless you're collecting the chapter-locked Mr. Raccoon (timing-critical).</p>
<p>RPD documents are critical for true ending unlock. Read all five required chapter 6 archive documents before exiting RPD. The Marcus Bridges case file specifically requires the manor study key from chapter 5.</p>

<h2>Chapter 7 ARK — the Commander X1 fight</h2>
<p>Chapter 7's Commander X1 boss is RE9's franchise-best boss design but also its second-hardest fight (Eveline phase 2 is hardest). His three phases require different strategies.</p>
<p>Phase 1 (HP 100-70%): direct rush + dodge pattern. Use W870 stagger + Hand Cannon (1 round) to skip mid-phase to phase 2.</p>
<p>Phase 2 (HP 70-40%): keep distance. Acid spit attack cannot be parried — must dodge. Tendril whip has 1-frame parry window on Insanity. Use Marksman's Eye for crit damage.</p>
<p>Phase 3 (HP 40-0%): grab attack added. Phase 3 grab is instant kill on Insanity. Maintain distance over 4 meters; the grab attack only triggers at close range. Quickstep dodge frames are essential.</p>
<p>The dialogue resolution after fight is critical — Commander X1 reveals the Master Plagas integration program and 62 candidate count. This information matters for RE10 preparation.</p>

<h2>Chapter 8 Eveline — the final climb</h2>
<p>Eveline final boss is three phases. Phase 2 tendril sweep is the franchise's most-deadly single attack on Insanity. Phase 3 is the multi-voice chorus phase.</p>
<p>Phase 1 strategy: parry-counter pattern. Mold network manifestations are predictable. Use Mortal Edge knife liberally; conserve firearm ammunition for phases 2-3.</p>
<p>Phase 2 strategy: tendril whip parry on 1-frame Insanity window. Tendril sweep cannot be parried — dodge through. Position management: stay within 3 meters of Eveline's center to avoid the sweep arc. Use Hand Cannon (2 rounds) to compress phase 2 timing.</p>
<p>Phase 3 strategy: parry-only. Multi-voice harmonics indicate parry windows. The fight ends shortly after entering phase 3 if you have conserved Hand Cannon ammunition for phase 3 finisher.</p>
<p>Critical dialogue: for true ending, choose "I don't accept this" as the third dialogue option. This option only appears if all five chapter 6 archive documents were read.</p>

<h2>Save management framework</h2>
<p>RE9 provides 6 manual save slots plus autosave at chapter transitions and safe rooms. On Insanity NG+, save management is the primary survival skill.</p>
<p>Rotation strategy: dedicate slots 1-3 to chapter-end saves (rotate as you progress). Dedicate slot 4 to pre-boss save (overwrite before each boss). Dedicate slot 5 to current-area save (overwrite frequently during exploration). Dedicate slot 6 to pre-critical-decision save (chapter 8 endings, chapter 5 dialogue choices).</p>
<p>Cloud backup: enable PS5 cloud save sync or Steam cloud sync. Without cloud backup, save corruption (rare but possible) destroys 25+ hours of progress. Do not attempt Insanity NG+ without cloud backup enabled.</p>

<h2>Common run-killers and recovery</h2>
<p>One-shot kills on Insanity bosses are the most common run-killers. Endurance charm prevents many. Phoenix Down auto-revive prevents one per encounter.</p>
<p>Save corruption — extremely rare but devastating. Cloud backup prevents. Recovery: restore from cloud, replay lost segment.</p>
<p>Quartz puzzle frustration in chapter 5 — adjust controller sensitivity before attempting. If unable to register input after 10 attempts, use sensitivity adjustment.</p>
<p>Mr. X chase failure — restart chapter 6 from beginning. Use Korean Naver routing precisely. Don't experiment.</p>
<p>Boss strategy failure — reload pre-boss save. Don't try to "recover" mid-fight; the boss has too much HP for ammunition-poor recovery attempts.</p>

<h2>Estimated time investment</h2>
<p>Skilled player familiar with RE9: 25-30 hours for clean Insanity NG+ run.</p>
<p>Average player familiar with Standard: 35-45 hours including learning curve.</p>
<p>Add: 10-20 hours for trophy completion (True Survivor specifically requires 100% on Insanity).</p>
<p>Total realistic time investment: 50-65 hours for complete Insanity NG+ True Survivor run.</p>

<h2>Closing — when to attempt and when to wait</h2>
<p>Attempt Insanity NG+ only after completing Standard, Hardcore, and first-Insanity. The unlocks matter; the experience matters more.</p>
<p>Don't attempt if you have not slept properly the previous night. Insanity NG+ requires sustained concentration over multiple sessions.</p>
<p>Don't attempt if your hardware is unstable. Save corruption on Insanity NG+ erases days of work.</p>
<p>The trophy is real but the time investment is the real cost. Plan accordingly.</p>
