<h2 id="tldr">TL;DR — which difficulty should you play?</h2>
<ul>
 <li><strong>First-time RE player:</strong> Casual. RE9 is the franchise's hardest entry; Casual is genuinely casual without trivializing the story.</li>
 <li><strong>Familiar with RE2 Remake / RE4 Remake:</strong> Standard. This is the intended experience; both story tension and combat feel right.</li>
 <li><strong>Want a real challenge but not torture:</strong> Hardcore. Tight ammo, limited saves, but no Insanity-only one-shot attacks.</li>
 <li><strong>Trophy hunter / completionist:</strong> Insanity NG+ with carryover gear. Required for 3 trophies + the Survivor gold trophy.</li>
 <li><strong>If you don't care about trophies but want the "best version":</strong> Standard first, then NG+ Hardcore. Skip Insanity unless you have 8+ hours to spare per replay.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Critical insight:</strong> The jump from Hardcore → Insanity is much steeper than Standard → Hardcore. Insanity's 2.2x enemy HP + 2.4x damage taken + 0.4x ammo means you're fighting more enemies, with more bullets, while taking more damage. a triple-stack difficulty curve.</p>

<p>The biggest jump is on Insanity: <strong>Lickers gain a heartbeat sensor that pierces all stealth</strong>. This single change forces you to engage Lickers head-on (or run past), removing one of the franchise's traditional crowd-control tactics.</p>

<h2 id="boss-changes">Boss attack pattern changes (Insanity-only moves)</h2>
<p>Several bosses gain entirely new attacks on Insanity that don't exist on lower difficulties:</p>

<ul>
 <li><strong><a href="/how-to-beat-victor-gideon/">Victor Gideon</a></strong>; Insanity adds a Phase 4 "Mortal Edge spam" sequence: 6 consecutive Mortal Edge attacks in 4 seconds. Requires perfect parry timing or instant death. This phase doesn't exist on Hardcore or below.</li>

 <li><strong><a href="/how-to-beat-the-commander/">The Commander</a></strong> — Insanity adds an instant-execute "command override" attack when player HP drops below 30%. Counters Eye Spy Charm. the charm CAN'T save you from this attack. Only avoidable via dodge-roll cancel.</li>

 <li><strong><a href="/how-to-beat-titan-spinner/">Titan Spinner</a></strong>; Insanity adds an entire 4th phase: after defeating the main body, an exposed core appears with 5x damage attacks. Total fight length doubles.</li>

 <li><strong><a href="/how-to-beat-chunk/">Chunk</a></strong> — Insanity changes the final QTE prompt from 5 seconds to 3 seconds. Misses default to "Destroy Sample" (same as lower difficulties), but the time pressure means many players accidentally fail the prompt entirely.</li>

 <li><strong><a href="/how-to-beat-mr-x-super-tyrant/">Mr. X / Super Tyrant</a></strong>, Insanity makes Mr. X effectively un-stunnable. Even Requiem Revolver headshots only stagger him for 0.3 seconds (vs 2 seconds on Hardcore).</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>The Insanity save strategy:</strong> Save only before bosses and major decision points. Treat each ribbon as gold; never save "just in case." Many Insanity completionists end the campaign with 1-2 ribbons unused because they've trained themselves to push through.</p>

<p><strong>NG+ ribbon carry:</strong> Unused ribbons DO carry to NG+. If you finish Hardcore with 5 unused ribbons, your NG+ Hardcore run starts with 8 (3 base + 5 carryover), a significant safety buffer.</p>

<h2 id="path">Recommended difficulty progression path</h2>

<p><strong>Casual approach (lowest risk, slowest):</strong> Casual → Standard → Hardcore → Insanity. 4 separate playthroughs. ~60 hours total. Best for first-time RE players.</p>

<p><strong>Optimal approach (recommended):</strong> Standard → NG+ Hardcore → NG+ Insanity. 3 playthroughs. ~30 hours total. Skip Casual since you can experience the intended difficulty curve on Standard, and Hardcore is meaningful preparation for Insanity.</p>

<p><strong>Trophy-rush approach:</strong> Standard → NG+ Insanity (skip Hardcore!). 2 playthroughs. ~24 hours. ONLY viable if you're a returning RE veteran. You'll lack Infinite Requiem Revolver for Insanity which makes it brutal. See our <a href="/re9-new-game-plus-complete-guide/">NG+ complete guide</a> for whether this is feasible for you.</p>

<p><strong>Speedrunner approach:</strong> Standard (with optimal route) → NG+ Hardcore (verify Infinite Requiem Revolver). Total ~14 hours. Doesn't include Insanity but unlocks 90% of content.</p>

<h2 id="worth">Is Insanity worth it? (Honest verdict)</h2>

<p><strong>For trophy hunters:</strong> Mandatory. 3 trophies are Insanity-exclusive and required for Platinum.</p>

<p><strong>For lore enthusiasts:</strong> Highly recommended. Insanity unlocks the full Chunk codex (Marcus Halverson backstory), the Eveline vat appearance, and the 47-name dev thank-you card. See our <a href="/chunk-origin-mystery-explained/">Chunk origin mystery</a> for what you'd miss.</p>

<p><strong>For pure gameplay enjoyment:</strong> Depends. Insanity's boss-fight changes are mechanically interesting; Victor Gideon's Phase 4 and Titan Spinner's expanded fight are genuinely better designed than their Standard versions. But the constant ammo scarcity can feel grindy.</p>

<p><strong>The honest "skip Insanity" case:</strong> If you finished Hardcore and felt "I got what I wanted from this game," skipping Insanity is reasonable. You miss 3 trophies and some codex content. The 8-12 hour replay isn't for everyone.</p>

<p><strong>The honest "play Insanity" case:</strong> If you enjoyed Hardcore's tension and want the deepest RE combat experience the franchise has ever offered, Insanity is the franchise's peak technical achievement. The Victor Gideon Phase 4 fight alone is widely cited as the best single boss encounter in RE history.</p>

<h2 id="troubleshoot">"I'm stuck on difficulty X". decision tree</h2>

<p><strong>Stuck on Standard's Chunk fight?</strong> → Drop to Casual to finish the campaign. Standard is hard if you skip optional resource gathering. Don't feel bad; ~30% of players downshift here.</p>

<p><strong>Stuck on Hardcore's Victor Gideon?</strong> → Equip <a href="/re9-eye-spy-charm-explained/">Eye Spy Charm</a> (carries from PT1) + stockpile 4 First Aid Sprays before entering. If still stuck, downshift to Standard mid-fight; you can change difficulty up to a difficulty boundary.</p>

<p><strong>Stuck on Insanity's any boss?</strong> → Use carry-over Requiem Revolver from NG+ Hardcore. If you don't have Infinite Requiem Revolver, you skipped the recommended path — go back to NG+ Hardcore first.</p>

<p><strong>Stuck on Insanity's zombie hordes (non-boss)?</strong> → Switch from combat-heavy to stealth-heavy playstyle. Crouch-walk past 60% of encounters. Save bullets for bosses only.</p>

<p><strong>Stuck on Insanity's ammo scarcity?</strong> → Make crafting your primary income source. Carry 8+ knives for melee finishers (knife kills don't spend ammo and give +1 crafting yield).</p>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ, RE9 difficulty mechanics</h2>

<p><strong>Q: Can I change difficulty mid-playthrough?</strong><br>
A: Only downward. Insanity → Hardcore is allowed at any save point. Going up (Standard → Hardcore) is locked once you start a difficulty.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Does difficulty affect endings?</strong><br>
A: Mostly no; all 8 endings are accessible across difficulties EXCEPT: Grace Insanity and Leon Insanity endings require Insanity-difficulty clear. See <a href="/re9-all-endings-explained/">our endings guide</a> for full conditions.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Do trophies carry across NG+ difficulty changes?</strong><br>
A: Yes. Earned trophies persist. If you got "Hardcore Complete" on PT1, it's yours even if you replay on Casual.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Is Casual considered "easy mode shame"?</strong><br>
A: No. Capcom explicitly designed Casual for narrative-focused players. Story experience is identical; you just spend less time fighting. Many critics played Casual for review playthroughs.</p>

<p><strong>Q: How does difficulty affect bosses I've beaten before in lower difficulties?</strong><br>
A: It doesn't. Each playthrough is a fresh attempt. Even if you 100%-ed Victor Gideon on Standard, you fight him fresh on Insanity.</p>

<p><strong>Q: What's the percentage of players who actually finish Insanity?</strong><br>
A: PS5 trophy data (May 2026): 4.2% of owners have the "Insanity Complete" trophy. ~12% have Hardcore. ~78% have Standard or higher. Casual exclusives are ~6%.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Can I use Infinite Requiem Revolver on Insanity for trophies?</strong><br>
A: Yes. Infinite Ammo does NOT void trophies in RE9 (unlike some prior RE entries). Use it freely for safer Insanity completion.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Does Eye Spy Charm work on Insanity?</strong><br>
A: Yes, but with one caveat: The Commander's Insanity-only "command override" instant-execute attack bypasses Eye Spy Charm. All other death-prevention works normally.</p>
