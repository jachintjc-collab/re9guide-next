<h2 id="what">What the Eye Spy Charm does</h2>
<p>The Eye Spy Charm is a small obsidian eye-shaped pendant equipped in the Charm slot of Grace's inventory (or Leon's, if you're playing the second campaign). When Grace takes lethal damage, a hit that would drop her HP to 0 — the charm activates <strong>before the death animation plays</strong>, auto-consuming the highest-tier healing item in her inventory.</p>
<p>The save is silent and instant. You'll see a flash of red around the screen edges and a faint chime, but you don't lose control or get pushed into a cutscene. It's identical in feel to the Felyne Insurance skill in Monster Hunter; most players don't even notice the first time it triggers.</p>

<div>
 <strong>⚠ KEY LIMITATION:</strong> The charm requires <em>at least one healing item</em> in your inventory. If you're empty, the charm does nothing and Grace dies. Always keep at least 1 Green Herb on hand if you're wearing this.
</div>

<h2 id="drop">Where it drops (Chunk fight)</h2>
<p>The Eye Spy Charm is the reward for defeating <strong><a href="how-to-beat-chunk.html">Chunk</a></strong> (the deformed cellar boss in Chapter 1) <em>without taking damage in the final phase</em>. If Grace gets hit during Phase 3 (the lunge phase, when Chunk's eye is exposed), the charm is replaced by a generic <strong>Strength Charm +1</strong> drop.</p>
<p>This is the <strong>only place in the entire game</strong> the Eye Spy Charm appears. There's no second copy in NG+, no DLC version, no shop sell. Missing it on your first Chunk fight means you have to NG+ to get it (Chunk respawns).</p>

<p><strong>How to no-damage Phase 3 quickly:</strong></p>
<ol>
 <li>Stock 4+ Requiem Revolver rounds <em>before</em> entering the cellar.</li>
 <li>When Chunk roars and his eye opens (Phase 3 start), back up to the wooden crate near the cellar door.</li>
 <li>Wait for the lunge animation start (~1.2 sec wind-up). The moment his head dips, fire 2 Requiem Revolver rounds at the eye.</li>
 <li>Side-roll left as the lunge passes. He'll slam into the crate, briefly stunned.</li>
 <li>Fire 2 more Requiem Revolver rounds at the eye while he's stunned. Eye breaks → Chunk falls → fight ends.</li>
 <li>Charm drops on his head where the eye was. <strong>Pick it up</strong>; auto-equipping is OFF by default.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="trigger">Exact trigger rules</h2>
<p>The charm has a specific trigger logic. Misunderstanding it gets people killed:</p>
<ul>
 <li><strong>Triggers on:</strong> any hit that would drop HP from &gt;0 to 0. Includes boss attacks, environmental damage, status effects.</li>
 <li><strong>Does NOT trigger on:</strong> instant-kill grabs (Mr. X grab, Plant 43 swallow, Commander insta-execute on Insanity), poison/bleed ticks that go from 1 HP to 0 (counts as DOT, not a "hit").</li>
 <li><strong>One activation per heal item</strong>: if you have 3 First Aid Sprays, the charm can save you 3 times in a row.</li>
 <li><strong>No cooldown between activations</strong>: you can theoretically be saved twice in 1 second if you have 2+ heals.</li>
 <li><strong>Replaces the heal animation</strong>: the heal is instant, no opening for Grace to be punished.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="priority">Healing item priority order</h2>
<p>When the charm triggers, it always uses the <strong>highest-tier healing item</strong> in your inventory. The priority order is:</p>
<div>
 <table>
 <tr><th>Priority</th><th>Item</th><th>HP restored</th></tr>
 <tr><td><strong>1</strong></td><td>First Aid Spray</td><td>Full HP</td></tr>
 <tr><td><strong>2</strong></td><td>Green + Red + Yellow Herb mix</td><td>Full HP + max HP up</td></tr>
 <tr><td><strong>3</strong></td><td>Green + Red Herb mix</td><td>Full HP</td></tr>
 <tr><td><strong>4</strong></td><td>Green + Yellow Herb mix</td><td>~75% HP + small max HP up</td></tr>
 <tr><td><strong>5</strong></td><td>Green Herb (single)</td><td>~50% HP</td></tr>
 </table>
</div>
<p><strong>Strategy tip:</strong> if you're going into a tough fight, <strong>keep one First Aid Spray in inventory as a charm reserve</strong>. The charm will burn it for full heal at the worst possible moment — exactly when you'd otherwise reload from a checkpoint.</p>

<h2 id="insanity">Why it's S-tier on Insanity</h2>
<p>On Insanity difficulty, several bosses (<a href="how-to-beat-victor-gideon.html">Victor Gideon</a>, <a href="how-to-beat-the-commander.html">The Commander</a>, <a href="how-to-beat-titan-spinner.html">Titan Spinner</a>) have one-shot attacks if you're below ~70% HP. The Eye Spy Charm effectively gives you a "second chance" on these one-shots; you eat the hit, the charm uses a First Aid Spray, and you're back to full HP in the same frame.</p>
<p>Speedrunners and Insanity completionists treat the Eye Spy Charm as <strong>mandatory equipment</strong>. There's no other charm that comes close. Strength Charms add 10% damage, Defense Charms add 8% damage resistance, but neither matters if you eat one bad hit and die.</p>
<p><strong>If you missed the Eye Spy Charm on your first run:</strong> finish the campaign, start NG+ (carries over weapons/items), beat Chunk no-damage on Phase 3 (much easier with Requiem Revolver+ from your previous run), then equip the charm before each tough fight in your second playthrough.</p>
