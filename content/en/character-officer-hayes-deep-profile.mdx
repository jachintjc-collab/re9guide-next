<h1>Officer Hayes — Wrenwood PD detective, complicit local authority</h1>
<p>Officer Hayes is RE9's Wrenwood PD point-of-contact. Local detective, mid-40s, complicit in the cover-up that killed the three previous investigators. His chapter 2 interactions establish the local authority hostility framework.</p>

<blockquote>Officer Hayes is the franchise's best small-supporting-villain in years. Locally evil rather than globally evil. Worth attention even though he's not a major character.</blockquote>

<h2>Background</h2>
<p>Officer Hayes is Wrenwood PD detective. Local resident, 22-year career, family ties to Wrenwood elite. Approximately 45 years old. Reports to Wrenwood PD Chief who's also implicated.</p>

<h2>Role in cover-up</h2>
<p>Hayes was the case officer for two of the three previous investigator deaths. He signed off on the "suicide" and "hiking accident" rulings. His complicity is documented in RE9 chapter 6 RPD recovered files.</p>

<h2>Chapter 2 interactions</h2>
<p>Hayes meets Grace as cooperating local contact. The meeting is performative — Hayes is gathering intelligence on Grace's investigation methods. His friendly demeanor is professional cover.</p>

<h2>Voice work</h2>
<p>Hayes is voiced by uncredited Capcom internal cast. The performance reads as professional friendliness with subtle wrongness. Players who pay attention catch the discomfort.</p>

<h2>His chapter 5 absence</h2>
<p>Hayes notably doesn't appear after chapter 4. The implication is that he reports to Wrenwood elite who handle direct Grace-elimination attempts. Hayes is mid-level corrupt, not active killer.</p>

<h2>Korean reception</h2>
<p>Korean RE9 community noticed Hayes's supporting villain quality. Naver discussion threads on supporting characters specifically credit Hayes as best minor antagonist.</p>

<h2>RE10 trajectory</h2>
<p>Hayes will likely not return in RE10 (different setting, different institutional villains). His character serves chapter 2-4 specifically.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Hayes is the kind of supporting villain that elevates RE9's overall character work. Most RE games skip the local-corruption level and jump to global conspiracy. RE9 builds the corruption from local to institutional. Hayes is the local layer.</p>
