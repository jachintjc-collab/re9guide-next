<h1>RE9 second trailer (TGS 2025) — combat reveal + reading the shift</h1>
<p>The second trailer at Tokyo Game Show 2025 was the gameplay reveal. Three minutes, combat-heavy, boss tease included. The tonal shift from the May 2025 reveal trailer is jarring on rewatch. Here's what changed and why.</p>

<blockquote>The TGS trailer is where RE9's marketing went wrong. Too much combat footage too early. The reveal trailer's restraint was correct; this trailer broke that and the franchise paid for it in early reception.</blockquote>

<h2>The full transcript</h2>
<div>
<p>[Cold open: aerial shot of Wrenwood at dusk. Music: low pulse.]</p>
<p><strong>NARRATOR (Grace, voice-over):</strong> "When Capcom asked me what I'd do if I had one more chance..."</p>
<p>[Cut: Grace draws her Silencer 9 in a hotel lobby. First combat frame.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "...I said I'd do this. One more time."</p>
<p>[Quick cuts: Mortal Edge parry, shotgun discharge, Chunk reveal, Blister Borne approach.]</p>
<p><strong>LEON (over comms):</strong> "Grace, you need to fall back. The Care Center is compromised."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Not yet."</p>
<p>[Cut to Vivien Davies (back to camera, lab coat): "Father Riley warned you."]</p>
<p>[Logo card. Release date: <strong>FEBRUARY 27, 2026</strong>]</p>
</div>

<h2>Why this trailer broke the reveal's tone</h2>
<p>The reveal trailer sold mystery. This trailer sells combat. The two narratives don't reconcile cleanly. Players who came in expecting RE7-style atmospheric horror got TGS footage that looked like RE2 Remake combat with a Plant 43 cameo.</p>
<p>Internal Capcom interviews after launch (Nakanishi at GDC 2026) confirmed the TGS trailer was pushed harder on combat than the dev team wanted. Marketing wanted gameplay; the dev team wanted continued atmosphere. Marketing won.</p>

<h2>Voice cast for this trailer</h2>
<p>Cissy Jones (Grace) delivers her voice-over with restraint. The line "Not yet" is the trailer's emotional anchor.</p>
<p>Nick Apostolides (Leon) gets one over-comms line. Brief but the franchise continuity establishes Leon as supporting protagonist, not Grace's equal.</p>
<p>Laila Berzins (Vivien) gets one line with her back to camera. Smart blocking — the unrevealed face is more menacing than a full reveal.</p>

<h2>Korean dub for TGS</h2>
<p>The Korean dub at TGS was a Capcom Korea exclusive premiere. Korean reception emphasized Grace's "이번엔 끝까지 갈 거야" line (the Korean rendering of "Not yet") as the trailer's emotional peak. The English subtitles back at translation: Korean cast worked off a shared script with Capcom Japan oversight.</p>

<h2>Production notes</h2>
<p>The trailer was assembled from chapter 1-3 footage, with some shots from chapter 6 (the Leon-Grace comms shot is from chapter 6 actually, not chapter 3 as the editing implies). The Vivien tease shot was filmed for the trailer specifically; it doesn't appear in the final game.</p>
<p>Director-level oversight: Yuya Nakanishi reviewed the trailer cut but the final edit was Capcom marketing's call. This shows in the pacing — the trailer cuts faster than Nakanishi's in-game direction style.</p>

<h2>Reception data</h2>
<p>Western reception was positive (8.5/10 average on enthusiast site reviews of the trailer). Japanese reception was mixed — some commentary criticized the combat emphasis as undermining the reveal trailer's tone. Korean reception split roughly down the middle on Naver forums.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>The TGS trailer shows the gap between what Capcom's marketing wanted and what Nakanishi wanted to make. The released game leans toward Nakanishi. The trailer leans toward marketing. Knowing which trailer you preferred tells you whether you were going to like the actual game.</p>
