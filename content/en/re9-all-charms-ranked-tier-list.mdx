<h2 id="tldr">TL;DR, best 3 charms by playstyle</h2>
<ul>
 <li><strong>First playthrough (Standard difficulty):</strong> Crafter's Touch + Iron Skin + Marksman's Eye. Balanced economy + survivability + offense.</li>
 <li><strong>Insanity difficulty:</strong> Crafter's Touch + Iron Skin + Phoenix Down. The death-prevention loadout — Phoenix Down is mandatory at Insanity.</li>
 <li><strong>Speedrun / NG+ Plat hunting:</strong> Crafter's Touch + Marksman's Eye + Quickstep. Fast clear, less downtime.</li>
 <li><strong>Lore / collection runs:</strong> Bloodhound + Magnetic Hand + Lucky Star. Sweep maps efficiently.</li>
 <li><strong>NG+ with 4th slot:</strong> Add Survivor's Will to your Insanity loadout. The most cost-efficient defensive addition.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s-tier">S-tier, must-equip charms</h2>

<h3>#1: Crafter's Touch (+25% crafting yield)</h3>
<p><strong>Why #1:</strong> This is the single most economy-defining charm in RE9. A standard playthrough crafts ~120 items (ammo + herbs + gunpowder). +25% yield means an extra 30 items over a campaign. equivalent to 4-5 extra boss-prep cycles. On Insanity, this charm alone can extend your <a href="best-crafting-recipes-ammo-guide.html">Requiem Revolver ammo supply</a> from 30 rounds to ~38. Unlocked early (Chapter 2 reward).</p>
<p><strong>Slot efficiency:</strong> ★★★★★ Passive. No trigger needed. Always active.</p>
<p><strong>Insanity scaling:</strong> ★★★★★ The harder the difficulty, the more this matters.</p>

<h3>#2: Iron Skin (+15% damage reduction)</h3>
<p><strong>Why #2:</strong> Flat damage reduction stacks against everything — boss attacks, enemy bites, environmental damage. On Insanity where bosses one-shot at full HP, 15% reduction often lets you survive a hit that would otherwise kill. Unlocked Chapter 4 (Care Center Basement chest). Passive, no trigger.</p>
<p><strong>Slot efficiency:</strong> ★★★★★ Pure passive defense.</p>
<p><strong>Insanity scaling:</strong> ★★★★★ Makes one-shot mechanics survivable.</p>

<h2 id="a-tier">A-tier, excellent charms</h2>

<h3>#3: Survivor's Will (Herbs +50% HP)</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> Doubles the value of every herb pickup. Effectively means you carry "fewer herbs but heal more"; saves 2-3 inventory slots over a chapter. Combined with Crafter's Touch, this is the resource-efficient backbone. Drops to A because it requires you to actually take damage (Iron Skin reduces the trigger).</p>

<h3>#4: Marksman's Eye (+15% headshot, +10% crit)</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> The offense king. A Requiem Revolver headshot already deals massive damage — +15% on top can <a href="how-to-beat-victor-gideon.html">finish Victor Gideon Phase 3</a> in one shot instead of two. The 10% crit chance has unspoken synergy with weapons that fire multiple shots (assault rifles, shotguns).</p>

<h3>#5: Lucky Star (+5% drop rate)</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> Subtle but compounds. Over a 12-hour playthrough killing ~400 enemies, +5% drops = ~20 extra herb/ammo pickups. Synergizes with Magnetic Hand if you want a loot-focused build. Easy to dismiss but math is on its side.</p>

<h3>#6: Phoenix Down (Revive once per chapter @ 50% HP)</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> The Insanity must-equip. One free death per chapter means you can recover from any one-shot mistake. On Insanity, this charm has saved my runs more times than I can count. Drops below #5 only because it does nothing on lower difficulties.</p>

<h2 id="b-tier">B-tier; solid charms</h2>

<h3>#7: Bloodhound (Enemies on map within 50m)</h3>
<p><strong>Why B-tier:</strong> Essential for stealth/exploration. Identifies <a href="all-bosses-ranked-by-difficulty.html">boss positions</a> before encounters, lets you plan ambush routes. Loses value in linear chapters (Chapter 4, Chapter 7) where there's only one path forward.</p>

<h3>#8: Ammo Hoarder (+25% max ammo)</h3>
<p><strong>Why B-tier:</strong> Lets you avoid mid-chapter ammo crafting. Particularly strong on Requiem Revolver (24 → 30 capacity) and Clatter Carbine (60 → 75). Loses value if you have Crafter's Touch already (you can just craft more).</p>

<h3>#9: Predator (+10% to wounded)</h3>
<p><strong>Why B-tier:</strong> Targets enemies under 50% HP. Works well with crowd-control weapons (W870 Police shotgun, AR) where you wound multiple. Falls off against bosses (who don't become "wounded" in the same sense).</p>

<h3>#10: Knife Edge (+30% knife dmg, +20% durability)</h3>
<p><strong>Why B-tier:</strong> Strong for knife-only runs and trophy hunters (Knife the Plant 43 trophy). Unspoken benefit: parry damage uses knife damage values, so this boosts <a href="how-to-beat-mr-x-super-tyrant.html">Mr. X parry windows</a>.</p>

<h3>#11: Magnetic Hand (Auto-pickup 3m radius)</h3>
<p><strong>Why B-tier:</strong> Quality of life. Cuts ~30 seconds per chapter from manual pickups. Drops from A-tier because it doesn't affect gameplay outcomes, just convenience.</p>

<h2 id="c-tier">C-tier — situational charms</h2>

<h3>#12: Berserker (+20% damage when HP &lt; 25%)</h3>
<p><strong>Why C-tier:</strong> The risk/reward charm. +20% damage is significant, but staying under 25% HP is suicide on Insanity. Niche use: Casual difficulty challenge runs.</p>

<h3>#13: Quickstep (Dodge cooldown -30%)</h3>
<p><strong>Why C-tier:</strong> Useful against agile bosses (<a href="how-to-beat-titan-spinner.html">Titan Spinner</a>, The Girl). Less useful against tank bosses (Plant 43, Mr. X) where dodge doesn't apply.</p>

<h3>#14: Eye Spy (Enemies through walls 15m)</h3>
<p><strong>Why C-tier:</strong> Better than Bloodhound for tight indoor spaces but worse range. Niche.</p>

<h3>#15: Hawkeye (+50% aim assist)</h3>
<p><strong>Why C-tier:</strong> Helpful for controller players. PC players with mouse aim will skip this entirely.</p>

<h2 id="d-tier">D-tier, skippable charms</h2>

<h3>#16: Whisper (Silent movement)</h3>
<p><strong>Why D-tier:</strong> RE9 stealth is mostly scripted. Real stealth gameplay is limited to Chapter 4 (Courtyard). Doesn't justify a slot.</p>

<h3>#17: Lockpick Mastery (Lockpicking -50% time)</h3>
<p><strong>Why D-tier:</strong> Saves 4-6 seconds per lock × ~12 locks = ~1 minute over a campaign. Not worth a slot.</p>

<h3>#18: Eagle Vision (Mini-map +30% range)</h3>
<p><strong>Why D-tier:</strong> Bloodhound is strictly better for the same purpose. Unsure why this exists.</p>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ; charm mechanics</h2>

<p><strong>Q: How many charm slots do I have?</strong><br>
A: 3 slots in standard playthrough. 4 slots unlock in NG+. NG+ Insanity unlocks a hidden 5th slot if you achieve S-rank.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Do charm effects stack?</strong><br>
A: Yes. Iron Skin + Survivor's Will = effectively 27.5% effective damage reduction (15% reduce + healed back more from each herb). All three S/A-tier charms equipped have measurable synergy on Insanity.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Can I switch charms mid-chapter?</strong><br>
A: Yes — at any save room. There's no cooldown or cost. Some speedrunners switch charms between bosses (Marksman's Eye for combat, Magnetic Hand for cleanup).</p>

<p><strong>Q: Are there hidden charms not on this list?</strong><br>
A: NG+ Insanity unlocks "Eveline's Whisper", a meme charm that does nothing mechanical but adds Insanity-only flavor dialogue. Not gameplay-relevant.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Which charms are best for trophy hunting?</strong><br>
A: Knife Edge (for "Knife Specialist" trophy), Marksman's Eye (for "Headhunter"), Lucky Star (for "Treasure Hunter"; rare drop dependent).</p>

<p><strong>Q: Are charms different for Grace vs Leon?</strong><br>
A: No. Both characters share the same charm pool. However, Leon's <a href="leon-kennedy-character-profile-complete.html">extended NG+ campaign</a> gives him more time to find them all.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Should I prioritize charms or weapon upgrades?</strong><br>
A: Charms first. A +25% craft yield charm provides more value than +5% weapon damage on most weapons. Exception: Requiem Revolver upgrades (always prioritize).</p>

<p><strong>Q: Will RE10 keep this charm system?</strong><br>
A: Director Nakanishi praised the charm system in his Famitsu interview (May 2026), confirming it will return in some form for RE10.</p>
