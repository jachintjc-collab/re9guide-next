<h1>S&S M232 — the high-end handgun that's not as good as the community says</h1>
<p>S&S M232 is RE9's premium handgun, unlocked through chapter 5 merchant purchase. The community has elevated it to S-tier status. The data doesn't fully support that ranking. Here's the honest assessment.</p>

<blockquote>The S&S M232's reputation is inflated. It loses to the Hand Cannon in NG+ and most players won't admit it.</blockquote>

<h2>Core stats (v1.02)</h2>
<p>Base damage: 75. Headshot multiplier: 2.5x. Magazine: 7 rounds. Reload speed: 2.4s. Stagger threshold: high.</p>

<h2>Why the community loves it</h2>
<p>Single-shot stopping power. Reliable Standard zombie one-shot to head. Stagger threshold breaks Crimson Heads. Looks premium in inventory.</p>

<h2>Why I'm skeptical</h2>
<p>The damage-per-magazine math doesn't favor M232 over W870 Police shotgun for close engagements or over Match Pistol for medium-range precision. M232 is "good at everything, best at nothing."</p>

<h2>Where M232 wins</h2>
<p>Single chapter 6 Cerberus encounters (one-shot at moderate range). Chapter 7 mid-boss precision shots. Crimson Head clearing without ammo waste.</p>

<h2>Where it loses</h2>
<p>Boss encounters (Hand Cannon is better). Sustained engagements (W870 Police is better). Stealth (Silencer 9 is better). Precision (Match Pistol is better).</p>

<h2>Insanity considerations</h2>
<p>Insanity M232 is a niche tool. Use for specific Crimson Head clearing. Don't make it primary handgun on Insanity.</p>

<h2>Korean community tier</h2>
<p>Korean Naver RE9 community ranks M232 as S-tier. I disagree with the Korean consensus on this one. The Korean ranking is influenced by tier list confirmation bias — once it was S-ranked, subsequent commentary defended the ranking.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>M232 is a B+ handgun being marketed as S-tier. Buy it if you have the currency. Don't expect it to be the franchise game-changer the community claims.</p>
