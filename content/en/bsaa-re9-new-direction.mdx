<h2 id="history">Leon's 25-year non-BSAA history</h2>
<div>
 <table>
 <tr><th>Year</th><th>Leon's affiliation</th><th>Why not BSAA?</th></tr>
 <tr><td>1998</td><td>Raccoon City PD</td><td>BSAA didn't exist yet</td></tr>
 <tr><td>2004 (RE4)</td><td>US Secret Service (presidential detail)</td><td>BSAA still forming; Leon recruited by Hunnigan</td></tr>
 <tr><td>2009 (Damnation)</td><td>USSTRATCOM</td><td>BSAA exists but Leon stayed in US chain of command</td></tr>
 <tr><td>2013 (RE6)</td><td>DSO (Division of Security Operations)</td><td>Created specifically for Leon; he answered directly to POTUS</td></tr>
 <tr><td>2013-2026</td><td>DSO (continued)</td><td>13-year gap; Leon stayed independent of BSAA</td></tr>
 <tr><td>2026 (RE9)</td><td>BSAA Special Agent (NEW)</td><td>First time Leon and Chris are on the same official team</td></tr>
 </table>
</div>
<p>For 25 years, Leon and Chris were positioned as parallel agents — same mission, different employers. RE9 collapses this into a single command structure for the first time. This isn't a small character beat; it's the franchise's first major institutional realignment since BSAA's founding.</p>

<h2 id="scene">The True Ending; Chris meets Leon</h2>
<details>
 <summary>▶ REVEAL: Leon delivers sample to Chris (True Ending cutscene)</summary>
 <div><p><strong>Setup:</strong> Leon True Ending requires Grace True save + "Preserve Sample" in Leon's Chunk Round 2.</p><p><strong>The cutscene (~7 minutes):</strong> Leon escapes ARK with Grace, hands the RNA sample to a BSAA extraction team at a secure airstrip. Cut to a BSAA lab. Chris Redfield (older, grayer, weight of command visible) walks into frame.</p><p><strong>Key dialogue (verbatim):</strong></p><ul><li>Chris: "You did good, Kennedy."</li><li>Leon: "Don't say that. I don't do good. I just do necessary."</li><li>Chris: "Same thing in this line of work. Coffee?"</li><li>Leon: "..." (long pause) "Yeah. Coffee."</li></ul><p><strong>The sample destruction:</strong> Wide shot of BSAA lab incinerator burning the RNA sample. No experimentation, no reverse-engineering, destroyed completely. This is the first time on-screen that a BSAA cell handles a bioweapon sample by destroying it rather than studying it.</p><p><strong>The credits stinger:</strong> Leon is shown wearing a BSAA-issued tactical jacket — the first time he's ever worn BSAA insignia in canon. This visually confirms his official enlistment.</p></div>
</details>

<h2 id="doctrine">The new BSAA doctrine. "destroy, don't study"</h2>
<details>
 <summary>▶ Capcom's new "destroy don't study" BSAA doctrine</summary>
 <div><p><strong>What changed:</strong> Across RE5/RE6/RE Revelations, BSAA frequently studied bioweapon samples (sometimes leading to leaks). In RE9, the new doctrine; verbalized through Chris — is "containment over knowledge."</p><p><strong>In-game evidence:</strong> Chris's dialogue in the True Ending cutscene includes: <em>"We learned the hard way. Knowledge of these weapons isn't worth the cost of holding onto them."</em></p><p><strong>Why this matters:</strong> This is Capcom retconning 20 years of "BSAA scientific arm" plot lines. The 2025 BSAA is purely a destruction-focused org. RE10 will likely build new tensions around: which factions still want to study samples? (Foreshadowing: probably The Family or new Wesker bloodline.)</p><p><strong>Real-world parallel:</strong> Capcom's writers explicitly cited post-2020 pandemic discourse about bioweapons research as inspiration for this doctrinal shift.</p></div>
</details>

<h2 id="chris">Chris Redfield in 2026; aging franchise lead</h2>
<p>Chris is visibly older in RE9's True Ending. gray hair, deeper lines, a knee brace visible in one shot. Voice actor Roger Craig Smith returns but plays him notably wearier. This is consistent with RE8's portrayal where Chris was already showing wear.</p>
<p><strong>Why this matters narratively:</strong> Chris is being positioned for retirement. The franchise needs a new generation of leadership, and RE9 is the bridge — Chris hands off operational responsibility to Leon (now BSAA) while remaining as Director. This frees up the protagonist slot for Grace + future Rose Winters.</p>
<p>Datamined files include unused dialogue for Chris: <em>"This is my last big one, Leon. After this, it's your show."</em>, strong implication that Chris will be a supporting character or retire by RE10.</p>

<h2 id="dso">What happens to Leon's old agency (DSO)?</h2>
<p>The Division of Security Operations (DSO) was created specifically for Leon in 2013. With Leon now BSAA, what happens to DSO?</p>
<p>RE9 codex documents reveal:</p>
<ul>
 <li>DSO is being absorbed into BSAA's North American Branch (effectively dissolved)</li>
 <li>Hunnigan (Leon's longtime handler) transferred with him; she's now BSAA Communications Officer</li>
 <li>Helena Harper (Leon's RE6 partner) is mentioned but absent; possibly setup for a later return</li>
</ul>
<p>This consolidation removes a layer of franchise complexity. Going forward, agents are either BSAA, military, or independent — no more parallel US-government agencies cluttering the org chart.</p>

<h2 id="re10">How RE10 rebuilds around the new BSAA</h2>
<p>Three concrete predictions:</p>
<ol>
 <li><strong>Leon as BSAA frontman.</strong> Chris's implied retirement means RE10 likely opens with Leon as senior BSAA agent leading a new operation; possibly partnered with Grace.</li>
 <li><strong>BSAA's "destroy doctrine" creates new conflict.</strong> Foreshadowed: a rival faction (probably <a href="/ada-wong-re9-reversal-arc/">the Wesker bloodline tied to Ada's attacker</a>) wants to study bioweapons. RE10 conflict = BSAA destroy vs. rivals study.</li>
 <li><strong>Multi-protagonist structure becomes the new norm.</strong> RE9's dual-campaign (Grace + Leon) succeeded. RE10 will likely formalize this, possibly Leon/Grace as a duo with Mia as a third recurring ally.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ — BSAA in RE9</h2>

<p><strong>Q: Is Leon officially BSAA in RE9 canon?</strong><br>
A: Yes, but only in the True Ending path (requires Grace True + Leon True both played). The credits stinger shows him in a BSAA tactical jacket. visual canon confirmation.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Is Chris Redfield retiring?</strong><br>
A: Not officially in RE9, but datamined dialogue and visual aging strongly imply he'll retire by RE10 or appear in a supporting role only.</p>

<p><strong>Q: What happens to Sheva, Jill, and other RE5/RE6 BSAA characters?</strong><br>
A: Not mentioned in RE9. Capcom is keeping their slate open. The most likely scenario: they'll return in RE10 or DLC as part of BSAA's broader team.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Does the "destroy doctrine" actually hold in canon?</strong><br>
A: Yes, multiple in-game documents reinforce it. This is a real retcon of previous RE games where BSAA scientists studied samples (leading to multiple plot leaks).</p>

<p><strong>Q: Will Leon return to DSO if RE10 needs it?</strong><br>
A: DSO is dissolved in RE9's canon. Leon's BSAA tenure is permanent unless Capcom retcons it. Hunnigan's transfer to BSAA Communications signals this is meant to stick.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Why didn't Chris appear in Grace's campaign?</strong><br>
A: Capcom kept Chris exclusively in Leon's storyline to preserve the existing Chris-Leon dynamic and avoid overloading Grace's arc with franchise legacy characters.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Is BSAA an in-universe acronym players need to remember?</strong><br>
A: It stands for Bioterrorism Security Assessment Alliance. Established 2004 in RE5's backstory. Tagline: "Obey the call of duty." RE9 modernizes this with the new "Destroy, don't study" doctrine.</p>
