<h1>Worst RE9 music — Takano's tracks that don't land</h1>
<p>Mitsuhiko Takano composed RE9's score. Most tracks work. Specific tracks (chapter 4 traverse theme, chapter 7 corridor ambient) are notably weaker than the rest.</p>

<blockquote>RE9's score is overall strong. Two specific tracks underperform. The opening cinematic motif and the chapter 5 manor theme are franchise-best.</blockquote>

<h2>Chapter 4 traverse theme</h2><p>Generic atmospheric drone. Doesn't establish chapter mood. Should have been silence instead.</p><h2>Chapter 7 corridor ambient</h2><p>Too quiet for the location's threat density. Should have built tension; instead enables player to forget the threat.</p><h2>What works</h2><p>The piano motif that returns throughout. The chapter 5 manor theme. The final boss multi-phase scoring. Father Riley confessional scene scoring.</p><h2>My take</h2><p>Takano's RE9 score is the franchise's strongest in 8 years. The weak tracks don't define the work. The strong tracks raise the bar for RE10.</p>
