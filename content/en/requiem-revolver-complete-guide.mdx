<h1>Requiem Revolver — the franchise tribute weapon, mid-tier reality</h1>
<p>The Requiem Revolver is named after RE9's subtitle. It's unlocked through chapter 5 puzzle completion. The naming suggests significance; the weapon stats are mid-tier. Capcom marketed the prestige; the gameplay didn't match.</p>

<blockquote>Requiem Revolver is marketing-driven design. The weapon stats are mediocre but the name and visual design make players want to use it. Don't fall for the prestige.</blockquote>

<h2>Core stats (v1.02)</h2>
<p>Base damage: 110. Headshot multiplier: 2.6x. Magazine: 6 rounds. Reload speed: 3.5s (very slow). Stagger: high but slower than M232.</p>

<h2>The slow reload problem</h2>
<p>3.5s reload is the slowest of any RE9 handgun. In sustained engagements, this matters. You'll get hit mid-reload regularly.</p>

<h2>Where Requiem Revolver wins</h2>
<p>Single-target precision at medium range. Boss encounter contribution (high stagger threshold). Cosmetic playthrough appeal.</p>

<h2>Where it loses</h2>
<p>Group engagements (slow reload kills you). Boss DPS (Hand Cannon better). Stealth (Silencer 9 better).</p>

<h2>The puzzle unlock</h2>
<p>Requires solving the chapter 5 manor study puzzle in under 2 minutes. Same puzzle that locks Quickdraw Army. Strict speed requirement.</p>

<h2>Insanity considerations</h2>
<p>Requiem Revolver on Insanity is borderline non-viable. The reload window kills you reliably. Skip for Insanity runs.</p>

<h2>Korean community tier</h2>
<p>Korean Naver community ranks Requiem Revolver as B-tier. Some Korean players use it for cosmetic/cinematic playthroughs. Speedrun community ignores it.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Use Requiem Revolver if you want the franchise tribute feel. Don't use it if you want competitive weapon performance. The name doesn't make the stats better.</p>
