<h1>High Roller — accumulate $50,000 in-game currency</h1>
<p>Accumulate $50,000 RE9 currency in a single save. The currency is used at merchants. The threshold can be reached organically in a thorough single playthrough.</p>

<blockquote>High Roller rewards merchant strategy and exploration. Don't grind it. Play normally with attention to currency-yielding actions and the trophy will tick.</blockquote>

<h2>Currency sources in RE9</h2>
<p>Killing enemies: 10-200 currency per kill depending on enemy type. Bosses give 500-2000.</p>
<p>Finding currency directly: most rooms have cash or valuables. Total game currency findable in one playthrough: ~35,000.</p>
<p>Selling unwanted items to merchants: weapon attachments, surplus ammo, decorative items. ~15,000 total sellable.</p>
<p>Side objective completion (e.g., bridges investigator favor): 1,000-5,000 each.</p>

<h2>The 50,000 calculation</h2>
<p>Full enemy kills + full collection + selling everything sellable = ~75,000-90,000 total currency in a thorough single playthrough. The 50,000 threshold is hit naturally around chapter 6 for thorough players.</p>

<h2>What the trophy doesn't require</h2>
<p>You don't need to maintain 50,000 — peak accumulation counts. Spend the currency before reaching 50,000 and the count still tracks peak.</p>
<p>NG+ inheritance: NG+ inherits currency, so if you didn't hit 50,000 on first playthrough, NG+ continuation can finish.</p>

<h2>Insanity currency rates</h2>
<p>Insanity enemies drop ~50% more currency than Standard. Insanity playthrough hits 50,000 around chapter 5 with normal exploration.</p>

<h2>Korean speedrun currency optimization</h2>
<p>Korean speedrun community documented currency-optimal routing. The route prioritizes high-value drops + minimizes spending. Achieves 50,000 by chapter 4 reliably.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>High Roller happens during normal play. Don't think about it. If you reach chapter 7 without the trophy, do one more thorough room sweep and you'll hit the threshold.</p>
