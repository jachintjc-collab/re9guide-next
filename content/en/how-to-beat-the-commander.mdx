<h2>Boss overview: HUNK reborn</h2>

<p>The Commander appears late in the ARK Facility, blocking Leon's path to the Operations Room. Visually, he's a clear callback to HUNK from Resident Evil 2. black tactical gear, gas mask, military bearing. But his fighting style is pure Krauser from RE4: a knife specialist with brutal close-quarters combos.</p>

<p>This is the first fight in RE9 where you <strong>cannot win by spamming the W870 Police shotgun</strong>. The Commander parries gunfire, dodges projectiles, and forces close engagement. You need to master the Hatchet parry system here — and that's exactly the point. Capcom designed this fight as the parry skill check before the final boss.</p>

<h2>Pre-fight preparation</h2>

<p>Required prep:</p>

<ol>
 <li><strong>Upgrade your Hatchet to max Tier 2</strong> at the previous Supply Box. Default Hatchet's parry window is too tight on Standard, almost impossible on Insanity.</li>
 <li><strong>Repair body armor</strong>, The Commander's combos break armor in 2-3 hits.</li>
 <li><strong>Stock 3+ First Aid Sprays</strong>. you will get hit, especially during your first attempts.</li>
 <li><strong>Consider Eye Spy Charm</strong> — random fatal damage save can be the difference between a clean fight and a save reload.</li>
 <li><strong>Save before triggering the cutscene</strong>. there's no checkpoint mid-fight.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Step-by-step strategy</h2>

<h3>// PHASE STRUCTURE</h3>

<p>The Commander has only one phase, but his behavior shifts based on his HP:</p>

<ul>
 <li><strong>100-70% HP</strong>; uses single and double knife slashes, sweep kicks. Aggressive but predictable.</li>
 <li><strong>70-40% HP</strong> — adds the three-hit finisher combo and charge dash.</li>
 <li><strong>40-0% HP</strong>. most aggressive. Frequent grab attempts and rapid combo chains.</li>
</ul>

<h3>// CORE LOOP</h3>

<ol>
 <li><strong>Wait for him to commit to an attack</strong>. never strike preemptively.</li>
 <li><strong>Parry with L1/LB</strong> just before impact (red flash glow on his weapon = parry timing).</li>
 <li><strong>Successful parry stuns him for ~1.5 seconds</strong> — your damage window.</li>
 <li><strong>Hatchet counter</strong> for guaranteed damage, OR<br>
 <strong>Quick switch to Requiem and headshot</strong> for higher damage but more risk.</li>
 <li><strong>Back off after the punish</strong>. don't try a second attack, he recovers fast.</li>
</ol>

<div>
 💡 <strong>The grab attempt is the most dangerous move:</strong> When The Commander lunges with his off-hand, he's trying to grab Leon. <strong>Do NOT try to parry the grab</strong>, you can't. Just sidestep or roll backward. Successful grab = severe damage + possible execute on Insanity.
</div>

<h2>Common mistakes to avoid</h2>

<ul>
 <li><strong>Mistake #1:</strong> Trying to keep distance with the W870 Police shotgun. The Commander closes gaps faster than you can reload.</li>
 <li><strong>Mistake #2:</strong> Spamming parry button. The window is generous but spam-parry uses stamina and locks you in animation.</li>
 <li><strong>Mistake #3:</strong> Trying to parry the grab attempt. It cannot be parried.</li>
 <li><strong>Mistake #4:</strong> Standing in corners. You need 360° dodge space.</li>
 <li><strong>Mistake #5:</strong> Saving Requiem rounds. This is one of the few fights where Requiem is great — use them after parry-stuns.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Insanity difficulty tips</h2>

<div>
 ⚠ On Insanity, The Commander deals roughly 3x damage. A successful 3-hit combo will kill Leon outright. Mortal Edge is non-negotiable here; but you don't have it yet (you GET it from beating this fight).
</div>

<p>Insanity-specific approach:</p>

<ul>
 <li><strong>Pre-buy the upgraded Hatchet at every Supply Box</strong> leading up to this fight</li>
 <li><strong>Use Infinite Ammo if unlocked</strong>. Infinite Requiem one-shots him after stagger</li>
 <li><strong>The grab one-shots</strong> if your armor is broken. Repair it pre-fight.</li>
 <li><strong>Patience over aggression</strong> — let him initiate every exchange</li>
 <li><strong>Reset to a safe save</strong> if you mess up Phase 2-3 setup. Better to retry than burn through First Aid Sprays.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Reward: The Mortal Edge Hatchet</h2>

<p>Defeating The Commander rewards the <strong>Mortal Edge Hatchet</strong>, the upgraded version of Leon's default Hatchet with these benefits:</p>

<div>
 <div><h4>// PARRY WINDOW</h4><p>Significantly larger parry window, easier to time successfully.</p></div>
 <div><h4>// SWING SPEED</h4><p>Faster swing animation = more counter-attacks per stun window.</p></div>
 <div><h4>// FINISHER DMG</h4><p>Hatchet finisher damage roughly 2x the default.</p></div>
 <div><h4>// VICTOR FIGHT</h4><p>Effectively required for the Victor Gideon fight on any difficulty.</p></div>
</div>

<h2>What comes next</h2>

<p>After defeating The Commander, Leon proceeds deeper into the ARK Facility. Your final encounter is <strong>Dr. Victor Gideon</strong> — the true final boss and the hardest fight in the game.</p>

<p>Before triggering Victor's cutscene, sweep the corridor for the Mr. Raccoon Memoriam (#23 in our locations guide), files, and any remaining ammo pickups. Once you enter Victor's chamber, there is no going back.</p>
