<h1>Silencer 9 pistol — Grace's starter sidearm + when to retire it</h1>
<p>The Silencer 9 is RE9's starter handgun, equipped from the chapter 1 opening. Its quiet profile and reliable stagger make it useful longer than starter weapons usually are. Most players retire it by chapter 4. Some stretch it to chapter 6. Here's where the line actually sits.</p>

<blockquote>The Silencer 9 is one of the most underrated starter handguns in the franchise. The silencer mechanic genuinely matters for stealth play. Don't retire it until you have a clear successor.</blockquote>

<h2>Core stats (v1.02)</h2>
<p>Base damage: 35. Headshot multiplier: 2.4x. Magazine: 10 rounds. Reload speed: 1.8s. Stagger threshold: low (single headshot staggers Standard zombies).</p>

<h2>Why the silencer matters</h2>
<p>Suppressed shots don't trigger nearby enemy alerts. In chapters 1-4, this lets you stealth-kill isolated zombies without alerting groups. By chapter 5 onward, enemy density makes suppressed shooting less situational.</p>

<h2>When to retire it</h2>
<p>If you have S&W M1911 + fully upgraded W870 Police, the Silencer 9 is redundant by chapter 5. If you haven't upgraded primary firearms, keep the Silencer 9 to chapter 6.</p>

<h2>Insanity considerations</h2>
<p>On Insanity, the Silencer 9 underperforms past chapter 3. Damage-per-round vs ammo-economy math falls behind heavier handguns. Replace earlier on Insanity.</p>

<h2>Korean community tier</h2>
<p>Korean speedrun community ranks the Silencer 9 as A-tier early game, C-tier mid game. Naver discussion threads on RE9 starter weapons consistently mention the silencer mechanic as the weapon's distinguishing feature.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Keep the Silencer 9 longer than wiki guides suggest. The stealth utility is real. Don't sell it when you upgrade — store it for stealth-required chapters.</p>
