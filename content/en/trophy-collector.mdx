<h1>Collector — find 75% of all collectibles in one playthrough</h1>
<p>Collector requires 75% of every collectible category in a single save. Mr. Raccoon plushies, Charms, audio logs, documents, safe contents, and weapon attachments all count. The 75% threshold is calibrated to be hard but not completionist-only.</p>

<blockquote>Collector is the trophy that recognizes engaged exploration without requiring perfect completion. The 75% threshold is the right design choice. Don't go for 100% unless you want True Survivor instead.</blockquote>

<h2>The collectible categories</h2>
<p>Mr. Raccoon plushies: 25 total, need 19 (76%).</p>
<p>Charms: 18 total, need 14 (78%).</p>
<p>Audio logs: 47 total, need 36 (77%).</p>
<p>Documents: 89 total, need 67 (75%).</p>
<p>Safe contents: 8 safes, need 6 (75%).</p>
<p>Weapon attachments: 23 attachments, need 18 (78%).</p>

<h2>The 75% threshold strategy</h2>
<p>You don't need to be near completion in any single category. The trophy ticks based on aggregated 75%+ across categories. If you're at 80% in three categories and 70% in three others, you still get the trophy.</p>
<p>This is the smart play approach. Focus on categories that match your gameplay style. If you don't enjoy hunting Mr. Raccoon, get to 76% on that one specifically and don't push further.</p>

<h2>Easiest categories to max</h2>
<p>Safe contents (only 8 items, six are obvious through gameplay). Weapon attachments (most are weapon merchant purchases, just have currency). Documents (most are environmental pickups you'll naturally collect).</p>

<h2>Hardest categories</h2>
<p>Audio logs (require specific corner exploration). Mr. Raccoon (the franchise's classic pain point). Charms (the chapter-locked ones in particular).</p>

<h2>Insanity Collector</h2>
<p>Same threshold applies. Insanity collectible locations are identical. The risk is that Insanity makes exploration more dangerous — easier to miss items if you're focused on combat survival.</p>
<p>Recommended approach: do Standard playthrough for Collector trophy, then NG+ Insanity for True Survivor (which requires 100%).</p>

<h2>Korean speedrun community</h2>
<p>The Korean RE9 community on Naver published a chapter-by-chapter Collector checklist that hits exactly 75% in every category with minimum backtracking. The list is in the Naver Café trophy guide thread.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Collector is RE9's "engaged player" recognition trophy. If you played thoroughly, you'll get it without trying. If you rushed, you'll need a deliberate second pass. Either way, 75% is the right threshold — completionist trophies should be separate.</p>
