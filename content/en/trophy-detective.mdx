<h1>Detective — read all 89 documents across the game</h1>
<p>Detective requires reading every text document in RE9. 89 documents total, spread across all four areas. Most are notes, letters, journal entries, or research files. Reading them all unlocks the trophy plus several lore reveals.</p>

<blockquote>Detective is the trophy that pays off lore engagement. Don't pursue it for the trophy. Pursue it because the documents are some of RE9's strongest writing.</blockquote>

<h2>Document distribution</h2>
<p>Wrenwood (18 documents): Hotel guest registry, Father Riley sermon notes, three town historical documents, multiple personal letters, the bridge investigator case files.</p>
<p>Rhodes Hill (22 documents): Vivien Davies personal journal, her husband's writings, Care Center patient records, manor history documents, conservatory research notes.</p>
<p>Raccoon City (15 documents): RPD archive files (including the brother case file reveal), Sherry Birkin reconstruction memos, Marvin Branagh case file callbacks.</p>
<p>ARK (34 documents): BSAA archive content, Commander X1 personal journal, Mia Winters cryostorage logs, Project W historical records, Wesker enhancement protocols.</p>

<h2>The five most consequential documents</h2>
<p>"Grace's brother — case 1998-0127" (RPD archive, chapter 6) — the document that confirms the brother's fate and BSAA's cover-up.</p>
<p>"Vivien Davies — personal journal entry October 7" (Rhodes Hill, chapter 5) — Vivien's last written reflection on her research.</p>
<p>"Project W subject reports 1967-1979" (ARK, chapter 7) — the document that establishes Wesker's childhood enhancement program.</p>
<p>"Sherry Birkin — BSAA personnel file" (RPD, chapter 6) — confirms Sherry's continued operational role.</p>
<p>"Commander X1 — integration log" (ARK, chapter 7) — his journal of his integration choice.</p>

<h2>Document tracking</h2>
<p>The game tracks read documents in the pause menu under "Read documents." If a document title appears greyed out, you have it but haven't read it. The trophy specifically requires reading, not just possessing.</p>
<p>This is a common failure mode — players collect documents but don't open them. Check the menu list before assuming you're at 100%.</p>

<h2>Insanity documents</h2>
<p>Document locations are identical across difficulties. But several documents only appear if you've completed specific Insanity-specific events. The Commander X1 journal specifically is only acquirable if you've defeated him through dialogue (chapter 7 specific choice) — destroying him without dialogue locks the document out.</p>

<h2>Korean community lore engagement</h2>
<p>The Korean RE9 lore community on Naver has translated all 89 documents to Korean independently of the official localization. The Korean fan-translated documents are widely circulated. Several Korean translations are considered superior to the official Capcom Korea versions.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>If you're reading this site's content, you already care about RE lore. Pursue Detective seriously. The documents are RE9's best writing and the trophy is incidental to the engagement. Read them on first playthrough.</p>
