<h1>Leon's farewell — chapter 7 ARK departure scene</h1>
<p>Leon S. Kennedy's chapter 7 farewell scene is the franchise's most consequential Leon moment in nearly twenty years. The four-minute scene establishes Leon's narrative position for RE10 and beyond. Players who grew up with Leon will find it emotional.</p>

<blockquote>Leon's farewell is the franchise saying goodbye to Leon as primary protagonist. Not as a character — he'll return. But as the protagonist position. RE10 will have a new primary. This scene marks the handoff.</blockquote>

<h2>The full transcript</h2>
<div>
<p>[ARK facility helipad. Late afternoon. Leon and Grace stand near a BSAA tactical helicopter. Mia Winters is already aboard, visible through the open side door.]</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "You sure about staying?"</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I'm sure."</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "Eveline's final form is in the next room. You're not going in alone."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I am. This part is mine."</p>
<p>[Beat. Leon recognizes the boundary.]</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "I was twenty-one when I walked into the RPD station. I thought I knew what I was doing."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "What changed?"</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "Everything that came after. Spain. Boulder. Las Plagas. Ada Wong. Every mission I survived because the missions before me taught me something I needed."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I don't have that."</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "You have your brother's story. You have what Vivien told you. You have Father Riley's letter. That's not nothing."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "It's not the same as twenty-three years of operational experience, Leon."</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "No. It's not. But the experience is mine. The brother is yours. The grief is yours. Whatever comes next — whatever you have to do in the next room — that has to be done by someone who carries that grief. I can't carry it for you."</p>
<p>[Pause.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Why are you telling me this?"</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "Because I want you to know I'm not leaving because BSAA called me back. I'm leaving because I trust you to finish this without me. There's a difference."</p>
<p>[Grace processes. Nods.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Thanks, Leon."</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "Grace. One more thing."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Yeah?"</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "When you publish what you find — and I know you will — make sure Mia and I are listed as part of the witness list. Don't protect us from accountability. We were part of the BSAA infrastructure that allowed this. We need to be on the record."</p>
<p>[Grace looks at him.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Are you sure?"</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "I'm sure. And I think Mia agrees."</p>
<p>[Cut to Mia in the helicopter. She nods toward Leon and Grace.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Okay. I'll list you both."</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "Go finish this."</p>
<p>[Leon boards the helicopter. The aircraft lifts off. Grace watches it depart, then turns toward the ARK core entrance.]</p>
</div>

<h2>What the scene establishes</h2>
<p>Leon is letting Grace handle the climactic moment alone. He's not being called away — he's actively choosing to step back. Mia agrees with the choice to be on the public accountability list. The handoff from Leon as RE protagonist to Grace as RE9 protagonist is explicit and intentional.</p>
<p>The "your brother is yours" line is the franchise's most explicit acknowledgement that Grace has a personal story that belongs to her, not to Leon as the longtime franchise face. RE9 commits to Grace as its protagonist.</p>

<h2>What the scene establishes about RE10</h2>
<p>Leon will not be the primary protagonist of the next game. He's choosing the step-back position. The new BSAA field commander RE10 will introduce is the actual handoff target, not Grace (who carries RE9 specifically).</p>
<p>This is generational handoff in franchise terms. Leon has been the primary RE protagonist across RE2, RE4, RE6, and various extended content. Stepping back is significant.</p>

<h2>The accountability list framing</h2>
<p>Leon's request to be on the accountability list is the scene's most ethically loaded moment. He's not asking for protection. He's asking to be named as part of BSAA infrastructure that enabled the Care Center research. This is moral consistency with Grace's investigation.</p>
<p>The franchise has not previously presented Leon as institutional figure carrying institutional accountability. The framing shift is significant for the character.</p>

<h2>Voice work</h2>
<p>Nick Apostolides (Leon) and Cissy Jones (Grace) play the scene with the warmth of established working relationship. Their characters have known each other professionally for years (implied background), and the scene's intimacy comes from that history.</p>
<p>The "your brother is yours" line is Apostolides's vocal performance peak in RE9. The line lands quietly but carries the scene's emotional weight.</p>

<h2>Korean dub notes</h2>
<p>Leon's Korean VA, 정승욱, handled the farewell scene as the chapter 7 emotional peak. Korean Naver discussion identified this as the scene that defined Korean RE9 reception — Korean RE fans grew up with Leon since RE2 Korean release in 1998. The handoff scene hit Korean fans harder than Western reception per discussion volume.</p>
<p>The Korean rendering of "I trust you to finish this without me" — "내가 없어도 이걸 끝낼 수 있다고 믿어" — became one of the most-quoted Leon lines in Korean RE community.</p>

<h2>Production notes</h2>
<p>This scene was filmed with both Apostolides and Jones present for motion capture. The conversational naturalness comes from real-time interaction rather than separately recorded performances. Nakanishi specifically required co-presence for this scene.</p>
<p>The blocking with Mia visible in the background of the helicopter was deliberate — Mia's nod when Leon mentions her name is unscripted but kept in the final cut. Aimee-Ffion Edwards's improvisation worked for the moment.</p>

<h2>What this sets up for RE10</h2>
<p>Leon's role in RE10 will likely be supporting, not lead. Mia's role will be more central than Leon's. The new BSAA field commander will be the primary protagonist. Sherry Birkin (per true ending) will be ally.</p>
<p>Korean fan theory consensus on Naver expects Leon to appear in RE10 as senior advisor or in two-three key scenes, not as playable character.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Leon's farewell is the franchise saying goodbye to its longest-tenured protagonist. RE10 will be the first mainline RE without Leon as primary protagonist since RE6. The scene earns the handoff. If you've followed Leon since 1998, watch this scene carefully. The franchise is acknowledging your decades with the character.</p>
