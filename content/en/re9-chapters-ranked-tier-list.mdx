<h2 id="tldr">TL;DR, must-play chapters</h2>
<ul>
 <li><strong>Best 2 chapters:</strong> ARK Facility (#1) + RPD/Orphanage (#2). The climax + the RE2 callback.</li>
 <li><strong>Best 4 chapters:</strong> Add Care Center (#3) and Raccoon City Square (#4). Story setup + Leon's urban exploration.</li>
 <li><strong>If you skip any:</strong> Wrenwood Hotel (#8) feels long for a tutorial. Courtyard (#4) can be sped through.</li>
 <li><strong>Most underrated:</strong> Care Center Basement (#5). the Organless Corpse puzzle deserves more praise.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="rank1">#1: Chapter 8: ARK Facility (S-tier)</h2>
<p><strong>Why #1:</strong> The franchise's most ambitious finale chapter in 20 years. Three boss fights back-to-back: Plant 43 → The Commander → Chunk. Multiple story reveals (Vivien research, Eveline foreshadowing, Mia's arrival). Connects both Grace and Leon's campaigns. The Final Choice QTE that determines your ending. 4-6 hours of content packed into one chapter.</p>

<p><strong>Standout moment:</strong> The Sterilization Chamber sequence — atmospheric horror peak. Grace's emotional breakdown is one of the strongest character beats in modern RE.</p>

<p><strong>Replay value:</strong> ★★★★★ Different ending paths require different choices. NG+ Insanity unlocks <a href="/chunk-origin-mystery-explained/">Chunk's full backstory</a> codex.</p>

<p>See our <a href="/ark-facility-complete-walkthrough/">complete ARK Facility walkthrough</a>.</p>

<h2 id="rank2">#2: Chapter 6: RPD &amp; Orphanage (S-tier)</h2>
<p><strong>Why #2:</strong> The RE2 callback chapter. Leon enters the Raccoon Police Department, same RPD as RE2 Remake (but 28 years older). Mr. X transformation into Super Tyrant. Orphanage section adds tonal variety (psychological horror). Every long-time RE fan will scream at the architecture.</p>

<p><strong>Standout moment:</strong> Walking into the RPD main hall. Capcom designed it to be identical to RE2 Remake but aged 28 years. Veterans cry.</p>

<p><strong>Replay value:</strong> ★★★★★ <a href="/how-to-beat-mr-x-super-tyrant/">Mr. X / Super Tyrant fight</a> changes drastically on Insanity.</p>

<p>See our <a href="/rpd-orphanage-walkthrough/">RPD &amp; Orphanage walkthrough</a>.</p>

<h2 id="rank3">#3: Chapter 2: Rhodes Hill Care Center (A-tier)</h2>
<p><strong>Why #3:</strong> Grace's primary exploration chapter. Major story setup. finding Vivien Ashcroft's research papers (the Eveline-Vivien theory starts here). Multiple hidden files. The atmosphere is thick — abandoned medical equipment, mold growing through walls. First mini-boss (Blister Borne). Just slightly below S-tier because pacing is exploration-heavy without major combat peaks.</p>

<p><strong>Standout moment:</strong> The records office discovery. finding <a href="/re9-hidden-easter-eggs-list/">Vivien's 2009 research notes</a> is the franchise's most lore-significant Easter egg.</p>

<p><strong>Replay value:</strong> ★★★★ Long chapter rewards re-exploration. NG+ unlocks hidden filing cabinet content.</p>

<p>See our <a href="/care-center-walkthrough/">Care Center walkthrough</a>.</p>

<h2 id="rank4">#4: Chapter 7: Raccoon City Square (A-tier)</h2>
<p><strong>Why #4:</strong> Leon's urban chapter. Open exploration of post-incident Raccoon City. Titan Spinner boss fight in elevated urban environment. Strong atmospheric continuity with chapter 6's RPD nostalgia. Slightly below #3 because story content is lighter. it's more action-focused.</p>

<p><strong>Standout moment:</strong> Vertical Titan Spinner phase — the city becomes the arena.</p>

<p><strong>Replay value:</strong> ★★★★ Insanity's Titan Spinner Phase 4 makes replay essential.</p>

<p>See our <a href="/raccoon-city-walkthrough/">Raccoon City walkthrough</a>.</p>

<h2 id="rank5">#5: Chapter 3: Care Center Basement (B-tier)</h2>
<p><strong>Why #5:</strong> Direct continuation of Chapter 2. Features the Organless Corpse puzzle (one of RE9's best mechanical puzzles). Anatomy lab atmosphere is genuinely creepy. Chunk's first proper encounter happens here. Drops from A-tier because it's essentially a continuation chapter without its own identity.</p>

<p><strong>Standout moment:</strong> The Organless Corpse puzzle reveal. finding the artificial heart and lungs feels rewarding.</p>

<p><strong>Replay value:</strong> ★★★ Puzzle solved is puzzle solved.</p>

<p>See our <a href="/care-center-basement-walkthrough/">Care Center Basement walkthrough</a> + <a href="/organless-corpse-puzzle/">puzzle solution</a>.</p>

<h2 id="rank6">#6: Chapter 5: Water Treatment Plant (B-tier)</h2>
<p><strong>Why #6:</strong> Grace's industrial chapter. The Girl boss fight is divisive (some love the concept, many find it tedious). Strong industrial atmosphere. The chapter is shorter than others and feels more like a corridor than open exploration. Drops to B-tier due to The Girl's mixed reception.</p>

<p><strong>Standout moment:</strong> Initial stealth approach to The Girl, the build-up is excellent before the fight itself becomes frustrating.</p>

<p><strong>Replay value:</strong> ★★★ The Girl fight is the limiting factor for many players.</p>

<p>See our <a href="/water-treatment-walkthrough/">Water Treatment walkthrough</a>.</p>

<h2 id="rank7">#7: Chapter 4: Courtyard (C-tier)</h2>
<p><strong>Why #7:</strong> Short outdoor stealth chapter. Atmospheric but undercooked. Mostly avoiding patrols and traversing exteriors. Lacks meaningful combat or story beats. Functions as a "breather" between Chapter 3 and Chapter 5 but feels skippable.</p>

<p><strong>Standout moment:</strong> First aerial drone encounter — interesting concept that goes underdeveloped.</p>

<p><strong>Replay value:</strong> ★★ Fast to speedrun, low replay incentive.</p>

<p>See our <a href="/courtyard-walkthrough/">Courtyard walkthrough</a>.</p>

<h2 id="rank8">#8: Chapter 1: Wrenwood Hotel (C-tier)</h2>
<p><strong>Why #8:</strong> The opening tutorial. Designed to teach mechanics and establish atmosphere. Drags slightly in the middle. Chunk's first appearance is here, which is good, but the chapter's pacing makes it feel like Capcom is dragging out player onboarding. Many speedrunners cite this as the chapter they wish they could skip.</p>

<p><strong>Standout moment:</strong> The basement boiler discovery; Chunk's introduction is genuinely unsettling.</p>

<p><strong>Replay value:</strong> ★★ NG+ players skip-walk through.</p>

<p>See our <a href="/wrenwood-hotel-walkthrough/">Wrenwood Hotel walkthrough</a>.</p>

<h2 id="first-playthrough">Optimal first-playthrough order</h2>
<p>RE9's chapters are mostly linear, but the campaign-split structure (Grace + Leon) gives you choice. Recommended first-time order:</p>

<ol>
 <li><strong>Grace campaign first (Chapters 1-5 then 8a):</strong> Grace's arc is more emotional and her chapters set up the Vivien/Eveline mystery. Playing Grace first makes Leon's payoff hit harder.</li>
 <li><strong>Leon campaign second (Chapters 6-8b):</strong> Leon's arc resolves what Grace started. Playing Leon second lets RPD/Orphanage feel like a callback rather than just a setting.</li>
 <li><strong>Chapter 8 split shared content:</strong> Both campaigns experience Chapter 8 (ARK Facility) but with character-specific bosses and ending paths.</li>
</ol>

<p>If you play Leon first, you'll miss some emotional resonance. Capcom's recommended order (per Famitsu) is Grace → Leon.</p>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ — chapter ranking</h2>

<p><strong>Q: This ranking has Wrenwood Hotel last. Isn't Chapter 1 important for first-time players?</strong><br>
A: Important for onboarding, yes. But "important" ≠ "good." Chapter 1 spends too long teaching mechanics. Newer RE games (RE4 Remake, RE8) handle tutorial chapters more efficiently.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Where does the Insanity chapter walkthrough rank?</strong><br>
A: <a href="/insanity-chapter-walkthrough/">Insanity walkthrough</a> is technically the same chapters but with brutal difficulty. The chapters don't change structure. only enemy scaling. Same tier rankings apply.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Are there hidden chapters or post-game content?</strong><br>
A: NG+ doesn't add new chapters but unlocks: Chunk encrypted file (Grace), Ada beacon (Leon), Eveline reference photo (Insanity-only). See <a href="/re9-new-game-plus-complete-guide/">NG+ guide</a>.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Can I skip chapters?</strong><br>
A: No. story chapters are mandatory. However, Casual difficulty + speedrun route can compress Chapter 1 and Chapter 4 significantly.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Is the chapter order canonical?</strong><br>
A: Yes. Chapter 1 (Wrenwood) is January 2026. Chapter 8 (ARK) is February 2026 in-universe. The campaign is roughly 6 weeks of game time.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Will RE10 follow a similar structure?</strong><br>
A: Director Nakanishi confirmed dual-protagonist campaigns may continue. Chapter count and structure aren't announced yet but RE10 will likely follow RE9's split-narrative model.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Are the rankings different for completionists vs casual players?</strong><br>
A: Slightly. Completionists value Chapter 2 (Care Center) and Chapter 3 (Basement) more highly because of <a href="/all-75-files-locations-guide/">missable collectibles</a>. Casual players will rank Chapter 8 #1 across the board.</p>
