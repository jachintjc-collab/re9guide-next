<h1>BSAA quarantine protocols — what RE9 reveals about real-world containment</h1>
<p>Quarantine is the unglamorous core of bioterror response. RE9's chapter 3 Care Center containment scenes show specific quarantine protocols that mostly match real-world WHO guidelines. The failures shown are real failure modes.</p>

<blockquote>RE9 is the first RE game where the quarantine failures feel realistic instead of villain-plot-convenient. That's a major writing improvement.</blockquote>

<h2>What RE9 shows about quarantine setup</h2>
<p>The Care Center quarantine deployment in chapter 3 follows real WHO outbreak response protocol structure: outer cordon (mobile barriers + access checkpoints), inner cordon (PPE-equipped staff), patient triage (isolation by symptom severity), and laboratory containment (biosafety levels appropriate to suspected pathogen).</p>
<p>This isn't generic action-game quarantine. The specific elements match WHO Outbreak Investigation Field Guide structure. Capcom's research team did real homework.</p>

<h2>Where the quarantine fails — and why it's realistic</h2>
<p>The Care Center quarantine collapses for three reasons, each of which is a real-world documented failure mode.</p>
<p>First, the pathogen identification took too long. Real outbreak responses fail when the pathogen is novel or unexpected — the response is built around the wrong assumptions for the first critical hours.</p>
<p>Second, ventilation system contamination wasn't anticipated. Real hospital ventilation systems are weak points for aerosol pathogen control. WHO guidelines specifically warn about this; many real outbreaks succeed because the response team underestimates ventilation spread.</p>
<p>Third, secondary infection of response staff occurred before adequate PPE was deployed. This is the historical failure mode that killed many of the early Ebola response workers in real outbreaks.</p>

<h2>What real-world quarantine looks like</h2>
<p>Real biocontainment quarantine deployments take 6-24 hours to fully set up. RE9's Care Center quarantine is shown setting up in roughly that time window. The pacing is biologically credible.</p>
<p>Real BSL-4 mobile labs (Joint Mobile Emerging Disease Intervention Treatment System, USAMRIID) exist and can deploy globally in 48-72 hours. RE9's BSAA mobile lab capability matches this real-world reference point. The fictional acceleration (BSAA labs deploy faster) is consistent with the "well-funded counter-bioterror agency" framing.</p>

<h2>What RE9 doesn't show — but should</h2>
<p>Community response. Real quarantines have to manage civilian populations outside the cordon. Information control, panic prevention, supply chain maintenance, secondary cordon enforcement. RE9 mostly ignores this dimension.</p>
<p>This is a missed worldbuilding opportunity. Future RE games (RE10 if BSAA failure is the plot) should address community management. The civilian experience of being inside or near a BSAA cordon would be compelling material.</p>

<h2>The political dimension</h2>
<p>Real bioterror response requires political coordination across agencies and jurisdictions. WHO, CDC, national agencies, military, local government. The coordination challenges are often the primary failure mode, not the biology.</p>
<p>RE games treat counter-bioterror as essentially apolitical — the BSAA acts, the politics are background. Real bioterror response is mostly political negotiation with biology added. The franchise doesn't engage with this.</p>

<h2>Korean community angle</h2>
<p>Korean readers have particular sensitivity to outbreak response after the 2015 MERS outbreak (which spread through Korean hospital ventilation systems — exactly the failure mode RE9 shows). Korean discussion of RE9 chapter 3 specifically references the MERS comparison. The realism lands harder in Korean reception than in some other markets.</p>

<h2>Verdict</h2>
<p>RE9's quarantine protocols are the most realistic worldbuilding detail in the game. Pay attention to the chapter 3 containment sequences. They're informed by real-world bioterror response practice in a way most action games don't bother with.</p>
