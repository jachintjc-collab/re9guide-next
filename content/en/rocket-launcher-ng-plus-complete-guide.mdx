<h1>Rocket Launcher — NG+ unlock, the franchise's biggest one-shot tool</h1>
<p>Rocket Launcher unlocks at NG+ for any difficulty. Five rockets total per playthrough. Each rocket essentially one-shots any boss. The franchise's most over-powered weapon, used sparingly by design.</p>

<blockquote>Rocket Launcher is RE's traditional NG+ reward. Use it for emergencies or comedy. Don't use it for completionist runs that require challenge.</blockquote>

<h2>Core stats (v1.02)</h2>
<p>Damage: 800 base + splash damage. Magazine: 1 round. Reload: 4.0s (slow but mostly irrelevant). Ammo: 5 total rockets per playthrough.</p>

<h2>What 800 damage means</h2>
<p>800 damage one-shots Standard zombies (50 HP), Crimson Heads (200 HP), most Cerberus (150 HP), and Plant 43 variants (350 HP). Bosses take 3-5 rockets for kill.</p>

<h2>Best Rocket Launcher scenarios</h2>
<p>Chapter 3 Vivien Plant 43 boss (2 rockets = guaranteed kill). Chapter 8 Eveline final phase (1 rocket per phase = trivial fight). Emergency Mr. X escape (single rocket clears him for 30 seconds).</p>

<h2>The 5-rocket budget</h2>
<p>Plan rocket usage across the entire playthrough. Recommended distribution: 1 chapter 3, 1 chapter 6 emergency, 3 chapter 8 boss. Don't waste on chapter 1-2 trash mobs.</p>

<h2>Insanity Rocket Launcher</h2>
<p>Rocket Launcher trivializes Insanity bosses. Speedrun community uses Rocket Launcher for time optimization. Casual NG+ players use it for difficulty relief.</p>

<h2>Korean community usage</h2>
<p>Korean speedrun community has documented optimal Rocket Launcher distribution for Speed Demon and Speedrunner trophy pursuit. Naver discussion treats the 5-rocket budget as the central NG+ resource management challenge.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Rocket Launcher exists to break difficulty. Use it intentionally. Don't pretend it doesn't exist when pursuing challenge trophies — use it strategically as part of the run plan.</p>
