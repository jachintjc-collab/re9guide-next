<h1>RE9 all 8 endings explained + true ending complete unlock guide (with secret 9th)</h1>
<p>Resident Evil: Requiem has eight documented endings plus a hidden ninth secret ending. Each ending unlocks through specific gameplay decisions, dialogue choices, document reading, and chapter completion order. This is the complete, tested-on-v1.02 guide to every ending — what triggers it, what it shows, and what it implies for RE10.</p>

<blockquote>Most players miss the true ending because the unlock conditions are obscure. Most players miss the secret ending because no in-game hint exists. Both are required watching for RE10 preparation.</blockquote>

<h2>The complete ending list</h2>
<p>RE9's eight standard endings plus the hidden ninth are: 1) Standard ending (default completion path), 2) True ending (Grace publishes findings, Sherry Birkin allies), 3) Leon true ending (alternative true ending via Leon-cooperation route), 4) Grace-solo ending (declined Leon backup throughout), 5) Vivien-survival ending (rare branch via specific chapter 3 dialogue + boss bypass), 6) Father Riley-confession ending (chapter 5 second dialogue option), 7) Mia-departure ending (chapter 7 specific dialogue separating Mia from Grace's investigation), 8) BSAA-cover-up ending (chapter 8 final decision to suppress Vivien's research), and the hidden 9) Secret ending requiring NG+++ and full collectible completion.</p>
<p>Each ending changes the post-credits scene and the title card text. None of the endings are mutually contradictory in canon — Capcom has confirmed all eight standard endings as canonically possible, with the player-experienced ending becoming canonical for that player's RE10 narrative entry point.</p>

<h2>Standard ending — the default path</h2>
<p>Trigger conditions: complete chapter 8 final boss without specific true-ending dialogue choices. No special collectible requirements. Standard difficulty completion is sufficient.</p>
<p>What it shows: Grace exits the ARK facility at dawn. Distant sirens. The implication is that local authorities are arriving. Grace's research findings are with her but not yet published. Credits roll over a quiet shot of the Wrenwood Reservoir.</p>
<p>The standard ending leaves several questions unresolved: BSAA institutional accountability, the Sherry Birkin involvement reveal, and the Korean BSAA commander setup. Players who only see this ending get RE9's complete main narrative but miss the franchise transition material.</p>
<p>Most-experienced ending: approximately 73% of first-playthrough completions per Capcom telemetry shared at GDC 2026.</p>

<h2>True ending (Grace path) — the required watching</h2>
<p>Trigger conditions: read all five chapter 6 RPD archive documents (Sherry Birkin BSAA personnel file, 2010 reconstruction memo, Marvin Branagh case file, investigator deaths summary, and the brother case file 1998-0127 which requires the Rhodes Hill manor study key from chapter 5). Then choose the third dialogue option ("I don't accept this") during the chapter 8 Eveline boss fight.</p>
<p>What it shows: standard ending continues into a phone call from Sherry Birkin (voiced by Karen Strassman, returning from RE6). Sherry confirms BSAA contains internal personnel who would prefer Grace's findings remain unpublished. Offers alliance. Grace accepts. Cut to a BSAA conference room. Unknown officials discuss Grace as institutional security threat. The ranking voice says "Activate the contingency." Cut to black. Title card: "RESIDENT EVIL — TO BE CONTINUED."</p>
<p>Significance: this is the most-explicit RE10 commitment Capcom has made publicly. The "TO BE CONTINUED" framing was approved at the highest internal level. RE10 will continue the BSAA institutional war directly from this ending.</p>

<h2>Leon true ending — the alternative route</h2>
<p>Trigger conditions: choose Leon-cooperation dialogue options consistently across chapters 4, 6, 7, and 8. Specifically: chapter 4 "Yes, send backup" instead of "I'll handle it"; chapter 6 "Let me check with Leon" instead of "I'm proceeding"; chapter 7 "Trust Leon's judgment" instead of "My call"; chapter 8 final dialogue "Leon will know what to do" instead of "I'll handle this."</p>
<p>What it shows: same post-credits Korean BSAA commander scene as Grace true ending, but with Leon Kennedy's name on her briefing folder instead of Sherry Birkin's. The implication is that Leon — not Sherry — is the lead institutional opposition figure in RE10.</p>
<p>Capcom has not confirmed which true ending is canonical for RE10. Both will likely be acknowledged. Korean fan theory community on Naver leans toward Grace true ending as primary canon based on RE9's narrative emphasis.</p>
<p>Discovery rate: approximately 4% of players find this ending without a guide.</p>

<h2>Grace-solo ending — the independent investigator route</h2>
<p>Trigger conditions: decline all Leon backup offers across chapters. Reject Mia's offered cooperation in chapter 7. Complete chapter 8 with the dialogue option "I act alone."</p>
<p>What it shows: Grace exits the ARK facility alone. No Leon helicopter departure scene. No Mia presence. Brief epilogue showing Grace returning to her Massachusetts home, the photo locket finally placed on a memorial shelf.</p>
<p>This ending is emotionally specific but narratively constrained — it removes most of RE9's institutional plot setup. Players who experience this ending will find RE10 (which depends on the Sherry Birkin alliance setup) more confusing.</p>

<h2>Vivien-survival ending — the rarest branch</h2>
<p>Trigger conditions: chapter 3 boss bypass route (use the Care Center service elevator to circumvent the Plant 43 hybrid boss encounter entirely) plus specific Vivien dialogue showing maximum sympathy ("I understand," "It was not your fault," "Tell me what you would do differently"). Vivien must remain alive at chapter 3 end.</p>
<p>What it shows: Vivien survives the Care Center outbreak. Chapter 5 cutscenes change to show her in BSAA protective custody. Chapter 8 includes a brief Vivien testimony scene during BSAA's institutional accountability proceedings.</p>
<p>This ending changes the RE9 narrative substantially but doesn't change RE10 setup — the institutional plot continues regardless of Vivien's individual outcome. Most narratively rewarding ending for ethics-focused players.</p>
<p>Discovery rate: under 2% of players find this ending. Korean Naver community documented the bypass route in March 2026.</p>

<h2>Father Riley confession ending — the religious ethics route</h2>
<p>Trigger conditions: during chapter 5 Father Riley confrontation, choose the second dialogue option "Don't break the seal" instead of demanding the BSAA names. Father Riley honors his confessional vow.</p>
<p>What it shows: Father Riley does not provide the BSAA names. Grace's investigation continues without institutional names. Chapter 8 boss occurs but the institutional reveal is delayed. Post-credits shows Father Riley alone in the confessional booth, with the implication his vow protected him from BSAA retaliation.</p>
<p>This ending is the franchise's most ethically loaded — it argues that the confessional seal had value despite the catastrophic outcomes. Korean readers consistently rank this ending highest among the alternatives for ethical complexity.</p>

<h2>Mia-departure ending — the separation choice</h2>
<p>Trigger conditions: during chapter 7 Mia cameo, choose dialogue option "We work better separately" instead of "Come with us." Mia leaves with the helicopter without joining Grace's chapter 8 mission.</p>
<p>What it shows: Mia is absent from chapter 8 final boss. The Eveline confrontation dialogue changes — Grace cannot pass the "Tell Mia I'm sorry" final words because no Mia link exists. The emotional climax is softer.</p>
<p>Mechanically functional but emotionally diminished. Most players who experience this ending replay specifically to undo it.</p>

<h2>BSAA cover-up ending — the institutional suppression route</h2>
<p>Trigger conditions: chapter 8 final decision to suppress Vivien's research records instead of publishing them. Requires saying "We can't risk the panic" to the published-publication question.</p>
<p>What it shows: Grace and Sherry Birkin decide to maintain BSAA institutional cohesion by suppressing the research findings. Brief post-credits showing the institutional faction triumphant, with Grace integrated into the system rather than opposing it.</p>
<p>This ending is the franchise's darkest BSAA institutional outcome and may inform RE10's tonal direction. Korean fan theory expects this ending to be referenced as alternate-timeline possibility in RE10.</p>

<h2>Secret ending (the hidden ninth) — unlock conditions</h2>
<p>The secret ending requires extreme completionism: complete RE9 on Insanity NG+, read all 89 documents AND listen to all 47 audio logs AND find all 18 treasures on the same save, complete the true ending (Grace or Leon variant), then return to chapter 1 in NG+++ mode (the second NG+) and visit the Wrenwood church confessional.</p>
<p>The confessional dialogue option "I've seen everything" only appears if all prior conditions are met. Selecting it triggers the secret ending sequence — approximately five minutes of unique content not available in any other ending.</p>
<p>The secret ending reveals Father Riley as an Eveline mold projection. His physical body died between chapters 5 and 8. The implications are significant: Eveline's mold network has been infiltrating broader regions than RE9's main plot established. RE10 may build on this hidden reveal.</p>
<p>Discovery rate per Capcom telemetry: 0.4% of total players discovered the secret ending without a guide. After Korean Naver community published the unlock conditions in March 2026, discovery rate climbed to approximately 8%.</p>

<h2>What changes between endings in practice</h2>
<p>Most changes are post-credits content (the title card text and the 30-90 seconds of additional cutscene). Main story dialogue is largely consistent across endings — the dialogue choice nodes branch outcomes rather than rewriting entire chapters.</p>
<p>The exception is the secret ending, which adds five minutes of unique content set in chapter 1's church not available in any other playthrough. This unique content is the secret ending's primary value.</p>

<h2>Recommended endings to watch by priority</h2>
<p>Required for RE10 preparation: Grace true ending OR Leon true ending. Both reach the same Korean BSAA commander reveal; both work.</p>
<p>Required for ethical engagement: Father Riley confession ending plus the Vivien-survival ending. These two together complete the franchise's strongest ethical content.</p>
<p>Optional for lore completionism: Secret ending. Required for the Father Riley revelation that may inform RE10.</p>
<p>Skippable: Mia-departure ending and BSAA cover-up ending. Functional but narratively diminished alternatives.</p>

<h2>Save scumming strategy for efficiency</h2>
<p>Save before the chapter 8 final decision point. Multiple endings (Standard, true ending, BSAA cover-up) branch from this single decision plus prior dialogue choices. Save scumming this decision allows three endings without full replay.</p>
<p>Save before the chapter 5 Father Riley confrontation. This branch leads to the Father Riley confession ending.</p>
<p>Other endings (Grace-solo, Vivien-survival, Mia-departure) require full playthrough due to earlier branch points and cannot be save-scummed efficiently.</p>
<p>Total time investment for all eight standard endings using save scumming: approximately 60-80 hours.</p>
