<h1>Hotel Survivor — complete chapter 1 without checkpoint use</h1>
<p>Complete the entire chapter 1 Wrenwood hotel sequence without using any checkpoint save. Trophy requires uninterrupted run from chapter start to chapter completion.</p>

<blockquote>Hotel Survivor is the franchise's most achievable no-save trophy. Chapter 1 is short. Don't think too hard about it.</blockquote>

<h2>What no-checkpoint means</h2>
<p>The game's automatic checkpoints save your progress at chapter transitions and certain story beats. Hotel Survivor requires completing chapter 1 without any of these checkpoint loads.</p>
<p>If you die at any point, reloading from checkpoint means the trophy is failed for that attempt. Must restart chapter 1 from start.</p>

<h2>Chapter 1 length</h2>
<p>Chapter 1 is the shortest chapter in RE9. 25-40 minutes typical playtime including cutscenes. On NG+ with cutscene skip, 12-18 minutes.</p>
<p>The brevity is what makes Hotel Survivor achievable. The chapter doesn't have time for major mistakes.</p>

<h2>Chapter 1 danger zones</h2>
<p>The opening zombie encounter (basement). Easy to underestimate.</p>
<p>The hotel staircase Cerberus encounter. Mobile target, can flank.</p>
<p>The bridge sequence. Slip mechanic on wet planks.</p>

<h2>Insanity Hotel Survivor</h2>
<p>Insanity makes Hotel Survivor more demanding. The basement zombies on Insanity can two-shot Grace if she's at default HP. Plan ammo + medication carefully.</p>

<h2>Korean community speedrun</h2>
<p>Korean RE9 community has documented optimal chapter 1 routes for no-damage AND no-checkpoint completion. The combined challenge is the basis for a niche Korean speedrun category.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Hotel Survivor on Standard with NG+ knowledge = 1-2 attempts. Insanity Hotel Survivor = more demanding but still manageable. Don't pursue with high anxiety — the chapter is short enough to retry quickly.</p>
