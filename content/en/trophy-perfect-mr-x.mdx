<h1>Perfect Mr. X — escape Mr. X chase without being struck once</h1>
<p>Complete the chapter 6 Mr. X pursuit sequence without taking any hit from Mr. X. Other damage during the chapter doesn't disqualify; only Mr. X-specific contact damage counts.</p>

<blockquote>Perfect Mr. X rewards specific routing mastery. The pursuit isn't actually that hard once you know the route.</blockquote>

<h2>What disqualifies</h2>
<p>Mr. X strikes Grace with any of his attack moves. Direct contact damage from his patrol movements. Mr. X-triggered environmental hazards (none in chapter 6 fortunately).</p>
<p>Cerberus attacks during chapter 6: don't count against Perfect Mr. X. Only the Tyrant unit matters.</p>

<h2>The Perfect Mr. X route</h2>
<p>Archive room exit → western corridor (Mr. X is in main lobby) → main lobby (he's left already if timed correctly) → eastern stairwell. Don't backtrack. Don't enter chief's office (his secondary patrol). Don't take the basement shortcut (Cerberus trigger).</p>
<p>The route takes 3-4 minutes of focused play. Practice attempts add to the time.</p>

<h2>Save management</h2>
<p>Save at the chapter start. Reload immediately on any Mr. X contact. Don't try to recover — the trophy is binary.</p>

<h2>Insanity Perfect Mr. X</h2>
<p>Insanity Mr. X moves faster and patrols more aggressively. Perfect on Insanity is significantly harder than Standard. Standard recommended for first completion.</p>

<h2>Korean perfect routing</h2>
<p>Korean speedrun community has documented Perfect Mr. X with frame-precise route timing. The Korean route includes one specific positioning trick (using the archive room desk as obstacle) not in English-language guides.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Perfect Mr. X is achievable in 5-10 attempts for engaged players. Don't pursue on Insanity unless you've mastered Standard first. The trophy rewards memorization of patrol patterns more than reflex skill.</p>
