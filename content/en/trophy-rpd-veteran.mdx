<h1>RPD Veteran — second-listing (chapter 6 completion variant)</h1>
<p>Note: there are two RPD-related trophies. This one is for completing chapter 6 with all RPD-specific side objectives. Different from the no-damage RPD Veteran (covered separately).</p>

<blockquote>Two RPD Veteran trophies is unusual naming. Capcom should have named them distinctly. Pursue the no-damage version (trophy-guide-rpd-veteran) for the more meaningful achievement.</blockquote>

<h2>What unlocks this RPD Veteran</h2>
<p>Complete chapter 6 with all RPD-specific side objectives. Objectives include: read all RPD archive documents, find all RPD audio logs (8 total), discover the Sherry Birkin office, complete the Mr. X pursuit successfully.</p>
<p>Trophy fires at chapter 6 completion when all side objectives are checked.</p>

<h2>The naming confusion</h2>
<p>"RPD Veteran" appears twice in RE9's trophy list. One is the chapter completionist (this trophy). One is the no-damage chapter completion. Capcom's trophy naming convention failed here.</p>
<p>The internal trophy IDs are distinct but visible names are the same. Players track them separately.</p>

<h2>Side objective tracking</h2>
<p>The pause menu in chapter 6 shows side objective progress. Look for "RPD Side Objectives" section. All 4 items must be checked before chapter end.</p>

<h2>Insanity considerations</h2>
<p>Same side objectives across difficulties. Insanity adds combat danger but doesn't add to objective count.</p>

<h2>Korean community on the naming issue</h2>
<p>Korean Naver community has long called for Capcom to rename one of the RPD Veteran trophies. Korean RE9 trophy guides distinguish them as "RPD Veteran (완수형)" and "RPD Veteran (무피해형)" to avoid confusion.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Pursue this version (completionist) on first chapter 6 playthrough. Pursue the no-damage version separately on NG+ or replay. They're independently achievable but require different play approaches.</p>
