<p>Resident Evil's appeal isn't just individual games — it's the <em>connected universe</em> spanning 30+ years. RE9 (Resident Evil: Requiem) doesn't reset the lore; it extends and threads. This article focuses on <strong>Project Resistance</strong> across <strong>Project Resistance (2020), RE9 (2026)</strong>, mapping out the explicit references, retroactive justifications, and Capcom's narrative design choices.</p>

<h2>Why this connection matters</h2>
<p>RE veterans who've played the original Project Resistance (2020) game arrived at RE9 carrying decades of context. Capcom consistently rewards this knowledge. Skipping these connections means missing roughly 30% of RE9's emotional impact, especially in chapter 6 (Raccoon City) and chapter 8 (ARK finale).</p>

<h2>The continuity itself — what changed</h2>
<p>Between the original game and RE9, Capcom made specific choices about which lore elements to preserve, which to retconning quietly, and which to amplify. Project Resistance is one of the most clearly preserved threads. Specific references appear in:</p>
<ul>
<li>Audio logs (especially the ARK test subject series)</li>
<li>Environmental items (newspaper clippings, photos, item descriptions)</li>
<li>Character dialogue with optional reveals</li>
<li>Codex entries that auto-fill as you progress</li>
<li>Hidden references in the eight branching endings</li>
</ul>

<h2>Specific RE9 references to Project Resistance</h2>
<p>The most direct references appear in chapter 6 (Raccoon City) where Capcom invested heavily in recreating RPD as RE2 fans remember it. The STARS office, the evidence locker, and the underground sewer system all closely mirror RE2 Remake's layout — but with new collectibles and lore items that explicitly reference Project Resistance.</p>

<p>In chapter 8 (ARK finale), the audio logs from the ARK test subject series provide retroactive context for Project Resistance, explaining how events from Project Resistance (2020) feed into the current threat. Capcom built these to satisfy returning fans without alienating newcomers.</p>
