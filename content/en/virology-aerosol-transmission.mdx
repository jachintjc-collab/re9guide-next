<h1>Aerosol transmission — what RE9 gets right and where it cheats</h1>
<p>Aerosol transmission is the scariest pathogen vector in real biosecurity. Capcom uses it across the RE franchise but rarely shows the physics. RE9's Care Center outbreak finally treats aerosol spread with something close to real-world rigor.</p>

<blockquote>RE9 is the first RE game to handle aerosol transmission without embarrassing science. RE6 was the worst offender. The bar was low; RE9 cleared it.</blockquote>

<h2>What RE9 specifies about aerosol mechanics</h2>
<p>Per the Care Center codex, the E-pathogen variants in chapter 3 use modified spore propagation rather than viral aerosol. Mold reproduces by releasing dormant spores that can stay airborne for hours and infect through respiratory inhalation. This is the real biology of fungi — Aspergillus, Histoplasma, and Coccidioides all do this.</p>
<p>The fictional extension: Capcom's mold spores are roughly 10-100 times more infectious per spore than real fungal pathogens. That's how a small outbreak fills a hospital ward in 36 hours. Real Aspergillus infection requires sustained exposure or immunocompromise.</p>

<h2>Why aerosol is the right vector for the story</h2>
<p>Mold-based aerosol explains why the Care Center can't be contained by simple isolation. You can't isolate against airborne spores in a building with shared HVAC. The story uses this — the BSAA's first containment attempt in chapter 3 fails because the ventilation system spreads the spores faster than the quarantine cordon can react.</p>
<p>This is a smart writers' room call. Tactical realism. Real biosecurity professionals would recognize the failure mode immediately.</p>

<h2>Where the science still bends</h2>
<p>The transmission speed is the limit. RE9 implies index-case to symptomatic-outbreak in 6-12 hours. Real fungal pathogens take days to weeks. The fictional handwave: E-pathogen variants have engineered shortcuts in the host cellular machinery. Not real. Acceptable for fiction.</p>

<h2>What RE9 still doesn't address</h2>
<p>Outdoor dispersal. The Care Center is contained because it's a building. What happens if E-pathogen spores get outside? Capcom doesn't run that simulation in canon. My guess is they're saving it for RE10. The "regional outbreak" plot is exactly the kind of escalation the franchise needs for next gen.</p>

<h2>Korean community on this</h2>
<p>Korean RE forums mostly skip aerosol transmission discussion. Most lore engagement is at character and weapon level. Where it does come up: Naver speculation threads about RE10 setting often assume an outdoor aerosol outbreak scenario. That's consistent with where RE9's plot points.</p>

<h2>Verdict</h2>
<p>RE9's aerosol handling is the franchise's best to date. Not real science, but smart fiction that respects real failure modes. Pay attention to the chapter 3 containment scenes — they're more thoughtful than they look.</p>
