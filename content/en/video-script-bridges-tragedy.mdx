<h1>Wrenwood bridges scene — chapter 1 tragedy reveal</h1>
<p>The Wrenwood bridges scene in chapter 1 is RE9's first emotional peak. Grace discovers what happened to the previous BSAA investigators who tried to look into the Care Center. Two minutes of cutscene that recontextualize the early game.</p>

<blockquote>The bridges scene is RE9's most underrated cutscene. Most players speedrun past chapter 1 and miss the emotional setup. The chapter 3 events hit much harder if you watched the bridges scene.</blockquote>

<h2>The full transcript</h2>
<div>
<p>[Grace at the Wrenwood Reservoir overlook. Dusk. The bridges across the reservoir are visible in the distance.]</p>
<p>[She holds a paper folder. Inside: photographs of three investigators (BSAA contractors, names redacted).]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (voice-over):</strong> "Three investigators came here before me. All in the last six months."</p>
<p>[Cut: Grace consulting her phone. Map shows three locations marked on the bridges.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (voice-over):</strong> "All three died on these bridges. Different bridges. Different days. Same... method."</p>
<p>[Cut: investigator photograph (man, mid-40s). Insert: brief news clip — "Local hiking accident."]</p>
<p><strong>NEWS ANCHOR:</strong> "...local resident found at the base of Wrenwood's Iron Bridge yesterday. Authorities have ruled the death a suicide pending..."</p>
<p>[Cut: second photograph (woman, late 30s). Brief insert: "Tragic family loss."]</p>
<p><strong>NEWS ANCHOR:</strong> "...the family is requesting privacy at this difficult time. Authorities have not released a formal..."</p>
<p>[Cut: third photograph (man, early 50s). Brief insert: "Police report contradicts witness statements."]</p>
<p><strong>NEWS ANCHOR:</strong> "...the Wrenwood Police Department has not responded to requests for clarification. The investigation is..."</p>
<p>[Cut: Grace at the overlook again. She folds the folder. Looks at the bridges.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (voice-over):</strong> "Three accidents. Three suicides. Three closed investigations. Same town. Six months."</p>
<p>[Pause. Camera tightens on Grace's face.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (to herself):</strong> "I'm number four."</p>
</div>

<h2>What the scene establishes</h2>
<p>Three previous investigators were killed under suspicious circumstances. Local police covered the deaths as suicides or accidents. Grace knows what she's walking into. The local establishment is complicit or compromised.</p>
<p>This is the scene that tells you Wrenwood isn't just a town with an outbreak. It's a town where the outbreak is being actively covered up at multiple levels. Father Riley's local prominence (established in the opening) takes on new meaning.</p>

<h2>The "I'm number four" line</h2>
<p>The scene's closing line is RE9's most quietly menacing moment in chapter 1. Grace acknowledges her likely fate without flinching. This is character work — she's not naive about the risk, she's choosing to proceed anyway.</p>
<p>Cissy Jones (Grace) delivers the line with controlled flat affect. No drama, no fear. Just acceptance. This is the line that defines Grace's character for the rest of the game.</p>

<h2>The news clip integration</h2>
<p>Three brief news clips from fictional Wrenwood local stations. The clips were recorded specifically for this scene with real local-news-style production (low-budget look, anchor reading from teleprompter, b-roll of unrelated stock footage). The authenticity reads as research-driven.</p>
<p>The clips also encode police misconduct. Each clip references "police not responding" or "investigation pending" — Wrenwood PD is deliberately not closing the cases. The cover-up is sloppy on purpose.</p>

<h2>Voice work</h2>
<p>Cissy Jones (Grace) delivers all the voice-over with restraint. No emotional escalation across the scene. The acceptance reading at the end is the line that lands.</p>
<p>The news anchors are uncredited Capcom internal voice cast. The three voices were specifically directed to sound like distinct local-news personalities — not voice actors performing news anchor parts. The realism is deliberate.</p>

<h2>Korean dub notes</h2>
<p>The Korean dub of this scene was particularly strong. Korean voice direction emphasized the news anchor authenticity — Korean rendering used actual Korean local-news anchor voice style. Korean RE community on Naver specifically commented on the Korean news clips feeling unsettlingly real.</p>
<p>The Korean "I'm number four" line — "네 번째는 나야" — is one of the most-quoted RE9 lines in Korean meme culture. Brief, foreboding, and quotable.</p>

<h2>Production notes</h2>
<p>The fake news clips required actual newsroom set construction at Capcom's Tokyo facility. The three anchors were real Japanese television personalities working under English-language voice direction. The production cost for the three brief clips exceeded the cost of most other RE9 cutscene props.</p>
<p>The bridges shown in the scene are based on real bridges in rural Massachusetts. Capcom location scouts visited and photographed multiple sites for reference.</p>

<h2>What this sets up for later</h2>
<p>The cover-up framework. Throughout RE9, Grace encounters local Wrenwood institutional figures (police, hospital staff, town officials) who are complicit at various levels. The bridges scene tells you to read these figures as obstacles, not allies.</p>
<p>Father Riley is the partial exception — his role is more complicated, as the chapter 5 confrontation reveals. The bridges scene helps establish that even seemingly helpful local figures should be regarded carefully.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>The bridges scene is the chapter 1 cutscene that justifies stopping to watch instead of speedrunning. The "I'm number four" line earns its place in the game's quotable line catalog. If you skipped chapter 1 cutscenes, go back and watch this one specifically. It changes how chapter 3 events read.</p>
