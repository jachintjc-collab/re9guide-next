<h2 id="unlock">How to unlock Insanity difficulty</h2>

<!-- INFOGRAPHIC: Unlock flow -->
<div>
 <div>// UNLOCK STEPS</div>
 <div>
 <div>01</div>
 <div>
 <div>Start a new game on any difficulty</div>
 <div>Casual, Standard (Modern), or Standard (Classic). they all count toward unlocking Insanity. The fastest path is Casual.</div>
 <div>// TIME: ~12-15 hours</div>
 </div>
 </div>
 <div>
 <div>02</div>
 <div>
 <div>Complete the main story</div>
 <div>Beat the final boss (Dr. Victor Gideon) and watch the ending credits roll. You must finish the credits; exiting early will not register the completion.</div>
 <div>// REQUIREMENT: Complete to credits end</div>
 </div>
 </div>
 <div>
 <div>03</div>
 <div>
 <div>Save when prompted</div>
 <div>After credits, the game will prompt you to save your clear data. This save unlocks all post-game content including Insanity mode and the Special Content Store.</div>
 <div>// IMPORTANT: Don't skip this save</div>
 </div>
 </div>
 <div>
 <div>04</div>
 <div>
 <div>Start a new game from the title screen</div>
 <div>Insanity now appears as a difficulty option. Select it and confirm to begin a fresh Insanity playthrough.</div>
 <div>// CARRY-OVER: Some weapons and unlocks transfer</div>
 </div>
 </div>
</div>

<div>
 💡 <strong>Pro tip:</strong> If you only care about unlocking Insanity (not 100% completion), do a Casual speedrun. Skip optional bosses, ignore collectibles, and focus on finishing the main path. Most players can clear Casual in 10-12 hours by ignoring side content.
</div>

<h2 id="whatchanges">What changes on Insanity</h2>

<p>Insanity is not just "Standard with more damage." It restructures how you approach the game. Major changes:</p>

<h3>// COMBAT CHANGES</h3>
<ul>
 <li><strong>Enemy damage 3x</strong> — most basic zombie attacks deal 50-70% HP damage</li>
 <li><strong>Enemy HP scaled up</strong>, even standard zombies take 2-3 extra shots</li>
 <li><strong>Boss HP scales 2-2.5x</strong>; Victor Gideon takes nearly twice as long to kill</li>
 <li><strong>Reduced parry windows</strong> — about 15% tighter; default Hatchet is barely usable</li>
 <li><strong>Boss attack patterns add variants</strong>; some bosses (Mr. X, Victor) have new combos</li>
</ul>

<h3>// RESOURCE CHANGES</h3>
<ul>
 <li><strong>Healing items rarer</strong>. First Aid Spray pickups reduced ~40%</li>
 <li><strong>Ammo drops reduced</strong> — most enemy drops are halved</li>
 <li><strong>Crafting materials scarcer</strong>. Scrap and Infected Blood drops less frequent</li>
 <li><strong>Save points unchanged</strong>; typewriters still in same locations</li>
</ul>

<h3>// CONTENT CHANGES</h3>
<ul>
 <li><strong>Safe codes are different</strong> — see our Safe Codes guide for the Insanity set</li>
 <li><strong>Some optional encounters spawn additional enemies</strong></li>
 <li><strong>Insanity-exclusive item placements</strong>. a few new pickups in late-game areas</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="strategy">Insanity strategy guide</h2>

<h3>// PRE-RUN PREP</h3>
<ol>
 <li><strong>Buy Infinite Ammo unlocks</strong> at the Special Content Store before starting Insanity</li>
 <li><strong>Have Mortal Edge Hatchet from previous run</strong>, carries over via NG+ option</li>
 <li><strong>Memorize Insanity safe codes</strong> — they're different from Standard</li>
 <li><strong>Plan a route</strong>; Insanity rewards efficiency, not exploration</li>
</ol>

<h3>// CORE INSANITY RULES</h3>
<ul>
 <li><strong>Avoid combat when possible</strong>. running past enemies saves resources</li>
 <li><strong>Mortal Edge parry → shoot, never melee twice</strong> — stamina runs out fast</li>
 <li><strong>Save before EVERY boss</strong>; multi-attempt fights are normal on Insanity</li>
 <li><strong>Conserve Requiem rounds religiously</strong>, only use them on bosses</li>
 <li><strong>Eye Spy Charm is non-negotiable</strong> — kill Chunk early as Grace</li>
</ul>

<div>
 ⚠ <strong>The 3 deadliest moments on Insanity:</strong><br>
 <strong>1. Mr. X's thrown car</strong>. one-shots if armor is broken<br>
 <strong>2. The Commander's grab attempt</strong>; combo can kill in one chain<br>
 <strong>3. Victor Gideon's purple energy blast</strong> — stand in it for half a second = death
</div>

<h2 id="infammo">Infinite Ammo trick (no trophy void)</h2>

<p>Here's the most important thing to know about Insanity:</p>

<div>
 ⚡ <strong>Infinite Ammo cheats DO NOT void any trophies.</strong> You can complete Insanity with Infinite Requiem and still earn the "Remarkable Agent" Gold trophy and the Platinum. This is confirmed for both PS5 and Steam versions.
</div>

<h3>// HOW TO UNLOCK INFINITE AMMO</h3>

<ol>
 <li><strong>Complete the main story once</strong> (any difficulty including Casual)</li>
 <li><strong>Earn Challenge Points (CP)</strong> from killing enemies, finding collectibles, and completing achievements</li>
 <li><strong>Visit the Special Content Store</strong> from the main menu</li>
 <li><strong>Purchase Infinite Ammo for desired weapons:</strong>
 <ul>
 <li>Infinite Requiem. ~6,000 CP</li>
 <li>Infinite RPG-7. ~8,000 CP</li>
 <li>Infinite Clatter Carbine — ~5,000 CP</li>
 </ul>
 </li>
 <li><strong>Equip from inventory</strong> at the start of your Insanity run</li>
</ol>

<h3>// RECOMMENDED INSANITY LOADOUT</h3>

<ul>
 <li><strong>Primary:</strong> Infinite Requiem (one-shots most enemies)</li>
 <li><strong>Secondary:</strong> Infinite RPG-7 (emergency boss damage)</li>
 <li><strong>Melee:</strong> Mortal Edge Hatchet (parries)</li>
 <li><strong>Charm:</strong> Eye Spy Charm (random fatal damage save)</li>
</ul>

<p>With this loadout, an experienced player can clear Insanity in roughly <strong>3 hours 40 minutes</strong>, easily qualifying for the "Speedrunner" trophy as a bonus.</p>

<h2>Common questions</h2>

<h3>// CAN I SKIP TO INSANITY WITHOUT FINISHING THE STORY?</h3>
<p>No. Insanity is locked behind a single completion of the main story. There are no console commands or save edits that can bypass this on console; on PC, modding voids your save data integrity.</p>

<h3>// DO I HAVE TO PLAY THROUGH STORY EVENTS AGAIN?</h3>
<p>Yes. Insanity is a full new game. Cutscenes can be skipped (button prompt) but the entire main path must be completed.</p>

<h3>// IS INSANITY HARDER THAN OTHER RE GAMES?</h3>
<p>Roughly comparable to Resident Evil 4 Remake's "Professional" difficulty. Slightly harder than RE Village's "Village of Shadows" due to the parry-focused combat and Victor Gideon's two-phase fight. Use of Infinite Ammo brings it back to manageable.</p>

<h3>// WILL DLC ADD A HARDER DIFFICULTY?</h3>
<p>Capcom has not announced anything beyond Insanity. Historically, RE titles have added new difficulty modes via free patches (Resident Evil 4 Remake's "Mercenaries Mode" added new challenges). Watch for community news.</p>
