<h1>Future RE virus prediction — what RE10 will probably feature</h1>
<p>RE9's background lore deliberately sets up several biological loose ends for the next game. Reading the codex carefully tells you roughly what RE10's biology focus will be. Here's what I think Capcom is preparing.</p>

<blockquote>RE10 will feature a regional mold-derivative outbreak with BSAA institutional failure as the inciting event. I'd bet money on this.</blockquote>

<h2>The setup signals in RE9</h2>
<p>RE9's codex repeatedly references three loose ends: BSAA Geneva pathogen inventory (introduced in chapter 6, not paid off), Eveline molecular consciousness preservation (introduced in chapter 7, partially paid off in final boss), and Master Plagas integration protocols (introduced through Commander X1, not extended to broader narrative).</p>
<p>One or more of these is the foundation for RE10's biology arc. The signals point to BSAA institutional failure most strongly.</p>

<h2>Why BSAA Geneva failure is the most likely RE10 plot</h2>
<p>RE9 spends substantial codex space establishing BSAA's centralized pathogen archive at Geneva. The detail is excessive if it's just flavor — it's clearly setup. A containment failure at BSAA Geneva would simultaneously:</p>
<p>Justify a regional outbreak with multiple pathogen types (BSAA holds samples of every recovered RE pathogen). Provide moral complexity (BSAA is supposed to be the good guys). Create direct conflict for protagonist (BSAA insider threat or external attack on BSAA facility).</p>
<p>These are the elements RE10 needs to advance the franchise narrative. The setup work is done.</p>

<h2>Alternative scenario — Eveline successor</h2>
<p>The Eveline molecular consciousness preservation thread is RE9's most narratively-charged loose end. RE10 could feature a successor Eveline — same consciousness, new substrate, new geography. The Korean fan theory community on Naver leans this direction.</p>
<p>My objection: this would feel like a retread. RE9 already used the "Eveline returns" plot. Going to that well again would diminish both games. RE10 should build a new biological antagonist, not reuse one.</p>

<h2>Alternative scenario — Master Plagas integration goes wrong</h2>
<p>Commander X1's successful integration in RE9 implies an integration program exists. RE10 could feature mass integration attempts producing varied outcomes — some successful (more Commander X1 figures), most failed (new B.O.W. variants).</p>
<p>This would extend RE9's strongest creature design rather than abandon it. I see this as the second-most-likely direction.</p>

<h2>What the franchise should NOT do</h2>
<p>New pathogen invention. RE9 spent significant codex space establishing the mold + Plagas framework as the franchise's biological foundation. Introducing a brand-new pathogen would waste that setup work. The franchise needs continuity at this point, not novelty.</p>
<p>The exception: a real-world inspired pathogen with no in-canon precedent (something modeled on prion diseases, or a CRISPR-engineered organism with no existing RE parallel). That would be earned novelty. Pure invention without grounding would not.</p>

<h2>Timeline prediction</h2>
<p>RE10 will probably arrive 2029-2030 based on Capcom's standard 4-year mainline cycle. That gives Capcom 3-4 years to develop whatever direction they're committing to. The early development decisions are happening now.</p>
<p>My bet stands: BSAA Geneva failure + new protagonist + extension of mold/Plagas framework rather than new pathogen. Probability range: 50-65%.</p>

<h2>Korean community predictions</h2>
<p>Korean fan theory posts on Naver and 디씨 RE 갤러리 are split between Eveline successor (most popular) and BSAA failure (second most popular). Less attention to Master Plagas extension. The Korean readings emphasize emotional continuity (Eveline) over institutional failure (BSAA).</p>

<h2>Verdict</h2>
<p>RE10 is being shaped right now by which RE9 setup threads Capcom commits to. Read the BSAA Geneva and Eveline preservation codex entries carefully. They're the future of the franchise.</p>
