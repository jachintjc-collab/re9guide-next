<h1>RE9 true ending — full transcript + what it means for RE10</h1>
<p>RE9's true ending unlocks after specific chapter 6 and chapter 8 conditions. It adds about three minutes of additional content past the standard ending. The true ending changes the franchise's trajectory more significantly than the standard ending implies.</p>

<blockquote>The true ending is required watching for anyone planning to play RE10. Most players miss it because the unlock conditions aren't obvious. Replay if you didn't see it.</blockquote>

<h2>The true ending unlock conditions</h2>
<p>Two requirements: read all chapter 6 archive documents (specifically the Sherry Birkin involvement files), and decline Eveline's final dialogue option in chapter 8 (the third dialogue choice, "I don't accept this," rather than the default "I had to" or the alternative "I'm sorry too").</p>
<p>Most players miss the true ending because the chapter 6 archives are easy to skip and the third dialogue option requires specific positioning. The Korean speedrun community has documented the unlock conditions; the English wiki coverage is less complete.</p>

<h2>The full true ending transcript</h2>
<div>
<p>[Standard ending scene plays: Grace exits the ARK facility. Dawn. Distant sirens.]</p>
<p>[Where standard ending cuts to credits, true ending continues.]</p>
<p>[Grace's phone rings. Caller ID: "S. Birkin"]</p>
<p>[Grace looks at the phone. Hesitates. Answers.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Birkin."</p>
<p><strong>SHERRY (phone):</strong> "Grace. I know what you found."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Then you know what I'm going to do about it."</p>
<p><strong>SHERRY:</strong> "I do. That's why I'm calling."</p>
<p>[Beat.]</p>
<p><strong>SHERRY:</strong> "There are people inside BSAA who would prefer the cover-up holds. People who were there twenty-three years ago. They will not let you publish."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I'm publishing."</p>
<p><strong>SHERRY:</strong> "I know. I'm calling to offer you what protection I can. And to ask one thing."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "What?"</p>
<p><strong>SHERRY:</strong> "When you publish, include my involvement in the reconstruction. Don't protect me from what I helped enable."</p>
<p>[Pause. Grace processes this.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "You knew what BSAA did to my brother's case."</p>
<p><strong>SHERRY:</strong> "I learned in 2010, during the reconstruction. By then it was too late to undo. I should have come to you. I didn't."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Why now?"</p>
<p><strong>SHERRY:</strong> "Because someone has to start telling the truth. And you started."</p>
<p>[Beat. Grace looks toward the distant horizon.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I'll include your name. All of it."</p>
<p><strong>SHERRY:</strong> "Thank you, Grace."</p>
<p>[Call ends. Grace stands alone. Cut to interior of a BSAA conference room. Multiple unfamiliar officials sit at a table. The ranking officer speaks.]</p>
<p><strong>BSAA OFFICIAL (unknown):</strong> "Ashcroft is going to publish."</p>
<p><strong>SECOND OFFICIAL:</strong> "Birkin?"</p>
<p><strong>BSAA OFFICIAL:</strong> "Birkin is with her now."</p>
<p>[Beat.]</p>
<p><strong>SECOND OFFICIAL:</strong> "Activate the contingency."</p>
<p>[Cut to black. Title card: <strong>RESIDENT EVIL — TO BE CONTINUED</strong>]</p>
</div>

<h2>What the true ending establishes</h2>
<p>Sherry Birkin is now an ally character for Grace (and presumably for RE10). The BSAA contains a faction that's hostile to Grace's investigation. The "contingency" mentioned by the official is unspecified but ominous. The franchise has set up RE10 as a direct continuation of RE9's institutional plot.</p>
<p>The "TO BE CONTINUED" title card is the franchise's most explicit RE10 confirmation. Capcom is committing to RE10 happening and being a continuation of RE9's plot threads.</p>

<h2>Why the dialogue choice matters</h2>
<p>The "I don't accept this" option in chapter 8 demonstrates Grace's refusal to let the situation be resolved with Eveline's death. This refusal is what triggers Sherry's confidence — she knows Grace won't drop the investigation. Without this refusal, Grace appears to accept resolution; Sherry's offer of alliance wouldn't make sense.</p>
<p>The Korean speedrun community documented this dependency. Most English-language guides don't explain why the dialogue choice matters; they just list it as the unlock condition.</p>

<h2>Voice work in the true ending</h2>
<p>Sherry Birkin is voiced by Karen Strassman (returning from RE6). The voice work has substantially deepened from her RE6 performance — Sherry is now an adult professional in tone, no longer the young woman of her last appearance.</p>
<p>The two BSAA officials at the table are uncredited. Their performances are deliberately flat-affect — generic institutional voices. The faceless authority framing is part of the threat setup for RE10.</p>

<h2>Korean dub notes</h2>
<p>Sherry's Korean VA, 김지영, recorded the true ending phone scene specifically. The Korean rendering of "I'll include your name. All of it." — "당신 이름까지 다 적을 거예요. 전부" — got specific praise on Naver discussion threads as one of the most affecting line reads in the game.</p>

<h2>Production notes</h2>
<p>The true ending was added late in development (according to a Nakanishi interview at GDC 2026). The original RE9 ending was just the standard version. The decision to add the true ending came from internal narrative team feedback that the chapter 6 Sherry Birkin reference needed payoff in the same game rather than waiting for RE10.</p>
<p>The "TO BE CONTINUED" title card was approved at the highest Capcom level. Including it required formal commitment to RE10 development.</p>

<h2>What this means for RE10</h2>
<p>RE10 will feature Grace Ashcroft as returning character (probably co-protagonist, not lead). Sherry Birkin will be involved as ally. The internal BSAA faction will be the antagonist institution. The "contingency" is the inciting event for RE10's plot.</p>
<p>The Korean fan theory consensus aligns with this read. The true ending essentially outlines RE10's premise.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>The true ending is essential watching. The "TO BE CONTINUED" framing changes how RE9 itself reads — knowing RE10 will continue the institutional thread makes RE9's setup work feel deliberate rather than open-ended. If you missed the true ending, replay specifically to see it. The investment is worth it.</p>
