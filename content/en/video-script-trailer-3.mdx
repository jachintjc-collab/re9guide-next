<h1>RE9 third trailer (December 2025) — extended cut + reading the marketing pivot</h1>
<p>The December 2025 trailer was Capcom's pivot back toward atmosphere after the TGS combat reveal got mixed reception. Four minutes, heavier on cutscene footage, light on combat. The course correction was visible to anyone paying attention.</p>

<blockquote>Capcom corrected the marketing direction in this third trailer. Too late to fully recover the reveal trailer's mystery, but the right move. The dev team won the internal argument that the second trailer lost.</blockquote>

<p>[Cold open: church interior, candles, distant rain. Father Riley in confession booth.]</p>
<p><strong>FATHER RILEY:</strong> "How long have you been carrying this?"</p>

<h2>Why this trailer worked when the second one didn't</h2>
<p>This trailer is 70% cutscene footage, 30% combat. The second trailer was the inverse. The shift back toward narrative content matched what the released game would actually deliver. Players who liked this trailer were the audience the game was made for.</p>
<p>The Father Riley confessional opener was the trailer's strongest choice. Lance Henriksen's voice carries the scene without visual support. Two minutes of his audio could have sold the game on their own.</p>

<h2>Voice cast in this trailer</h2>
<p>Lance Henriksen (Father Riley) gets the trailer's strongest material. Henriksen's resume — Aliens, Millennium, Pumpkinhead — gives the character horror genre credibility. The casting was perfect.</p>
<p>Cissy Jones (Grace) reads from a journal in the trailer's longest single take. The reading is staged for emotional buildup; the journal contents become significant later in the game.</p>
<p>Laila Berzins (Vivien) speaks without facing camera again. The director's choice to never show Vivien's face in marketing material until the launch trailer was correct.</p>

<h2>Korean dub for the December trailer</h2>
<p>Capcom Korea released the Korean dub one week after the English version. The delay was longer than typical — Korean fan forums (Naver, DCInside) noted the gap and speculated about quality control issues. Final Korean version was well-received; the delay didn't damage reception.</p>
<p>Father Riley's Korean VA, 김기현, brought significant emotional weight to the confessional scene. Korean reception ranked Father Riley above Grace in trailer character impact, which was unusual for an English-language original.</p>

<h2>Production notes</h2>
<p>This trailer was directed by Capcom's narrative team rather than marketing. The shift in tone reflects this. Nakanishi was credited as creative director on the trailer cut. The piano motif (Mitsuhiko Takano) was promoted from background score to centerpiece.</p>
<p>The Vivien lab scene is from chapter 3; the manor library is from chapter 5. The compression of game timeline for trailer purposes was less aggressive than in the second trailer.</p>

<h2>Reception data</h2>
<p>The third trailer hit 18M YouTube views in the first week — fewer than the reveal trailer but with higher comment engagement. Critical reception was strong, particularly for the Father Riley material. Korean reception was particularly positive.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>The third trailer is the marketing piece Capcom should have released second. The sequence reveal-combat-atmosphere should have been reveal-atmosphere-gameplay. The released order made the game look more action-focused than it actually is. Newcomers who only watched the second trailer got a misleading impression.</p>
