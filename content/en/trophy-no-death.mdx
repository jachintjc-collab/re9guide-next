<h1>No Death — complete a full playthrough without dying</h1>
<p>Complete RE9 from chapter 1 to Eveline final boss without dying once. Reloads from death break the trophy attempt. Trophy fires at credits if death count is zero.</p>

<blockquote>No Death is the discipline trophy. Requires extensive save scumming or extreme play conservatism. Achievable but stressful.</blockquote>

<h2>What counts as death</h2>
<p>Any time the game shows the "Continue/Restart" screen. This includes one-shot deaths, accumulated damage deaths, and environmental hazard deaths.</p>
<p>Save scumming (reloading before death registers) is the standard No Death strategy. Save before any dangerous encounter, reload immediately if HP drops below safe threshold.</p>

<h2>The save scumming framework</h2>
<p>Standard save slots: use all 6 for rotating saves through chapters. Cloud backup: enable for redundancy. Manual save points: any time HP is at 95%+ and current chapter section is stable.</p>

<h2>Most dangerous chapters for No Death</h2>
<p>Chapter 3 (Vivien Plant 43 boss). Chapter 6 (Mr. X pursuit). Chapter 7 (Commander X1 phase 3). Chapter 8 (Eveline phase 2 tendril sweep).</p>
<p>Each of these has one-shot potential on Insanity. Save before, reload on any HP drop.</p>

<h2>Difficulty considerations</h2>
<p>Casual: easiest No Death. Reduced one-shot scenarios. Recommended for first attempt.</p>
<p>Standard: moderate difficulty. Save scumming works reliably.</p>
<p>Hardcore: harder. More frequent one-shot scenarios.</p>
<p>Insanity: hardest. Combined with True Survivor pursuit if pursuing both simultaneously, manageable; pursuing No Death alone on Insanity is grueling.</p>

<h2>Korean No Death speedrun</h2>
<p>Korean RE9 No Death community has documented chapter-by-chapter No Death routes with save management protocols. Korean Naver coverage includes specific HP threshold rules for when to reload.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>No Death on Casual is the lowest-stress completion path. Insanity No Death is for completionists pursuing multiple high-tier trophies together. Don't underestimate the cumulative stress over 25+ hours of careful play.</p>
