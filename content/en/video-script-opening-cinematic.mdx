<h1>RE9 opening cinematic — full transcript + scene breakdown</h1>
<p>The opening cinematic runs three minutes ten seconds and establishes the entire premise of RE9 in compressed form. It's the most-edited piece of footage in the game. Every cut is intentional. Here's the full text and what it's doing.</p>

<blockquote>RE9's opening cinematic is the franchise's best opener since RE4 (2005). Tight, atmospheric, character-establishing. RE7's opener was looser; this is more disciplined.</blockquote>

<h2>The full transcript</h2>
<div>
<p>[Black. Audio only: faint mechanical hum. Distant rain.]</p>
<p><strong>VOICE (Grace, voice-over):</strong> "There's a question I ask myself every morning."</p>
<p>[Fade in: extreme close-up on Grace's eyes. She blinks once.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Do I really believe it's over?"</p>
<p>[Cut: Wrenwood, Massachusetts — early morning. Grace exits a beat-up Subaru. Rain.]</p>
<p><strong>RADIO (in-car):</strong> "...local authorities report no further sightings since November. The Care Center has resumed standard operations..."</p>
<p>[Grace turns off the radio. Walks toward the Wrenwood Hotel entrance.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (voice-over):</strong> "Twenty-three years since Raccoon City. Eight since the Baker farmhouse. Three since Dimitrescu Castle."</p>
<p>[Cut: hotel lobby. A receptionist looks up. Background: a framed photo of Father Riley.]</p>
<p><strong>RECEPTIONIST:</strong> "Checking in?"</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Yes. Ashcroft. Three nights."</p>
<p><strong>RECEPTIONIST:</strong> "Welcome to Wrenwood. Father Riley's mass is at six. Most guests attend."</p>
<p>[Grace pauses. Looks at the photo.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I'll think about it."</p>
<p>[Cut: hotel room. Grace unpacks. Window: rain. She places a S&W M1911 on the nightstand.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (voice-over):</strong> "The official report said the outbreak had been contained. Three weeks ago. Vivien Davies, lead researcher, was praised for her work."</p>
<p>[Cut: distant view of the Care Center. Lights in two windows on the top floor.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (voice-over):</strong> "I came because I'm not sure I believe the official report."</p>
<p>[Black. Title card: <strong>RESIDENT EVIL: REQUIEM</strong>]</p>
</div>

<h2>What the opening establishes</h2>
<p>Three pieces of crucial information are conveyed in three minutes ten seconds: Grace is investigating something the official narrative says is finished, Father Riley is locally significant (foreshadowing his role), and Vivien Davies has been publicly cleared (which we'll see is wrong).</p>
<p>The radio dialog does heavy lifting. Three sentences establish the entire backstory of the Care Center outbreak. Capcom trusts the audience to extrapolate.</p>

<h2>Visual choices</h2>
<p>The opening uses two colors primarily: cold blue-gray (exteriors, rain) and warm yellow (interior lights). The two color palettes never mix in single frames. This visual choice will reappear throughout the game — exterior = cold + dangerous, interior = warm + temporary safety.</p>
<p>The framing on Grace emphasizes isolation. Single-character shots throughout. No other character appears in frame with her until the receptionist scene. This is deliberate establishment of Grace as solitary investigator.</p>

<h2>Voice work</h2>
<p>Cissy Jones (Grace) delivers her voice-over lines with controlled minimalism. The line "I'll think about it" is the cinematic's emotional anchor — three words that establish Grace's wariness of religious institutional authority.</p>
<p>The receptionist (uncredited Capcom internal cast) speaks the line about Father Riley with rehearsed neutrality. The character knows what Grace is here for; the rehearsed line is part of community-level information control.</p>

<h2>Korean dub notes</h2>
<p>Capcom Korea voice direction for the opening: Grace's voice-over lines were re-recorded multiple times for tonal precision. The Korean version's "정말 끝났다고 믿는지" (the rendering of "Do I really believe it's over") got specific praise in Korean reviews for matching Cissy Jones's restrained delivery.</p>

<h2>Music</h2>
<p>The opening's score (Mitsuhiko Takano) is built on a single piano motif that returns throughout the game. The motif uses only five notes; the recurrences vary instrumentation and tempo. Hearing the motif return in chapter 8's final boss is one of the game's most affecting musical moments.</p>

<h2>Production notes</h2>
<p>The cinematic was Nakanishi's first directed shot in the game. He spent four months on the three minutes ten seconds. The reception team's pre-launch screening reportedly produced strong reaction; the cinematic was confirmed as the right opening with no notes.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>If you're skipping cutscenes in RE9, don't skip the opening. The three minutes ten seconds tell you what the game is about and how it wants to be watched. Players who skipped the opening reported missing key context for chapter 3 events. The opening is doing essential work.</p>
