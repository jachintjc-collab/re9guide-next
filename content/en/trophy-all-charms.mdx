<h1>All Charms collected — 18 of them, where they actually live</h1>
<p>Eighteen Charms across the four areas. Missing one locks you out of the trophy permanently on that save. Here's where each one actually sits, not the wiki-summary version.</p>

<blockquote>Six of the 18 Charms are needed for Insanity NG+. The other 12 are flavor. Collect them all once for the trophy, then ignore most for replays.</blockquote>

<h2>Where the trophy hides</h2>
<p>The All Charms trophy unlocks the moment you pick up the eighteenth Charm. There's no submit step, no menu confirm. Pick up #18, hear the chime, done.</p>
<p>The 18 are not equally distributed. Wrenwood has 3, Rhodes Hill has 5, Raccoon City has 4, ARK has 6. The Rhodes Hill density catches first-time players off guard.</p>

<h2>The two Charms most players miss</h2>
<p>Hotel storage room ceiling vent (Wrenwood chapter 1). Requires you to shoot the vent grate from below, then climb the bedside table to reach. Skipped on most speedrun routes because the input window is small. The Charm: Cold Hands (mild damage resistance — useless).</p>
<p>Rhodes Hill conservatory north wall (chapter 5). Hidden behind a removable wood panel. The panel requires you to push it from a specific 30-degree angle. Most players push from front, get nothing, leave. Charm: Marksman's Eye (essential).</p>

<h2>Insanity difference</h2>
<p>None. Charm locations don't shift across difficulties. The only Insanity wrinkle: some Charm-adjacent rooms have respawning enemies on Insanity that don't appear on Standard. The conservatory in Rhodes Hill specifically has two Care Center Hybrids on Insanity that aren't there otherwise. Bring shotgun ammo.</p>

<h2>Common mistake — selling a Charm</h2>
<p>The merchant in chapter 4 will offer to "appraise" your Charms. The appraisal action removes them from inventory. Don't do this. The trophy checks current possession at the moment of pickup, but later trophy versions (the 100% trophy specifically) check total inventory at game end. Selling locks you out of completion.</p>

<h2>Korean speedrun community notes</h2>
<p>Korean RE9 speedrun community on Naver has documented an optimal Charm collection route for the Charm-required category. The route saves roughly 90 seconds compared to wiki-standard ordering. The route is published in the Naver Café charm-collection guide thread.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>The trophy is a backlog problem more than a difficulty problem. Twelve of the eighteen Charms are easy if you read room descriptions. Six require either specific puzzle attention or specific routing. Collect them in chapter order; don't backtrack. The trophy will unlock organically.</p>
