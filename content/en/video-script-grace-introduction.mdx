<h1>Grace Ashcroft's introduction scene — chapter 1 hotel sequence</h1>
<p>Grace's character introduction continues the opening cinematic. The hotel room sequence (about 4 minutes) establishes her professional approach, her trauma, and the investigative method that drives the rest of the game.</p>

<blockquote>Grace's introduction is RE's best protagonist introduction since Leon Kennedy in RE2. Tight character work in short screen time. The hotel sequence is required watching even on speedrun routes.</blockquote>

<h2>The full transcript</h2>
<div>
<p>[Grace enters hotel room 207. Closes door. Locks deadbolt + chain.]</p>
<p>[She unpacks: laptop, notebook, S&W M1911 sidearm, two extra magazines, a folded photo.]</p>
<p>[Camera lingers on the photo. It's of two people — Grace and a man (her brother, unconfirmed in this scene). They're standing in front of the original Raccoon City PD building, pre-1998.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (alone, to herself):</strong> "Okay. Day one."</p>
<p>[She sets up the laptop. Opens a case file with photos: Vivien Davies's official portrait, Care Center floor plans, a Wrenwood public records map.]</p>
<p>[Phone rings. Caller ID: Leon Kennedy.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "Leon."</p>
<p><strong>LEON (phone, audio only):</strong> "You're there."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I am."</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "You're not supposed to be."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "You knew I'd come anyway."</p>
<p>[Beat.]</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "I'm going to be honest with you. The intelligence we have on Wrenwood says you're walking into something we don't understand."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I'm aware."</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "If you find anything that looks like Veltro, BSAA, or — anything that looks like 1998 — you call me before you do anything."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I always do."</p>
<p><strong>LEON:</strong> "Grace."</p>
<p><strong>GRACE:</strong> "I always do, Leon."</p>
<p>[Hangs up. Looks at the photo on the desk. Picks it up.]</p>
<p><strong>GRACE (to the photo, almost inaudible):</strong> "I'll find out what happened."</p>
<p>[She places the photo in the desk drawer. Closes it.]</p>
<p>[Cut: Grace at the window. The Care Center is visible in the distance. Lights still on in two windows.]</p>
</div>

<h2>What the scene establishes about Grace</h2>
<p>Five things in four minutes: she's professional (the deadbolt+chain, the unpacking ritual), she's been doing this work for a long time (Leon's familiarity, the case file preparation), she has personal investment (the photo, her brother's presumed connection), she has institutional support (BSAA via Leon) but operates independently, and she has agency BSAA didn't approve.</p>
<p>The photo of the original Raccoon City PD building is the scene's most loaded prop. Players who recognize it understand Grace's history. Players who don't will piece it together by chapter 6 (the RPD chapter explicitly references this).</p>

<h2>Voice work in the scene</h2>
<p>Cissy Jones (Grace) and Nick Apostolides (Leon) have natural conversational rhythm. The two characters were recorded together for this scene — uncommon in modern AAA games where voice work is typically isolated. The recording method shows in the timing.</p>
<p>Jones's delivery of "I always do, Leon" is the scene's strongest line. The slight emphasis on "do" carries Grace's whole history of acting before consulting.</p>

<h2>Korean dub notes</h2>
<p>The Korean dub of this scene was praised on Naver RE forums for matching the conversational naturalness. Grace's Korean VA, 박지윤, and Leon's Korean VA, 정승욱, recorded together for the same reasons as the English version. The Korean rendering of "I always do" — "항상 그래왔잖아" — landed well with Korean audiences.</p>

<h2>Production notes</h2>
<p>This scene took the longest motion capture sessions of any RE9 cutscene. Cissy Jones did 14 takes of the hotel room unpacking sequence. Nakanishi specifically wanted the physical movements to read as ritual (something Grace had done many times before), not as nervous activity (first-time investigator).</p>
<p>The photo prop was specifically designed by the art team — three weeks of work on a prop that appears in two scenes total. The detail level (visible weathering, correct period dress for the brother) rewards close inspection.</p>

<h2>What the scene tells us about Grace's brother</h2>
<p>Nothing explicit. The photo doesn't get named context until chapter 6. But the scene tells you the brother is dead (Grace handles the photo as memorial, not as missing person), connected to Raccoon City somehow, and important enough that Grace organized this whole investigation around his unfinished story.</p>
<p>The chapter 6 reveal pays off this scene's restraint. The opening establishes "there's a personal story" without explaining it. The patience pays off.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Grace's introduction scene is the strongest piece of RE protagonist establishment since RE2's police station opening with Leon. The economy of information density, the patience of revelation, the conversational naturalness — all signs of careful writing. Don't skip this cutscene. The chapter 6 callbacks won't land as hard if you do.</p>
