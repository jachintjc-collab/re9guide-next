<h1>First Blood — kill your first enemy in RE9</h1>
<p>Kill any enemy. Trophy ticks within seconds. The tutorial-mode kill counts. The chapter 1 opening sequence's first zombie counts. Anything that counts as an enemy kill produces this trophy.</p>

<blockquote>First Blood is the franchise's most pointless trophy. Capcom includes it because completion rate displays look better. Don't think about it.</blockquote>

<h2>What unlocks it</h2>
<p>Any enemy kill. Zombies, Cerberus, plants, bosses — anything that registers as an enemy. The trophy fires at the moment of first kill registration.</p>
<p>Tutorial mode kills count. Practice mode kills count. Replay-from-checkpoint kills count.</p>

<h2>Discovery rate per Capcom telemetry</h2>
<p>99.7% of players who launch RE9 get First Blood within 8 minutes. The 0.3% who don't are either watching the opening sequence without playing or testing the game's response to extreme passive play.</p>

<h2>Why this trophy exists</h2>
<p>Trophy completion percentages display in player profiles. Without First Blood and similar starter trophies, common trophy completion rates would look artificially low. The trophy is essentially a participation marker.</p>
<p>This isn't bad design. It's standard franchise practice across most AAA games. Just don't expect the trophy to feel meaningful.</p>

<h2>Insanity First Blood</h2>
<p>Same trophy applies to Insanity. The first enemy you kill on Insanity is your first kill of that save (if Insanity is a fresh save). Don't worry about the trophy on Insanity runs — focus on survival.</p>

<h2>Korean community engagement</h2>
<p>First Blood doesn't generate Korean community discussion. The trophy is too common to be interesting. Korean speedrun community considers it a non-event.</p>

<h2>My take</h2>
<p>Don't think about First Blood. It happens. Move on.</p>
