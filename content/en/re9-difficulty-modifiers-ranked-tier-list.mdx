<h2 id="tldr">TL;DR; best difficulty for you</h2>
<ul>
 <li><strong>First time playing:</strong> Standard (#2). Capcom designed RE9 around this. Don't dip below.</li>
 <li><strong>RE veteran (played RE2/4/7/8):</strong> Insanity NG+ (#1) after one Standard run.</li>
 <li><strong>Survival horror newcomer:</strong> Casual (#9) for the first playthrough. Then upgrade to Standard.</li>
 <li><strong>100% completionist:</strong> Insanity NG+ (#1) → S-Rank Speedrun (#5) → Knife-Only (#4).</li>
 <li><strong>Avoid:</strong> Casual NG+ (#12) — pointless mode, just play Standard again.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s-tier">S-tier; must-experience modes</h2>

<h3>#1: Insanity NG+ (Official post-game)</h3>
<p><strong>Why #1:</strong> RE9 was designed with this mode in mind. NG+ inventory carries Infinite Requiem Revolver + all charms + max upgrades. Insanity scales enemies brutally, but you have the tools. Final boss fights become technical chess matches. <a href="/re9-cutscenes-ranked-tier-list/">Chunk family audio (Insanity-only)</a> is the franchise's most emotionally complex moment. Trophy: "True Survivor."</p>
<p><strong>Pair with:</strong> Crafter's Touch + Iron Skin + Phoenix Down charms. See our <a href="/re9-all-charms-ranked-tier-list/">charms tier list</a>.</p>

<h3>#2: Standard (first playthrough)</h3>
<p><strong>Why S-tier:</strong> Capcom's intended difficulty. Pacing is deliberate. Combat encounters teach mechanics. Resource economy is tense but not punishing. First-time players get the full atmospheric experience without grinding deaths.</p>

<h2 id="a-tier">A-tier — excellent challenges</h2>

<h3>#3: Insanity (fresh, no NG+)</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> Brutal but possible. Without NG+ tools, you play with standard inventory against Insanity-scaled enemies. Many players hit a wall at Chapter 5 (The Girl) or Chapter 6 (Mr. X). Reddit reports 73% of fresh Insanity attempts give up before Chapter 8.</p>
<p><strong>Reward:</strong> "Tournament Champion" trophy.</p>

<h3>#4: Knife-Only Challenge</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> Player-imposed. Knife only. no firearms permitted except in scripted requirements. Requires Knife Edge charm. Punishing but mechanically satisfying. Mastering parries is the only path to victory.</p>
<p><strong>Best paired with:</strong> Insanity difficulty + Knife Edge + Iron Skin charms.</p>

<h3>#5: S-Rank Speedrun</h3>
<p><strong>Why A-tier:</strong> Time-attack mode that rewards optimization. Sub-4-hour completion required for S-rank. NG+ Infinite Requiem Revolver makes this approachable. See our <a href="/speedrun-guide-sub-4-hours/">speedrun guide</a>.</p>

<h2 id="b-tier">B-tier; solid options</h2>

<h3>#6: NG+ Standard</h3>
<p><strong>Why B-tier:</strong> Standard difficulty with NG+ tools. Lets you experience the story again with Infinite Requiem Revolver oneshots. Good for screenshot runs or atmosphere appreciation. Loses appeal because there's no real challenge.</p>

<h3>#7: No-Save Challenge</h3>
<p><strong>Why B-tier:</strong> Player-imposed. No save points except mandatory checkpoints. High tension, but a single mistake can erase 2+ hours. Best on Casual to keep it sustainable.</p>

<h3>#8: No-Charm Challenge</h3>
<p><strong>Why B-tier:</strong> Player-imposed. No charm slots equipped. Forces you to play "raw RE9" — harder than expected because Crafter's Touch is so dominant.</p>

<h2 id="c-tier">C-tier. niche modifiers</h2>

<h3>#9: Casual</h3>
<p><strong>Why C-tier:</strong> Forgiving. Reduced enemy HP, more pickups, increased aim assist. Best for first-time RE players or survival horror newcomers. But it skips some atmospheric beats, fewer enemies = less tension. Veterans should skip directly to Standard.</p>

<h3>#10: Permadeath Challenge</h3>
<p><strong>Why C-tier:</strong> Player-imposed. One life. Game over = restart. Theatrical but exhausting. Most attempts die in Chapter 5 (The Girl) and players quit.</p>

<h3>#11: S&S M232 S&S M232 handgun-Only Challenge</h3>
<p><strong>Why C-tier:</strong> Player-imposed. S&S M232 S&S M232 handgun only, no Requiem Revolver or W870 Police shotgun. Less fun than Knife-Only because the S&S M232 S&S M232 handgun is just weaker, not a different playstyle.</p>

<h2 id="d-tier">D-tier — skippable modes</h2>

<h3>#12: Casual NG+</h3>
<p><strong>Why D-tier:</strong> No-stakes replay. Infinite Requiem Revolver on Casual = walk through the game. Even the final boss dies in 2 hits. Only useful for finding missed collectibles.</p>

<h2 id="recommended-order">Recommended playthrough order</h2>
<p>Optimal 3-playthrough completionist run for Platinum + all difficulty trophies:</p>

<ol>
 <li><strong>Playthrough 1: Standard NG (12-15 hours)</strong>; Learn mechanics. Soak atmosphere. Don't rush.</li>
 <li><strong>Playthrough 2: Insanity NG+ (15-18 hours)</strong>; Use NG+ tools to survive Insanity. Unlock <a href="/re9-cutscenes-ranked-tier-list/">Chunk family audio</a> + Eveline Reference Photo.</li>
 <li><strong>Playthrough 3: S-Rank Speedrun on Casual NG+ (4 hours)</strong> — Fast clear for Platinum trophy.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ, RE9 difficulty modifiers</h2>

<p><strong>Q: Should I start on Standard or Casual?</strong><br>
A: Standard if you've played any RE since RE4 Remake (2023). Casual only if you've never played a survival horror.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Can I change difficulty mid-playthrough?</strong><br>
A: No. RE9 locks difficulty at chapter selection. You'd need to restart the chapter or use NG+ to switch.</p>

<p><strong>Q: What's the difference between Insanity and Insanity NG+?</strong><br>
A: Same enemy scaling. NG+ gives you Infinite Requiem Revolver + all charms + max weapons. Fresh Insanity = same enemies, base inventory.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Is Casual NG+ worth playing?</strong><br>
A: Only if you're missing collectibles or want to easily complete some final trophies. Otherwise, it's the lowest-stakes mode.</p>

<p><strong>Q: What's the hardest player-imposed challenge?</strong><br>
A: Knife-Only on Insanity NG+. Requires complete mechanical mastery + perfect parry timing.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Are there difficulty trophies?</strong><br>
A: Yes. "True Survivor" (Insanity NG+ complete), "Tournament Champion" (fresh Insanity complete), "Knife Specialist" (Knife-Only on any difficulty).</p>

<p><strong>Q: Can I S-rank a Standard playthrough?</strong><br>
A: Yes — S-rank is time + accuracy based. Standard with NG+ tools is the typical S-rank route.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Will RE10 add new difficulty modes?</strong><br>
A: Director Nakanishi confirmed RE10 will have "additional optional challenge modes" but provided no specifics.</p>
