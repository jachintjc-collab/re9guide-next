<h1>Treasure Hunter — find all 18 treasures</h1>
<p>Find all 18 hidden treasures across the four areas. Treasures are distinct from collectibles — they're separately tracked and contribute to a different category. Each is a unique item with lore significance.</p>

<blockquote>Treasure Hunter is the lore-engagement trophy that doesn't get enough credit. Each treasure has a story. Worth doing for the documentation, not just the trophy.</blockquote>

<h2>The 18 treasures</h2>
<p>Each treasure is a unique item with a specific in-game description that connects to RE series lore. Examples: Spencer's signet ring (Wrenwood church, chapter 1), Marvin Branagh's badge (RPD, chapter 6), Vivien's wedding photograph (Rhodes Hill manor, chapter 5).</p>
<p>The full list spans all four areas. Each treasure has a hidden location requiring specific exploration.</p>

<h2>Tracking mechanism</h2>
<p>Treasures appear in the pause menu under a dedicated "Treasures" section. Found treasures show their image and text description. Missing slots show silhouettes with location hints.</p>
<p>The location hints in the menu are the trophy's main UI assist. Most are specific enough that you can find the missing treasures on first reread.</p>

<h2>The four chapter-locked treasures</h2>
<p>Same as Mr. Raccoon plushy chapter locks. Missing a chapter-locked treasure requires NG+ to recover.</p>

<h2>Lore reveals through treasures</h2>
<p>Reading all 18 treasure descriptions provides a parallel lore narrative about the series's institutional history. Spencer's signet ring connects to Project W. Marvin's badge connects to the 1998 outbreak. Vivien's wedding photo connects to her research motivation.</p>
<p>The treasures are essentially a hidden lore campaign that runs parallel to the main story.</p>

<h2>Insanity treasures</h2>
<p>Same locations across difficulties. No Insanity-specific treasures.</p>
